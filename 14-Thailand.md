# Thailand — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Country 14 (Batch 3). ASEAN bloc.*

Thailand's tech policy is nested within two meta-frameworks: **Thailand 4.0** (10 "S-Curve" target industries to escape the middle-income trap) and the **Bio-Circular-Green (BCG) Economy Model**. Implementation runs through MHESI and NSTDA's centers (NECTEC, MTEC, BIOTEC), with FDI incentivized via the Board of Investment (BOI). A recurring constraint: human-capital bottlenecks (e.g. 230,000 semiconductor engineers, 30,000 AI specialists targeted).

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** Draft Thailand Power Development Plan 2024–2037 (PDP 2024)
- **Body:** Ministry of Energy, Energy Policy and Planning Office (EPPO), EGAT
- **Year:** 2024 (late draft / public-hearing stage; implementation early 2025)
- **URL:** EPPO/Ministry of Energy public-hearing disclosures; EGAT announcements
- **Summary:** Thailand's third attempt to integrate nuclear, targeting 600 MW via two 300 MW SMRs by 2037 (northeastern and southern regions, operated by EGAT). Supports carbon neutrality by 2050 / net-zero by 2065, cutting gas from 57% to 41% and raising renewables to 51%. EGAT is studying China's Linglong One (ACP100); a US–Thailand "123 Agreement" (early 2025) enables US reactor tech transfer. Analysts call the 2037 target uncertain (regulatory ecosystem, LCOE vs. renewables+storage, public opposition post-Fukushima).

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** Thailand National AI Strategy and Action Plan (2022–2027) + draft Thailand AI Act ("Draft Principles of the Law on Artificial Intelligence")
- **Body:** National AI Committee (PM-chaired), MHESI, Ministry of Digital Economy and Society (MDES), NECTEC/NSTDA; Electronic Transactions Development Agency (ETDA, under MDES) for the AI law
- **Year:** 2022 (strategy, Cabinet-approved July 2022); 2025–2026 (draft AI Act in drafting/refinement)
- **URL:** https://www.ai.in.th · https://www.etda.or.th
- **Summary:** The 2022–2027 strategy aims to build a full AI ecosystem generating ~48 billion THB (~$1.3B) of accumulated impact by 2027, with 100+ R&D prototypes, AI in 600+ agencies, and 30,000+ AI professionals. Five pillars: ethics/law, infrastructure (HPC), talent (the "Super AI Engineer" program, 33,000+ trained), R&D (incl. a sovereign Thai LLM), and adoption. As of 2025–2026 Thailand is moving from voluntary 2022 AI ethics guidelines toward binding rules: ETDA ran public hearings (from ~May 2025) on its "Draft Principles of the Law on AI," now being consolidated into a comprehensive, EU-AI-Act-style risk-based AI Act (prohibited-risk and high-risk tiers, duty-of-care for high-risk providers, sector regulators, mandatory local representatives for foreign providers, plus TDM copyright carve-outs and regulatory sandboxes). Pending enactment (still ~1–2 years out), ETDA manages compliance via an AI Governance Clinic (AIGC) and an AI Sandbox. Thailand hosted UNESCO's 3rd Global Forum on AI Ethics (2025).

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** Quantum Technology Roadmap 2020–2029 (+ Siam Quantum Square); "Quantum-Ready 2030" national post-quantum-cryptography roadmap
- **Body:** MHESI / Office of National Higher Education Science Research and Innovation Policy Council (NXPO); National Cyber Security Agency (NCSA) for "Quantum-Ready 2030"
- **Year:** 2020 (research roadmap, 2020–2029); 2026 (Quantum-Ready 2030, unveiled June 2026)
- **URL:** https://www.nxpo.or.th · https://www.ncsa.or.th
- **Summary:** The 2020–2029 roadmap aims to put Thailand among Asia's top 5 in quantum tech across three pillars — computing/simulation, communication, and metrology/sensing — seeded with 200M THB (~$6M) for a distributed national lab network (Chulalongkorn — computing/Siam Quantum Square; Chiang Mai — simulation; Prince of Songkla — communication; Suranaree/NIMT — sensing, optical-lattice atomic clock). Funding is dwarfed by US/China/EU, so the realistic focus is sovereign quantum communication networks and sensors rather than competing on general-purpose quantum computers. In June 2026 the NCSA added a defensive "Quantum-Ready 2030" roadmap (alongside a national AI-security strategy) to prepare government and enterprises for the post-quantum-cryptography (PQC) era: four pillars cover migrating cryptographic systems to PQC, hardening national digital infrastructure, building specialist personnel, and setting governance/technical standards against "harvest-now-decrypt-later" risks to RSA/ECC.

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** Bio-Circular-Green (BCG) Economy Model — Medical & Wellness Sector (+ 2025 Medical Economy Strategy)
- **Body:** Cabinet Secretariat, Ministry of Public Health, MHESI, BIOTEC/NSTDA
- **Year:** 2021 (BCG 2021–2027); 2025 (Medical Economy Strategy)
- **URL:** https://www.bcg.in.th
- **Summary:** Targets ~690 billion THB (3.4% of GDP) in healthcare value by 2025, with the broader BCG sectors at 4.4 trillion THB (24% of GDP). Funds genomic medicine, biopharma, precision medicine, and high-tech medical devices to correct vulnerabilities (>70% of medicines imported). Flagship: a genomic-medicine center in the Eastern Economic Corridor (Burapha University) plus the Genomics Thailand network across 22 hospitals. Driven by aging demographics (21% aged 65+ by 2030) and a $2.5B+ medical-tourism market.

## 5. Materials Science
- **Plan:** National Materials Technology Development Framework / "Material Solution for Net Zero" (within BCG 2021–2027)
- **Body:** National Metal and Materials Technology Center (MTEC) / NSTDA / NXPO
- **Year:** Rolling roadmaps under BCG 2021–2027
- **URL:** https://www.mtec.or.th
- **Summary:** Materials as the foundational enabler of all 10 S-Curve industries. Prioritizes advanced ceramics, magnetic materials, bio-based composites, and 3D-printing filaments (e.g. commercial-grade 316L stainless steel) with strong circular-economy enforcement for e-waste and EV-battery critical minerals (lithium, cobalt; LCA/CFP under ISO 59004). Funded via NXPO's Program Management Unit for Competitiveness (PMUC). National R&D spend (~1.14% of GDP) lags the 2% target; "valley of death" between prototype and scale is the key challenge.

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** National Semiconductor and Advanced Electronics Strategy (2030/2040/2050 milestones; "Made-in-Thailand" chip ecosystem)
- **Body:** National Semiconductor and Advanced Electronics Policy Committee (National Semiconductor Board) + Board of Investment (BOI)
- **Year:** 2026 (finalized draft approved Jan 7, 2026)
- **URL:** Thailand BOI official press releases
- **Summary:** A multi-decade roadmap targeting 2.5 trillion THB (~$79.5B) investment by 2050, shifting from OSAT (assembly/test) to IC design and upstream wafer fab. Prioritizes niches with existing advantage — power, sensor, photonics, and analog chips for EVs and AI data centers (electronics = ~25% of exports). Targets training 230,000 engineers (86,000 within 5 years), CAPEX subsidies, and power/water upgrades. Critiqued for lacking concrete anchor-investor MOUs vs. Malaysia/Singapore.

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** Thailand Digital Economy and Society Development Plan (2018–2037), plus the Digital Government Development Plan Framework (2025–2027) and the Digital Data Infrastructure Roadmap (2025–2029)
- **Body:** Ministry of Digital Economy and Society (MDES) / Office of National Digital Economy and Society Commission (ONDE); Digital Government Development Agency (DGA)
- **Year:** 2018 (20-year master plan); 2025–2026 (Digital Government Plan 2025–27, public hearing Mar 2026; Digital Data Infrastructure Roadmap 2025–29)
- **URL:** https://onde.go.th · https://www.dga.or.th
- **Summary:** The 20-year, 4-phase master plan aims to make Thailand a global digital leader by 2037, central to Thailand 4.0, via universal high-speed broadband (to every village), e-Government, closing the digital divide, and 5G/IoT/big data (5G alone projected to add ~$9.3B, ~10% of GDP, by 2035 per NBTC). Phase 3 (full transformation, current) targets data-driven "Open Government," though reform lags due to bureaucracy and data silos. New 2025–2026 sub-plans operationalize this: the Digital Government Development Plan Framework (2025–2027, primary public hearing March 2026) mandates a "Single Portal" model built on standard-API interoperability, Open Data, and the national Digital ID (ThaiD); the Digital Data Infrastructure Roadmap (2025–2029, World Bank–endorsed) overhauls data governance and pushes AI-cloud investment to make Thailand a top regional data-center hub (digital economy reached ~5.6 trillion THB by early 2026).

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** 30@30 EV Policy (via EV 3.0 / EV 3.5) + National Robotics Development Plan (2022–2027)
- **Body:** National EV Policy Committee (EV Board), BOI, Ministry of Industry
- **Year:** 2021/22 (30@30); 2024 (EV 3.5); 2022 (Robotics Plan)
- **URL:** https://www.boi.go.th
- **Summary:** "30@30" mandates that by 2030, 30% of vehicles manufactured in Thailand (~725,000 cars/pickups, 675,000 motorcycles/yr) are Zero Emission Vehicles. EV 3.5 offers up to 8-year tax exemptions and consumer subsidies, backed by 137.7B THB (~$4.2B) in approved investment (mid-2025). The parallel National Robotics Development Plan targets domestic production of 20,000 robotic units/yr by 2027 to automate manufacturing amid rising wages and an aging workforce ("Detroit of Asia" defending its auto base).
