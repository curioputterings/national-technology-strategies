# Qatar — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Entity 42 (Batch 9). Gulf.*

Qatar's tech investments are driven by hydrocarbon diversification, anchored in **Qatar National Vision 2030**, the **Third National Development Strategy (NDS3, 2024–2030)**, and the **QRDI 2030** R&D framework (which foregrounds *resilience* after the 2017 blockade). A QR 9 billion (~$2.5B) AI/digital incentive package signals intent to become a regional tech power. Maturity is uneven: digital infrastructure, AI, and biomedical sciences are mature and fully funded; nuclear is confined to peaceful non-power uses; quantum and semiconductors are nascent but accelerating via marquee partnerships (QC2, IMEC). Two distinctive sovereign assets: the **Fanar** Arabic LLM and Qatar's position as ~30% of global **helium** supply — a critical input for chips, MRI, and quantum cooling.

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** Radiation Protection Law No. 31 of 2002; National System for the Account and Control of Nuclear Material Law No. 3 of 2018 *(no nuclear power program)*
- **Body:** Ministry of Environment and Climate Change (MoECC) — Department of Radiation Protection
- **Year:** 2002 / 2018 (IAEA legislative review May 2024)
- **URL:** https://caa.gov.qa / https://mofa.gov.qa
- **Summary:** Qatar has no nuclear power program and explicitly avoids large-scale civilian plants — its vast LNG reserves make the capital cost and geopolitical risk unnecessary. Policy is confined to peaceful, non-power applications: the 2002 law created the Department of Radiation Protection to license/inspect radiation facilities (heavily used in oil/gas industrial radiography, well-logging, gauges, generating NORM waste), and the 2018 law criminalizes breaches of IAEA Safeguards obligations. Qatar has publicly voiced caution about neighbors' (UAE, Saudi) civilian programs on regional-stability and disaster-planning grounds. It cooperates with the IAEA (a May 2024 legislative-assistance mission reviewed draft nuclear legislation) and, aligned with NDS3 energy-resilience goals, is *quietly exploring* Small Modular Reactors (SMRs) for potential future seawater desalination.

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** National Artificial Intelligence Strategy (2019); National AI Policy (draft out for public consultation, closed Jan 2026); proposed National Centre for AI; Fanar LLM; GovAI Program
- **Body:** Ministry of Communications and Information Technology (MCIT); AI Committee; Qatar Computing Research Institute (QCRI/HBKU)
- **Year:** 2019 (strategy); 2025–2026 (policy consultation)
- **URL:** https://mcit.gov.qa
- **Summary:** AI is a cornerstone of NDS3 and the Digital Agenda 2030, backed by a QR 9B (~$2.5B) incentive package (May 2024); the AI market is projected to reach $1.9B by 2030. The 2019 strategy set six pillars (education, data, workforce, business, research, ethics) and led to a Cabinet AI Committee (2021). MCIT released the **National AI Policy** for public consultation via the Sharek platform (consultation closed mid-January 2026) — built on five pillars (digital infrastructure, data, human capabilities, institutional capacity, governance) and structured as a risk-based governance framework that applies existing sector laws to AI rather than a standalone "AI Act"; a National Centre for AI has been proposed to centralize governance. Sector mandates are already live (Qatar Central Bank AI Guidelines Sept 2024; NCSA secure-AI guidelines). The sovereign centerpiece is **Fanar** ("Lighthouse"), QCRI's Arabic-centric LLM — Fanar-1-9B (continually pretrained on Gemma-2-9B, 1T tokens, MSA + Gulf/Levantine/Egyptian dialects, aligned with Islamic values) and **Fanar 2.0** (Dec 2025, multimodal: poetry, translation, Islamic RAG, image/video; its 34B version scored 75.36% atop the Open Arabic LLM Leaderboard). The GovAI Program drives public-sector adoption (5-year Scale AI partnership for 50+ use cases; Microsoft Azure OpenAI).

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** Quantum program (no horizontal national strategy yet) via QC2; national QKD testbed
- **Body:** Qatar Center for Quantum Computing (QC2) at HBKU; Ooredoo; Ministry of Defense
- **Year:** Ongoing; first quantum-safe link mid-2026
- **URL:** https://www.hbku.edu.qa/en/cse/qc2/about
- **Summary:** No published horizontal "National Quantum Strategy," but the domain is funded under QRDI and higher-education mandates — a $10M seed into QC2 at HBKU plus Ooredoo investment. QC2 (co-located with QCRI) spans quantum communication, computing, and sensing, and runs executive programs ("Quantum Innovations for Strategic Leaders") to build workforce and assess quantum cyber threats. A major achievement: in mid-2026 a national alliance (QC2, Ooredoo, Ministry of Defense) established Qatar's **first quantum-safe communications link** using QKD (with ID Quantique) on Ooredoo's live commercial dark-fiber network — countering "harvest-now, decrypt-later" threats. QC2 also partners with Quantinuum for cloud access to trapped-ion quantum computers (quantum chemistry, ML, cybersecurity).

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** Qatar Genome Programme (QGP) + Qatar Precision Health Institute mandate
- **Body:** Qatar Foundation; Qatar Precision Health Institute (QPHI); Qatar Biobank (QBB)
- **Year:** 2015 (launch); Phase 3 (2022–2025)
- **URL:** Qatar Foundation institutional sites
- **Summary:** A mature, heavily funded domain supporting QNV 2030's human-development pillar. The QGP (launched 2015) is a population-genomics program for precision medicine, operating with the Qatar Biobank under the new Qatar Precision Health Institute. Targets: 100,000 whole genomes by 2025 (30,000–35,000 sequenced by 2023/24), scaling toward 350,000 (the entire Qatari population). Science: mapped six Qatari ancestry clusters and 24M+ novel variants, identifying founder mutations (e.g., DCAF17 for Woodhouse-Sakati syndrome) tied to high consanguinity. Clinical translation runs via Hamad Medical Corporation and Sidra Medicine (pharmacogenomic-guided dosing). Technological outputs include the **Q-Chip** (2018, with Weill Cornell-Qatar, for localized variant screening) and a Thermo Fisher Axiom pan-Arab genotyping array drawing on 19 Arab nations — reinforcing Qatar as the regional biomedical hub.

## 5. Materials Science / Critical Raw Materials (Helium)
- **Plan:** NDS3 Priority Economic Clusters (Manufacturing); Helium production at Ras Laffan
- **Body:** National Planning Council; QatarEnergy
- **Year:** 2024 (NDS3)
- **URL:** https://www.npc.qa/en/planning/nds3/Documents/QNDS3_EN.pdf
- **Summary:** Materials strategy is tied to maximizing the gas value chain. NDS3's Manufacturing Cluster targets blue gas-based petrochemicals, an integrated plastics sub-cluster, and low-carbon metals/downstream aluminium. But Qatar's most strategic global contribution is **helium**: as a byproduct of LNG processing at Ras Laffan, Qatar is the world's 2nd-largest producer at ~30% of global supply (~63 million cubic feet/yr in 2025). Helium is irreplaceable for semiconductor manufacturing (wafer cooling, vacuum, carrier gas), MRI, aerospace, and quantum-computing cooling. Early-2026 geopolitical disruptions (Strait of Hormuz drone strikes/blockades) cut Ras Laffan output, removing ~1/3 of global helium and spiking spot prices 40–100% — exposing Qatar's strategic leverage. Under QRDI 2030's resilience mandate, Qatar focuses on securing logistics/export infrastructure as an indispensable high-tech supply-chain node.

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** QRDI 2030 + IMEC Qatar R&D Hub agreement
- **Body:** QRDI Council; Invest Qatar
- **Year:** 2025/2026 (IMEC agreement signed Nov 25, 2025)
- **URL:** https://www.imec-int.com/en/press/imec-strengthens-its-global-presence-new-regional-research-development-hub-qatar
- **Summary:** Historically absent from fabrication, Qatar is building a sovereign microelectronics ecosystem focused on *research, design, and innovation* rather than capital-intensive mass fabrication. The landmark move: Invest Qatar and the QRDI Council signed an agreement with **IMEC** (the Belgian nanoelectronics leader) at Mobile World Congress Doha on Nov 25, 2025 to launch a regional R&D hub in Qatar Science and Technology Park by early 2026 — IMEC's first permanent Middle East presence. Targets: hire 100 specialists by 2030 (with PhD/internship pipelines); transfer IMEC's IC-link chip-design tech; develop Process Design Kits for silicon photonics, 3D ICs, advanced interposers, and advanced packaging; and pioneer generative/agent-based AI for ASIC design. This complements QIA sovereign-wealth stakes in global chip firms (e.g., Kokusai Electric).

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** Digital Agenda 2030 (DA2030); TASMU Smart Qatar Program
- **Body:** Ministry of Communications and Information Technology (MCIT)
- **Year:** 2024 (DA2030)
- **URL:** https://mcit.gov.qa
- **Summary:** DA2030 (early 2024) is the comprehensive digital-transformation roadmap on six pillars (infrastructure, digital economy, innovation, government, technologies, society). Targets: ~QR 40B (~$11B) cumulative digital economic impact by 2030, 26,000 new ICT jobs, 7.8% GDP CAGR, and a National Skilling Program (with GSMA/Microsoft) training 50,000 people by 2025. A core goal is digitizing 90% of government services (~1,500) by 2030. Qatar localized hyperscale cloud for data sovereignty — Microsoft Azure (2022, expanded via a Dec 2025 MCIT–MEEZA capacity agreement) and Google Cloud Doha (2023, projected $18.9B economic impact by 2030); in Feb 2026 MCIT and Oracle agreed to stand up an additional dedicated government cloud region (2025–2028) as a redundant backup for public-sector services. **TASMU Smart Qatar** integrates IoT/AI across five sectors (transport, healthcare, environment, sports, logistics); its accelerator startups report a combined QR 2.92B valuation and QR 228M raised.

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** Autonomous Vehicles Strategy (2023); draft UAV Law (2026); nationwide drone ban (Mar 2026); QCAA Unmanned Aircraft Regulations
- **Body:** Ministry of Transport (MoT); Ministry of Interior (MoI); Qatar Civil Aviation Authority (QCAA)
- **Year:** 2023 (AV Strategy); 2026 (UAV Law / drone ban); ongoing (QCAA)
- **URL:** https://mot.gov.qa / https://www.caa.gov.qa
- **Summary:** The MoT's September 2023 Autonomous Vehicles Strategy is a five-year plan to integrate zero-emission autonomous transit, supporting QNV 2030 and the Transportation Master Plan for Qatar 2050. Qatar runs operational trials of **Level 4 self-driving electric robotaxis** via Mowasalat (Karwa) — LiDAR/radar-equipped Lexus RX 450s in tourist/service zones, with manned/unmanned phases through Q1 2026 — plus Level 3 infrastructure and a C-V2X national pilot. Airspace automation has tightened sharply: in **March 2026 the Ministry of Interior imposed a nationwide ban** on operating or launching any drone (by citizens, residents, or companies) "until further notice," with arrests for violations, hardening an already restrictive regime in which historically only Qatari citizens could fly drones under permit. Looking past the ban, in **May 2026 the Cabinet — following Shura Council review — approved a draft UAV Law** establishing a formal legal framework to balance air-navigation security with drone innovation and investment, and in early 2026 the QCAA tendered a national UAS Traffic Management (UTM) system to safely integrate drone traffic with conventional airspace. The QCAA also participates in ICAO Advanced Air Mobility and UTM efforts to enable future air-taxi operations.
