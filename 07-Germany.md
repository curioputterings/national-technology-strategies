# Germany — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Country 7 (Batch 2). OECD bloc.*

Germany frames its tech policy around "technological sovereignty," reducing non-European supply-chain dependencies, with large federal investments (€3B quantum, €1.6B AI) and tight interlocking with EU frameworks (Chips Act, AI Act, Quantum Flagship). The CDU/SPD Merz government (2025) restructured the machinery: the former BMBF became the Federal Ministry of Research, Technology and Space (BMFTR, under Minister Dorothee Bär), a new Federal Ministry for Digital and State Modernisation (BMDS) consolidated digital competencies previously split across six ministries, and the €18B **Hightech Agenda Deutschland** (cabinet-adopted July 2025) became the overarching innovation frame, concentrating funding on six future technologies (AI, quantum, microelectronics, biotech, fusion/climate-neutral energy, batteries). Notable exception: Germany has fully phased out commercial nuclear power.

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** Atomic Energy Act (Atomgesetz) / official phase-out framework (Atomausstieg)
- **Body:** Federal Government / BMWK / Federal Office for Safety of Nuclear Waste Management (BASE)
- **Year:** 2011 (core phase-out) / 2022 (stretch-out) / 2023 (final shutdown) / 2025–26 reassessments
- **URL:** https://www.base.bund.de/en/nuclear-safety/nuclear-phase-out/nuclear-phase-out_content.html
- **Summary:** Germany completed its nuclear phase-out on April 15, 2023, shutting its last three commercial reactors (Isar 2, Neckarwestheim 2, Emsland). Domestic commercial nuclear fission is prohibited under current law, a stance crystallized after Fukushima (2011). However, the Merz government funds nuclear-fusion research (a dedicated mission of the 2025 Hightech Agenda Deutschland) and has reversed Germany's historical opposition to EU-level nuclear support—signing a joint Franco-German energy agreement (September 2025) and dropping resistance to recognizing nuclear power in EU taxonomies and to EU funding for SMRs. Amid high energy prices, Chancellor Merz called the 2023 shutdown a "serious strategic mistake" (January 15, 2026), but the official government position still treats the domestic shutdown as irreversible and no restart is legislated.

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** Hightech Agenda Deutschland (AI mission) — building on the AI Action Plan (KI-Aktionsplan)
- **Body:** Federal Ministry of Research, Technology and Space (BMFTR, formerly BMBF)
- **Year:** July 2025 (Hightech Agenda) / November 2023 (KI-Aktionsplan)
- **URL:** https://www.hightech-agenda-deutschland.de/ ; https://www.ki-strategie-deutschland.de/
- **Summary:** AI is the lead mission of the €18B Hightech Agenda Deutschland (cabinet-adopted July 2025), which aims to make Germany a premier AI hub by securing sovereign domestic supercomputing power, sovereign AI models, and data access. It builds on the 2023 KI-Aktionsplan's eleven fields of action for "AI Made in Germany/Europe" and the €1.6B+ committed this legislative period for compute infrastructure, an "AI Skills Offensive," and tech transfer (KI4KMU for SMEs). A flagship 2026 measure is the "AI Robotics Booster," integrating industrial AI with next-generation robotics for industry, caretaking, and medicine. The agenda responds to low adoption (~15% of German firms) and aligns with the EU AI Act's risk-based approach and Franco-German research networks to make the EU an independent tech pole.

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** Hightech Agenda Deutschland (quantum mission) + Action Plan Quantum Technologies (Handlungskonzept Quantentechnologien)
- **Body:** Federal Government (coordinated by BMFTR, with BMWK, BMF, BMI, BMVg)
- **Year:** July 2025 (Hightech Agenda) / April 2023 (Handlungskonzept)
- **URL:** https://www.quantentechnologien.de ; https://www.hightech-agenda-deutschland.de/
- **Summary:** The €3B Action Plan Quantum Technologies (€2.8B federally coordinated; ~€1.37B from the research ministry) targets making Germany a global quantum leader by 2026 via three pillars — technology development, commercial utilization, ecosystem creation — with a headline target of a domestically built universal quantum computer of ≥100 qubits by 2026 (scaling to 500), hedged across six hardware platforms. The 2025 Hightech Agenda makes quantum a dedicated mission, sharpening the focus on moving quantum computing, high-precision quantum sensors, and secure communication out of the lab and into the market by connecting universities with industry and building domestic manufacturing capability. It anchors the EU Quantum Flagship; the QUTAC industry consortium guides application research; emphasis on quantum communication and post-quantum security.

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** National Strategy for Gene- and Cell-Based Therapies (Nationale Strategie für Gen- und Zelltherapien, GCT)
- **Body:** BMFTR (formerly BMBF) / Berlin Institute of Health (BIH at Charité)
- **Year:** June 2024 (GCT) / July 2025 (Hightech Agenda biotech mission); broader 2020 National Bioeconomy Strategy also applies
- **URL:** https://www.nationale-strategie-gct.de/en/
- **Summary:** Built by ~150 experts across eight action fields to make Germany a global hub for advanced therapies (gene/cell therapies for cancer and rare diseases). Focuses on defragmenting/standardizing clinical-trial regulation, agile funding formats, a national GCT network (50+ partner institutions), and fostering a bio-entrepreneurial culture. Operates alongside the National Pharma Strategy to reshore pharmaceutical innovation and set the EMA benchmark for ATMPs. The 2025 Hightech Agenda Deutschland adds biotechnology as a dedicated mission, backing AI-supported bioprocess development, sustainable manufacturing, and a bio-based economy for health and food systems.

## 5. Materials Science
- **Plan:** Mat2Twin — Material Innovations for the Transformation of Economy and Society (+ Raw Materials Strategy / EU RESourceEU alignment)
- **Body:** BMFTR (formerly BMBF) / BMWK
- **Year:** January 2025 (Mat2Twin, 2025–2034) / December 2025 (RESourceEU Action Plan)
- **URL:** https://www.werkstofftechnologien.de/programm ; https://www.bmwk.de/Redaktion/DE/Artikel/Industrie/rohstoffstrategie-der-bundesregierung.html
- **Summary:** Decade-long framework (embedded in FITS2030) treating materials as the foundation of green/digital transitions (~70% of German product innovations rely on new materials). Core directives: digitalization of materials engineering (multiscale modeling, AI, "digital twins"), circular-economy/resilient materials to cut critical-raw-material dependence, biologization of technology, and high-risk disruptive innovation. Non-repayable grants (up to 100% for non-economic research). On critical minerals, Germany's Raw Materials Strategy has shifted from framework-setting to operational delivery, aligning with the EU Critical Raw Materials Act and the new RESourceEU Action Plan (adopted December 3, 2025), the European Critical Raw Materials Centre (2026), 12-month processing/recycling permit timelines, joint stockpiling, and "friend-shoring" partnerships.

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** Microelectronics Strategy of the Federal Government (Mikroelektronik-Strategie) + Hightech Agenda Deutschland (microelectronics mission)
- **Body:** BMWK & BMFTR (formerly BMBF)
- **Year:** October 2025 (Mikroelektronik-Strategie, Federal Cabinet) / July 2025 (Hightech Agenda)
- **URL:** https://www.bundeswirtschaftsministerium.de/Redaktion/DE/Publikationen/Industrie/mikroelektronik-strategie-der-bundesregierung.html ; https://www.hightech-agenda-deutschland.de/
- **Summary:** Unified masterplan to reclaim ground in chip manufacturing and design, in alignment with the EU Chips Act (which targets doubling the EU's global share to 20% by 2030). Three pillars — Research, Skilled Workforce, Manufacturing ("lab to fab") — with subsidies for fabs, advanced packaging, and localized equipment/materials. Germany holds ~30% of European wafer capacity (largest EU hub). Funding largely via IPCEI state-aid framework; aims to capture value in chip design/IP. The 2025 Hightech Agenda reinforces this with a dedicated microelectronics mission, including a new Competence Centre for Chip Design and open-source chip-design tooling (2026 milestones) to reduce reliance on foreign supply chains.

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** Föderale Modernisierungsagenda + Deutschland-Stack (building on Gigabit Strategy + Digital Strategy)
- **Body:** Federal Ministry for Digital and State Modernisation (BMDS) — newly created 2025
- **Year:** 2025 (BMDS founded May 2025; Föderale Modernisierungsagenda Oct/Nov 2025) / 2022 (Gigabit & Digital Strategy)
- **URL:** https://bmds.bund.de ; https://bmds.bund.de/themen/digitale-infrastrukturen/glasfaser
- **Summary:** Fulfilling a Merz-government election promise, the new Federal Ministry for Digital and State Modernisation (BMDS, founded May 2025 under independent Minister Karsten Wildberger) consolidated digital competencies previously scattered across six ministries. Its Föderale Modernisierungsagenda (cabinet, autumn 2025) drives a "Deutschland-Stack" unified federal IT infrastructure, an integrated e-ID wallet by 2027, an "EinfachMachen" debureaucratization portal, and AI in public services (e.g. automated visa processing). This sits atop the inherited 2022 Gigabit Strategy (nationwide FTTH fiber and 5G/6G by 2030; interim 50% fiber by 2025, gapless mobile by 2026, unlocking ~€50B private telecom investment) and Digital Strategy; aligns with the EU Digital Decade.

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** Autonomous Driving Act (Gesetz zum autonomen Fahren) + Teleoperated Driving Regulation (Straßenverkehr-Fernlenk-Verordnung, StVFernLV) + Robotics Research Action Plan (Aktionsplan Robotikforschung)
- **Body:** BMV (mobility) & BMFTR (robotics, formerly BMBF)
- **Year:** 2021 (driving act) / 2025 (StVFernLV) / November 2023 (robotics plan)
- **URL:** https://www.bmv.de/SharedDocs/EN/Articles/StV/act-on-autonomous-driving.html ; https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/stvfernlv/ ; https://www.bmftr.bund.de/SharedDocs/Publikationen/DE/5/846858_Aktionsplan_Robotikforschung.pdf
- **Summary:** The 2021 Autonomous Driving Act made Germany the first country to legally permit SAE Level 4 driverless vehicles in regular public traffic within defined operational areas, replacing the safety driver with remote "technical supervision" and centralizing approval under the Federal Motor Transport Authority. The new StVFernLV (adopted July 16, 2025; BGBl. 2025 I Nr. 176; in force December 1, 2025) establishes Germany's first nationwide framework for teleoperated driving, letting an operator in an external control center fully control a vehicle remotely — under an experimentation clause running to end-2030 with continuous monitoring, data logging, and UNECE-aligned safety standards. The 2023 Robotics Research Action Plan commits €40M+/year to create a decentralized "Robotics Institute Germany" (RIG), reinforced by the 2025 Hightech Agenda's "AI Robotics Booster." Frames AI-driven robotics and automated mobility as tools for reshoring production and offsetting labor shortages.
