# Germany — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Country 7 (Batch 2). OECD bloc.*

Germany frames its tech policy around "technological sovereignty," reducing non-European supply-chain dependencies, with large federal investments (€3B quantum, €1.6B AI) and tight interlocking with EU frameworks (Chips Act, AI Act, Quantum Flagship). Notable exception: Germany has fully phased out commercial nuclear power.

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** Atomic Energy Act (Atomgesetz) / official phase-out framework (Atomausstieg)
- **Body:** Federal Government / BMWK / Federal Office for Safety of Nuclear Waste Management (BASE)
- **Year:** 2011 (core phase-out) / 2022 (stretch-out) / 2023 (final shutdown) / 2025–26 reassessments
- **URL:** https://www.base.bund.de/en/nuclear-safety/nuclear-phase-out/nuclear-phase-out_content.html
- **Summary:** Germany completed its nuclear phase-out on April 15, 2023, shutting its last three commercial reactors (Isar 2, Neckarwestheim 2, Emsland). Domestic commercial nuclear fission is prohibited under current law, a stance crystallized after Fukushima (2011). However, the government funds nuclear-fusion research and has dropped resistance to EU-level funding for SMRs in other member states. Amid high energy prices, Chancellor Merz called the 2023 shutdown a "serious strategic mistake" (early 2026), but no domestic restart is legislated.

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** AI Action Plan (KI-Aktionsplan) — update to the National AI Strategy
- **Body:** Federal Ministry of Education and Research (BMBF)
- **Year:** November 2023
- **URL:** https://www.ki-strategie-deutschland.de/
- **Summary:** Eleven fields of action to achieve "AI Made in Germany/Europe" and technological sovereignty. BMBF commits €1.6B+ this legislative period to expand AI compute infrastructure, an "AI Skills Offensive," and tech transfer (funding 50 ongoing + 20 new measures, e.g. KI4KMU for SMEs). Responds to generative-AI proliferation and low adoption (~15% of German firms). Supports the EU AI Act's risk-based approach and a Franco-German research network to make the EU an independent tech pole.

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** Action Plan Quantum Technologies (Handlungskonzept Quantentechnologien)
- **Body:** Federal Government (coordinated by BMBF, with BMWK, BMF, BMI, BMVg)
- **Year:** April 2023
- **URL:** https://www.quantentechnologien.de
- **Summary:** €3B envelope (€2.8B federally coordinated; BMBF ~€1.37B) to make Germany a global quantum leader by 2026. Three pillars: technology development, commercial utilization, ecosystem creation. Headline target: a domestically built universal quantum computer of ≥100 qubits by 2026, scaling to 500 medium-term, hedged across six hardware platforms. Anchors the EU Quantum Flagship; industry consortium QUTAC guides application research; emphasis on quantum communication and post-quantum security.

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** National Strategy for Gene- and Cell-Based Therapies (Nationale Strategie für Gen- und Zelltherapien, GCT)
- **Body:** BMBF / Berlin Institute of Health (BIH at Charité)
- **Year:** June 2024 (broader 2020 National Bioeconomy Strategy also applies)
- **URL:** https://www.nationale-strategie-gct.de/en/
- **Summary:** Built by ~150 experts across eight action fields to make Germany a global hub for advanced therapies (gene/cell therapies for cancer and rare diseases). Focuses on defragmenting/standardizing clinical-trial regulation, agile funding formats, a national GCT network (50+ partner institutions), and fostering a bio-entrepreneurial culture. Operates alongside the National Pharma Strategy to reshore pharmaceutical innovation and set the EMA benchmark for ATMPs.

## 5. Materials Science
- **Plan:** Mat2Twin — Material Innovations for the Transformation of Economy and Society
- **Body:** BMBF
- **Year:** January 2025 (10-year programme, 2025–2034)
- **URL:** https://www.werkstofftechnologien.de/programm
- **Summary:** Decade-long framework (embedded in FITS2030) treating materials as the foundation of green/digital transitions (~70% of German product innovations rely on new materials). Core directives: digitalization of materials engineering (multiscale modeling, AI, "digital twins"), circular-economy/resilient materials to cut critical-raw-material dependence, biologization of technology, and high-risk disruptive innovation. Non-repayable grants (up to 100% for non-economic research); aligns with the EU Critical Raw Materials Act.

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** Microelectronics Strategy of the Federal Government (Mikroelektronik-Strategie)
- **Body:** BMWK & BMBF
- **Year:** October 2025 (Federal Cabinet)
- **URL:** https://www.bundeswirtschaftsministerium.de/Redaktion/DE/Publikationen/Industrie/mikroelektronik-strategie-der-bundesregierung.html
- **Summary:** Unified masterplan to reclaim ground in chip manufacturing and design, in alignment with the EU Chips Act (which targets doubling the EU's global share to 20% by 2030). Three pillars — Research, Skilled Workforce, Manufacturing ("lab to fab") — with subsidies for fabs, advanced packaging, and localized equipment/materials. Germany holds ~30% of European wafer capacity (largest EU hub). Funding largely via IPCEI state-aid framework; aims to capture value in chip design/IP.

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** Gigabit Strategy (Gigabitstrategie) + Digital Strategy (Digitalstrategie)
- **Body:** Federal Ministry for Digital and Transport (BMDV)
- **Year:** July/August 2022
- **URL:** https://bmdv.bund.de/SharedDocs/DE/Anlage/K/gigabitstrategie.pdf
- **Summary:** Mandates nationwide FTTH fiber and 5G/6G to all households/businesses by 2030 (interim: 50% fiber by 2025, gapless mobile by 2026). 100+ measures cut bureaucracy, digitalize permits, promote trenching, and create a "Gigabit Land Register." Relies primarily on regulatory optimization to unlock ~€50B private telecom investment, with targeted subsidies for unprofitable rural areas. The Digital Strategy adds five action fields (skills, infrastructure, innovation, society, state modernization); aligns with the EU Digital Decade.

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** Autonomous Driving Act (Gesetz zum autonomen Fahren) + Robotics Research Action Plan (Aktionsplan Robotikforschung)
- **Body:** BMDV (mobility) & BMBF (robotics)
- **Year:** 2021 (driving act) / November 2023 (robotics plan)
- **URL:** https://www.bmv.de/SharedDocs/EN/Articles/StV/act-on-autonomous-driving.html ; https://www.bmftr.bund.de/SharedDocs/Publikationen/DE/5/846858_Aktionsplan_Robotikforschung.pdf
- **Summary:** The 2021 Autonomous Driving Act made Germany the first country to legally permit SAE Level 4 driverless vehicles in regular public traffic within defined operational areas, replacing the safety driver with remote "technical supervision" and centralizing approval under the Federal Motor Transport Authority. The 2023 Robotics Research Action Plan commits €40M+/year to create a decentralized "Robotics Institute Germany" (RIG) networking elite research clusters. Frames AI-driven robotics as a tool for reshoring production and offsetting labor shortages.
