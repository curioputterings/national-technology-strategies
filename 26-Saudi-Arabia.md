# Saudi Arabia — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Entity 26 (Batch 5).*

Saudi Arabia runs an aggressive, state-led pivot from a hydrocarbon to a knowledge economy under **Vision 2030**, with the Public Investment Fund (PIF) and bespoke vehicles (SDAIA, HUMAIN, Alat, Manara, K.A.CARE) deploying trillions to build full-stack ecosystems (compute, fabs, reactors, mines) plus human capital and regulation. Giga-projects like NEOM act as regulatory sandboxes. Distinctive: nuclear ambitions with "strategic optionality" on enrichment, and a newly revalued $2.5T mineral base.

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** Saudi National Atomic Energy Project (SNAEP)
- **Body:** King Abdullah City for Atomic and Renewable Energy (K.A.CARE), under the Council of Ministers
- **Year:** 2017 (Cabinet approval); 2022 (Nuclear Holding Company)
- **URL:** https://world-nuclear.org/information-library/country-profiles/countries-o-s/saudi-arabia
- **Summary:** Three components: large reactors (1,200–1,600 MW) for baseload, SMRs for remote desalination/industry, and a domestic fuel cycle. Prioritizes institutional governance and IAEA Comprehensive Safeguards ahead of vendor selection. Maintains "strategic optionality" on uranium enrichment — building exploration/yellowcake capability and negotiating leverage without prematurely committing to (or precluding) domestic enrichment. Driven by energy diversification and water desalination.

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** National Strategy for Data and Artificial Intelligence (NSDAI) + "Project Transcendence" / HUMAIN
- **Body:** Saudi Data and AI Authority (SDAIA); Public Investment Fund (PIF)
- **Year:** 2020 (NSDAI); execution vehicles 2024–2026; 2026 = "Year of AI"
- **URL:** https://saudipedia.com/en/national-strategy-for-data-and-ai-nsdai ; https://vision2030.ai/sectors/technology/ai-strategy/
- **Summary:** Aims for a top-15 global AI position by 2030 (20,000+ specialists, 300 startups); ~66 Vision 2030 objectives tie to AI. The PIF-backed "Project Transcendence" (~$100B) builds sovereign compute, mega data centers, and state-backed champion HUMAIN. Prioritizes Arabic-language models (ALLaM) and data sovereignty, partnering with global hyperscalers (e.g. a prospective $5–10B Google hub).

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** "Piloting the WEF Quantum Economy Blueprint" (national quantum roadmap)
- **Body:** Centre for the Fourth Industrial Revolution (C4IR) Saudi Arabia, with the World Economic Forum
- **Year:** December 2025
- **URL:** https://www.c4irsaudiarabia.org ; https://reports.weforum.org/docs/WEF_Piloting_the_Quantum_Economy_Blueprint_Lessons_from_Saudi_Arabia_2026.pdf
- **Summary:** First nation to pilot the WEF Quantum Economy Blueprint, embedding quantum into the economy (cybersecurity, energy, healthcare, logistics) to avoid a "quantum divide." Pragmatic "access model" — cloud partnerships and joint research rather than full domestic hardware ownership. Prioritizes talent and deploying quantum-safe cryptography (PQC/QKD) across smart-city infrastructure (NEOM), incl. Hybrid Quantum-Classical Architectures.

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** The National Biotechnology Strategy
- **Body:** Launched by the Crown Prince; implemented via Ministry of Health, Ministry of Investment, SFDA
- **Year:** January 2024
- **URL:** https://saudipedia.com/en/national-biotechnology-strategy
- **Summary:** Aims to make the Kingdom a global biotech hub by 2040 — biotech contributing 3% of non-oil GDP (~SAR 130B / $34.7B impact; 11,000 jobs by 2030). Four pillars: vaccine localization, biomanufacturing (biologics/biosimilars), genomics/precision medicine (Saudi Biobank), and agricultural biotech for food security. Targets 2.5% of GDP to R&D by 2040 and a pioneering longevity focus; aggressively courts FDI.

## 5. Materials Science / Mining
- **Plan:** National Industrial Strategy (NIS) + National Minerals Program
- **Body:** Ministry of Industry and Mineral Resources (MoIMR); Ma'aden; PIF (Manara Minerals)
- **Year:** 2022 (NIS); 2024–25 mining revaluation
- **URL:** https://www.vision2030.gov.sa/en/explore/strategies/national-industrial-strategy
- **Summary:** Mining elevated to the "third pillar" of industry; mineral wealth revalued to ~SAR 9.375T ($2.5T) across the Arabian Shield (transition metals, gold, rare earths). Targets 36,000 factories by 2035 and mining GDP of ~SAR 240B ($64B) by 2030 (200,000 jobs), subsidizing up to 75% of exploration-development costs. Ma'aden expands domestic extraction (lithium, copper, REEs); PIF's Manara Minerals secures global assets — positioning the Kingdom as a non-aligned critical-minerals node.

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** National Semiconductor Hub (NSH) + Alat semiconductor strategy
- **Body:** PIF (via Alat); Research, Development and Innovation Authority (RDIA); KACST
- **Year:** 2024
- **URL:** https://saudipress.com/saudi-arabia-s-national-semiconductor-hub-... ; https://www.eetimes.com/how-egypt-saudi-arabia-and-oman-are-building-the-middle-east-chip-future/
- **Summary:** A phased, "fabless-first" entry — the NSH aims to attract 50+ chip-design firms and train 5,000 IC-design engineers by 2030 (SAR 1B VC fund + SAR 150M product support). Alat (a $100B PIF company) targets power, perception, and processing chips, linking design to demand from NEOM and AI infrastructure. Projected ~$9.3B non-oil GDP and ~39,000 jobs by 2030.

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** Digital Economy Policy + ICT Sector Strategy / Digital Government Strategy 2023–2030
- **Body:** Ministry of Communications and IT (MCIT); Digital Government Authority (DGA)
- **Year:** 2020 (Digital Economy Policy); 2023–24 updates
- **URL:** https://www.mcit.gov.sa/sites/default/files/digitaleconomypolicy_en.pdf
- **Summary:** A >$1T digital-economy ecosystem (incl. $24.8B+ already into infrastructure). Targets 1,300 MW of data-center capacity by 2030, 99% internet penetration, and 4,000+ digitized public services (rising UN e-gov rankings). Hosts global cloud providers (Microsoft, Oracle, Tencent) under data-sovereignty/digital-trust frameworks; fosters startups via the National Technology Development Program. The prerequisite layer for AI, autonomy, and smart cities.

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** GACA Advanced Air Mobility (AAM) Roadmap (aligned with the National Transport & Logistics Strategy; NEOM testbeds)
- **Body:** General Authority of Civil Aviation (GACA), with NEOM and the Ministry of Transport
- **Year:** January 2024
- **URL:** https://gaca.gov.sa/-/media/Files/PDF/Reports/Air-Mobility-Reports/2024-01-22-Air-Mobility-Report-Vision-EN.pdf
- **Summary:** Backed by a ~$100B aviation investment pledge, the AAM Roadmap charts a path from Visual to fully Automated Flight Rules (AFR) to integrate eVTOLs and logistics drones (with Boeing, KAUST), assessing vertiports and air-traffic management. NEOM/The Line serve as greenfield sandboxes for driverless shuttles, robotaxis, and drones (no legacy roads/lights). Targets up to ~$38B in AAM economic opportunity by 2040; commercial drones regulated via GACA Remote Pilot Certificates.
