# Saudi Arabia — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Entity 26 (Batch 5).*

Saudi Arabia runs an aggressive, state-led pivot from a hydrocarbon to a knowledge economy under **Vision 2030**, with the Public Investment Fund (PIF) and bespoke vehicles (SDAIA, HUMAIN, Alat, Manara, K.A.CARE) deploying trillions to build full-stack ecosystems (compute, fabs, reactors, mines) plus human capital and regulation. Giga-projects like NEOM act as regulatory sandboxes. Distinctive: nuclear ambitions with "strategic optionality" on enrichment, and a newly revalued $2.5T mineral base.

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** Saudi National Atomic Energy Project (SNAEP)
- **Body:** King Abdullah City for Atomic and Renewable Energy (K.A.CARE), under the Council of Ministers
- **Year:** 2017 (Cabinet approval); 2022 (Nuclear Holding Company); November 2025 (US–Saudi 123 Joint Declaration)
- **URL:** https://world-nuclear.org/information-library/country-profiles/countries-o-s/saudi-arabia ; https://www.state.gov
- **Summary:** Three components: large reactors (1,200–1,600 MW) for baseload, SMRs for remote desalination/industry, and a domestic fuel cycle. Prioritizes institutional governance and IAEA Comprehensive Safeguards ahead of vendor selection. Maintains "strategic optionality" on uranium enrichment — building exploration/yellowcake capability and negotiating leverage without prematurely committing to (or precluding) domestic enrichment. During Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman's November 2025 Washington visit, the US and Saudi Arabia signed a Joint Declaration completing negotiations on a civil-nuclear "123 Agreement," opening the path for US export of reactor components and SMR technology subject to a bilateral nonproliferation/IAEA-safeguards compromise. Driven by energy diversification and water desalination.

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** National Strategy for Data and Artificial Intelligence (NSDAI) + "Project Transcendence" / HUMAIN
- **Body:** Saudi Data and AI Authority (SDAIA); Public Investment Fund (PIF)
- **Year:** 2020 (NSDAI); HUMAIN launched May 2025; AI Adoption Framework Nov 2025; 2026 = "Year of AI" (Cabinet, March 2026)
- **URL:** https://sdaia.gov.sa ; https://www.pif.gov.sa
- **Summary:** Aims for a top-15 global AI position by 2030 (20,000+ specialists, 300 startups); ~66 Vision 2030 objectives tie to AI. The PIF-backed "Project Transcendence" (~$100B) builds sovereign compute, mega data centers, and the state-backed champion HUMAIN, launched by PIF in May 2025 to run gigawatt-scale data centers and the ALLaM Arabic models. In November 2025 the US Commerce Department authorized export of advanced Nvidia Blackwell (GB300) AI chips to HUMAIN, and SDAIA published a binding AI Adoption Framework governing public-sector procurement, data governance and risk. The Cabinet designated 2026 the "Year of Artificial Intelligence" (March 2026), unifying a national roadmap around mega-compute (e.g. the 480 MW Hexagon data center) and the SAMAI goal of training 1M+ citizens; HUMAIN has since invested in xAI and partners with global hyperscalers.

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** WEF Quantum Economy Blueprint pilot + National Quantum Alliance (national quantum roadmap)
- **Body:** C4IR Saudi Arabia / World Economic Forum; RDIA (National Quantum Alliance); Aramco–Pasqal
- **Year:** 2025 (Blueprint pilot, National Quantum Alliance at LEAP 2025); May 2026 (first quantum computer)
- **URL:** https://www.c4irsaudiarabia.org ; https://www.weforum.org/agenda/2026/04/5-lessons-from-piloting-the-quantum-economy-blueprint-in-saudi-arabia/
- **Summary:** First nation to pilot the WEF Quantum Economy Blueprint, embedding quantum into the economy (cybersecurity, energy, healthcare, logistics) to avoid a "quantum divide." At LEAP 2025 the Kingdom launched the National Quantum Alliance, convening government, academia and industry under RDIA. The previously pragmatic "access model" (cloud partnerships, PQC/QKD across NEOM, Hybrid Quantum-Classical Architectures) gained domestic hardware: in May 2026 Aramco and Pasqal inaugurated Saudi Arabia's first quantum computer at Dhahran and launched the Middle East's first commercial Quantum-Computing-as-a-Service (QCaaS) platform for industrial, energy and materials problems.

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** The National Biotechnology Strategy
- **Body:** Launched by the Crown Prince; implemented via Ministry of Health, Ministry of Investment, SFDA
- **Year:** January 2024
- **URL:** https://saudipedia.com/en/national-biotechnology-strategy
- **Summary:** Aims to make the Kingdom a global biotech hub by 2040 — biotech contributing 3% of non-oil GDP (~SAR 130B / $34.7B impact; 11,000 jobs by 2030). Four pillars: vaccine localization, biomanufacturing (biologics/biosimilars), genomics/precision medicine (Saudi Biobank), and agricultural biotech for food security. Targets 2.5% of GDP to R&D by 2040 and a pioneering longevity focus; aggressively courts FDI.

## 5. Materials Science / Mining
- **Plan:** National Industrial Strategy (NIS) + National Minerals Program + US–Saudi Critical Minerals Framework
- **Body:** Ministry of Industry and Mineral Resources (MoIMR); Ma'aden; PIF (Manara Minerals)
- **Year:** 2022 (NIS); $2.5T endowment revaluation (late 2025); Manara spin-off + US–Saudi framework (2026)
- **URL:** https://www.vision2030.gov.sa/en/explore/strategies/national-industrial-strategy ; https://mim.gov.sa
- **Summary:** Mining elevated to the "third pillar" of industry; mineral wealth revalued upward from ~$1.3T to ~SAR 9.375T ($2.5T) in late 2025 across the Arabian Shield (transition metals, gold, rare earths). Targets 36,000 factories by 2035 and mining GDP of ~SAR 240B ($64B) by 2030 (200,000 jobs), subsidizing up to 75% of exploration-development costs. In January 2026 PIF announced spinning off Manara Minerals into an independent operator with deepened technical capability, and the Kingdom signed a critical-minerals MOU with Canada (Feb 2026). A US–Saudi Critical Minerals Framework (2026) — including a US DoD-backed stake in a domestic rare-earths refinery (with MP Materials) — positions Saudi Arabia as a non-aligned node reducing joint dependence on Chinese mineral processing.

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** National Semiconductor Hub (NSH); Alat exits chip fabrication (Apr 2026)
- **Body:** Research, Development and Innovation Authority (RDIA); KACST; Ministry of Investment; PIF (Alat)
- **Year:** 2024 (NSH); 2026 (Alat strategic pivot)
- **URL:** https://www.eetimes.com/how-egypt-saudi-arabia-and-oman-are-building-the-middle-east-chip-future/ ; https://www.semafor.com/article/04/09/2026/saudis-alat-removes-head-drops-chip-ambitions
- **Summary:** A phased, "fabless-first" entry — the NSH aims to attract 50+ chip-design firms and train 5,000 IC-design engineers by 2030 (SAR 1B VC fund + SAR 150M product support), with AI chipmaker EdgeCortix joining the hub in Feb 2025. The semiconductor agenda was sharply scaled back in April 2026: in a PIF spending review, the $100B vehicle Alat dismissed its founding CEO and abandoned its ambitions to enter chip production, reallocating to AI infrastructure, data centers and device assembly (PCs/servers with Lenovo). The Kingdom thus retains a design/ecosystem play (RDIA/KACST/NSH) while shelving sovereign fabrication, with prior projections of ~$9.3B non-oil GDP and ~39,000 jobs by 2030 now uncertain.

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** Digital Economy Policy + ICT Sector Strategy / Digital Government Strategy 2023–2030
- **Body:** Ministry of Communications and IT (MCIT); Digital Government Authority (DGA)
- **Year:** 2020 (Digital Economy Policy); 2023–24 updates; Hexagon data center launched Jan 2026
- **URL:** https://www.mcit.gov.sa/sites/default/files/digitaleconomypolicy_en.pdf ; https://spa.gov.sa/en/N2269411
- **Summary:** A >$1T digital-economy ecosystem (incl. $24.8B+ already into infrastructure). Targets 1,300 MW of data-center capacity by 2030, 99% internet penetration, and 4,000+ digitized public services (rising UN e-gov rankings). In January 2026 SDAIA launched the Tier IV Hexagon data center in Riyadh — described as the world's largest government data center at 480 MW capacity — and by Q1 2026 the Kingdom ranked 2nd globally (after the US) in data-center market attractiveness with ~467 MW operational. Hosts global cloud providers (Microsoft, Oracle, Tencent) under data-sovereignty/digital-trust frameworks; fosters startups via the National Technology Development Program. The prerequisite layer for AI, autonomy, and smart cities.

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** GACA Advanced Air Mobility (AAM) Roadmap (aligned with the National Transport & Logistics Strategy; NEOM testbeds)
- **Body:** General Authority of Civil Aviation (GACA), with NEOM and the Ministry of Transport
- **Year:** January 2024
- **URL:** https://gaca.gov.sa/-/media/Files/PDF/Reports/Air-Mobility-Reports/2024-01-22-Air-Mobility-Report-Vision-EN.pdf
- **Summary:** Backed by a ~$100B aviation investment pledge, the AAM Roadmap charts a path from Visual to fully Automated Flight Rules (AFR) to integrate eVTOLs and logistics drones (with Boeing, KAUST), assessing vertiports and air-traffic management. NEOM/The Line serve as greenfield sandboxes for driverless shuttles, robotaxis, and drones (no legacy roads/lights). Targets up to ~$38B in AAM economic opportunity by 2040; commercial drones regulated via GACA Remote Pilot Certificates.
