# United States — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Country 1 of 5 (Batch 1). OECD bloc.*

The U.S. uses a decentralized, "whole-of-government" model coordinated by the White House Office of Science and Technology Policy (OSTP) and the National Science and Technology Council (NSTC), rather than a single technology ministry. Geopolitical competition (supply-chain resilience, national security) is the dominant driver, and most plans favor public-private partnerships.

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** Office of Nuclear Energy: Strategic Vision
- **Agency:** U.S. Department of Energy (DOE), Office of Nuclear Energy
- **Year:** 2021
- **URL:** https://www.energy.gov/sites/prod/files/2021/01/f82/DOE-NE%20Strategic%20Vision%20-%2001.08.2021.pdf
- **Summary:** Aims to sustain the existing light-water reactor fleet while accelerating advanced reactors (SMRs, microreactors). Targets include a commercial U.S. microreactor by 2025, two advanced reactor designs deployed via cost-shared partnerships by 2028, and widespread accident-tolerant fuels by 2030. Treats domestic HALEU supply as an urgent national-security priority; leverages public-private partnerships and national labs.

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** National Artificial Intelligence Research and Development Strategic Plan — 2023 Update
- **Agency:** NSTC Select Committee on AI / White House OSTP
- **Year:** 2023
- **URL:** https://www.nitrd.gov/pubs/National-Artificial-Intelligence-Research-and-Development-Strategic-Plan-2023-Update.pdf
- **Summary:** Nine strategic pillars to maintain U.S. AI leadership while managing ethical, societal, and security risks. Commits to long-term fundamental research, shared public datasets, and robust safety evaluation frameworks/standards. Emphasizes public-private partnerships, an "AI-ready" workforce, and coordinated international collaboration.

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** National Strategic Overview for Quantum Information Science
- **Agency:** NSTC Subcommittee on Quantum Information Science
- **Year:** 2018 (alongside the National Quantum Initiative Act)
- **URL:** https://www.quantum.gov/wp-content/uploads/2020/10/2018_NSTC_National_Strategic_Overview_QIS.pdf
- **Summary:** Establishes a "science-first" national approach to quantum computing, sensing, and communications. Backed by the $1.2B National Quantum Initiative Act (2018); mandates expanded QIS infrastructure, multidisciplinary research centers, and a "quantum-smart" workforce. Prioritizes post-quantum cryptography transition and standards-setting with trusted allies.

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** Bold Goals for U.S. Biotechnology and Biomanufacturing
- **Agency:** White House OSTP (with DOE, USDA, DOC, HHS, NSF)
- **Year:** 2023 (responsive to Executive Order 14081, Sept. 2022)
- **URL:** https://bidenwhitehouse.archives.gov/wp-content/uploads/2023/03/Bold-Goals-for-U.S.-Biotechnology-and-Biomanufacturing-Harnessing-Research-and-Development-To-Further-Societal-Goals-FINAL.pdf
- **Summary:** Sets targets across climate, food innovation, human health, and supply-chain resilience. Backed by $1B+ in federal/DoD funding; goals include expanding cell-based therapies (75% patient viability) and using AI to accelerate therapeutic discovery and bioproduction. Commits agencies to scaling domestic biomanufacturing and shifting to low-carbon bio-based chemicals/materials.

## 5. Materials Science
- **Plan:** Materials Genome Initiative Strategic Plan
- **Agency:** NSTC
- **Year:** 2021
- **URL:** https://www.mgi.gov/sites/mgi/files/MGI-2021-Strategic-Plan.pdf
- **Summary:** Aims to accelerate discovery, manufacturing, and deployment of advanced materials at lower cost. Unifies the "Materials Innovation Infrastructure" — integrating computational modeling, autonomous/high-throughput experimentation, and large-scale data (with AI/ML). Expands the interdisciplinary materials workforce to support defense, clean energy, and sustainable semiconductors.

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** National Strategy on Microelectronics Research (Amended)
- **Agency:** NSTC Subcommittee on Microelectronics Leadership / OSTP
- **Year:** 2023 (amended/re-released 2024–25)
- **URL:** https://www.whitehouse.gov/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/Amended-National-Strategy-on-Microelectronics-Research.pdf
- **Summary:** Maximizes impact of CHIPS and Science Act investments via a five-year R&D framework to revitalize domestic semiconductor manufacturing. Focuses on advanced packaging, novel circuit designs, post-quantum hardware security, and bridging the "lab-to-fab" gap. Prioritizes supply-chain resilience and a domestic microelectronics workforce.

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** U.S. International Cyberspace and Digital Policy Strategy
- **Agency:** U.S. Department of State
- **Year:** 2024
- **URL:** https://2021-2025.state.gov/united-states-international-cyberspace-and-digital-policy-strategy/
- **Summary:** Introduces "digital solidarity" — aligning democratic allies around an open, secure, rights-respecting digital ecosystem. Four action areas: resilient digital infrastructure, ethical data governance, responsible state behavior in cyberspace, and cyber capacity-building for partners. Mobilizes diplomacy and foreign assistance to counter cyber threats and secure telecom supply chains.

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** Unmanned Systems Integrated Roadmap FY2013-2038 (defense framework; supplemented by branch plans e.g. 2023 Coast Guard Unmanned Systems Strategic Plan)
- **Agency:** U.S. Department of Defense
- **Year:** 2013/2014 (evolving via branch-specific strategies 2023/24)
- **URL:** https://apps.dtic.mil/sti/tr/pdf/ADA592015.pdf
- **Note:** The U.S. lacks a unified *civilian* national robotics/autonomy strategy; policy is defense-led and otherwise fragmented across agencies. Pending legislation (e.g. proposed Unmanned Autonomous Systems Strategy Act) reflects urgency to scale low-cost AI-enabled drones.
- **Summary:** A 25-year DoD roadmap for integrating uncrewed systems across air/land/sea. Prioritizes highly autonomous platforms, scalable sensor networks, and "manned-unmanned teaming" (MUM-T). Emphasizes rapid R&D-to-field transition, cybersecurity, interoperability, and resilient domestic supply chains.
