# Poland — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Entity 31 (Batch 6). OECD/EU/CEE.*

Poland is pivoting from a heavy-industry base toward deep-tech, codified in a matrix of state strategies (rooted in the Strategy for Responsible Development) and heavily co-funded by EU cohesion funds and the National Recovery Plan (KPO). The Ministry of Digital Affairs is unusually central (AI, quantum, semiconductors). Big swings: the first nuclear plant (Westinghouse AP1000 at Choczewo, ~2036) and a pioneering early lead in drone/U-space regulation.

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** Polish Nuclear Power Programme (Program polskiej energetyki jądrowej, PPEJ)
- **Body:** Council of Ministers / Ministry of Industry / Government Plenipotentiary for Strategic Energy Infrastructure
- **Year:** 2014 (original); 2020 (major update); 2025 update in public consultation
- **URL:** https://www.gov.pl/web/przemysl/konsultacje-publiczne-zaktualizowanego-programu-polskiej-energetyki-jadrowej
- **Summary:** Aims for 6–9 GWe of Gen III/III+ nuclear to decarbonize a coal-heavy grid and ensure security. First plant at Lubiatowo-Kopalino (Choczewo) with Westinghouse (AP1000) + Bechtel, first reactor ~2033–2036, units through 2043. The Ministry of Industry directed an updated PPEJ to public consultations on 25 June 2025, extending the strategic investment horizon to 2070 and confirming the Choczewo/AP1000 build while clarifying the financing and state-aid framework; a major investment-framework and progress update followed in mid-2026. The Ministry is also developing parallel SMR roadmaps (sites incl. Włocławek, Stawy Monowskie) for industrial decarbonization.

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** Policy for the Development of Artificial Intelligence in Poland from 2020 (+ extension to 2030); AI Fund (Fundusz Sztucznej Inteligencji) and PL-Guard
- **Body:** Council of Ministers / Ministry of Digital Affairs (AI Fund jointly with Ministry of National Defence; PL-Guard by NASK)
- **Year:** December 2020 (2030 update in progress); AI Fund 2024, PL-Guard July 2025
- **URL:** https://www.gov.pl/web/ai/polityka-dla-rozwoju-sztucznej-inteligencji-w-polsce-od-roku-2020
- **Summary:** Six pillars (society, innovative companies, science, education, international cooperation, public sector) aimed at making Poland a creator, not just consumer, of AI. Builds research-to-market pipelines via digital sandboxes and grants; bolstered by the "PL/AI" advisory team and the "AI HUB Poland" portal, aligned with the EU AI Act. In 2024–2025 the government added concrete instruments: an inter-ministerial AI Fund (Fundusz Sztucznej Inteligencji), launched with the Ministry of National Defence to finance sovereign AI capacity, and PL-Guard (HerBERT-PL-Guard), a native Polish-language AI safety/moderation model released by NASK on 3 July 2025 to defend against prompt injection and algorithmic risks. Together they signal a shift toward "sovereign AI" — domestic models and a dedicated financing vehicle rather than strategy text alone.

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** "Poland in the Quantum Era" — assumptions for the National Quantum Technology Development Policy (Polska w erze kwantowej)
- **Body:** Ministry of Digital Affairs
- **Year:** 2024/2025 (consultations Aug/Sep 2025)
- **URL:** https://www.gov.pl/web/cyfryzacja/polska-w-erze-kwantowej-czyli-zalozenia-polityki-rozwoju-technologii-kwantowych
- **Summary:** Closes a long-standing policy gap (despite strong academic quantum/cryptography tradition), committing ≥€1B over ten years for "quantum sovereignty." Pillars: national infrastructure (the PIONIER-Q quantum communication network), post-quantum cryptography for critical infrastructure, and talent cultivation. Responds to the European Quantum Pact and emphasizes the civilian/military dual-use nature of quantum tech.

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** Government Plan for the Development of the Biomedical Sector 2022–2031
- **Body:** Council of Ministers / Ministry of Health / Medical Research Agency (ABM)
- **Year:** July 2022
- **URL:** https://www.gov.pl/web/zdrowie (Biomedical Sector Development resolution)
- **Summary:** A COVID-prompted push to make Poland the CEE biomedical leader ("Polish Medical Valley"). Four domains: personalized medicine, biological drugs/API manufacturing, translational medicine/clinical trials, and advanced diagnostics. Standout target: discover, develop, and authorize the first fully indigenous innovative Polish drug by 2030, coordinated and funded by the ABM toward commercially scalable projects and national drug security.

## 5. Materials Science / Critical Minerals
- **Plan:** State Raw Materials Policy (Polityka Surowcowa Państwa, PSP2050)
- **Body:** Council of Ministers / Ministry of Climate and Environment / Polish Geological Institute (PIG)
- **Year:** March 2022
- **URL:** https://www.gov.pl/web/klimat/polityka-surowcowa-panstwa
- **Summary:** Governs the full minerals value chain to 2050, identifying strategic and EU-critical raw materials for economic independence and the green transition. Priorities: legally protecting documented geological deposits from being sterilized by development, minimizing geological investment risk, and a strong circular-economy push for secondary-material recovery. The Polish Geological Institute (PIG-PIB) executes via annual geological plans.

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** "Poland in the Game for the Future" — Policy for the Semiconductor Sector 2026+ (Polska w grze o przyszłość)
- **Body:** Ministry of Digital Affairs
- **Year:** 2026 (retitled "...2026+"; on Council of Ministers legislative works list Jan 2026; consultations Mar–Apr 2026; pre-adoption)
- **URL:** https://www.gov.pl/web/cyfryzacja/kolejny-krok-na-drodze-do-polityki-rozwoju-technologii-polprzewodnikowych
- **Summary:** A focused-niche strategy (not full end-to-end) targeting Poland's strengths: back-end processing (test/packaging — already #1 in Europe for back-end investment attractiveness per Kearney), photonics, and wide-bandgap materials (GaN, SiC). Commits ≥800M PLN capex to build ≥1 national pilot production line by 2028 and doubles the IC-designer pool from 1,500 to 3,000 by 2030, leveraging the EU Chips Act. The draft was retitled "Poland in the Game for the Future — Policy for the Semiconductor Sector 2026+", entered the Council of Ministers' legislative and programming works list in January 2026, and underwent inter-ministerial agreement and public consultations from 2 March to 2 April 2026; it now awaits formal Council of Ministers adoption.

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** National Broadband Plan (Narodowy Plan Szerokopasmowy, NPS)
- **Body:** Council of Ministers / Ministry of Digital Affairs
- **Year:** 2014 (original); 2020 (major update); 2026–2030 update in consultation (from late 2025)
- **URL:** https://www.gov.pl/web/cyfryzacja/narodowy-plan-szerokopasmowy
- **Summary:** The architecture for digital expansion and ending digital exclusion (esp. eastern Poland), aligned with the EU gigabit-society targets. Mandates universal ≥100 Mbps (upgradable to gigabit) for all households, with 1 Gbps direct connections for schools, hospitals, and public hubs, plus full 5G along major corridors and urban centers. Heavily uses EU cohesion funds and cuts bureaucratic barriers for operators. After the prior plan's targets reached 2025 and a Supreme Audit Office (NIK) report flagged infrastructure delays, the Ministry of Digital Affairs opened work in late 2025 on a "National Broadband Plan 2026–2030" focused on closing remaining "white spots" and delivering full gigabit access to all households by 2030.

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** White Paper on the Unmanned Aerial Vehicle Market (Biała Księga Rynku Bezzałogowych Statków Powietrznych); operationalized by ULC Guidelines No. 7/2025 (Wytyczne Nr 7/2025)
- **Body:** Ministry of Infrastructure / Polish Economic Institute (PIE); Civil Aviation Authority (ULC)
- **Year:** 2019; Guidelines No. 7/2025 issued 28 March 2025
- **URL:** https://www.gov.pl/web/infrastruktura/biala-ksiega-rynku-bezzalogowych-statkow-powietrznych
- **Summary:** A pioneering European blueprint for integrating drones into low-level airspace via "U-space," thanks to permissive early legislation. Prioritizes commercial BVLOS operations (logistics, agriculture, infrastructure monitoring), projecting the drone market at 3.26B PLN by 2026 with macroeconomic spillovers up to 576B PLN. Backed by the state air-navigation provider's PansaUTM digital traffic-management system as the backbone for safe autonomous drone scaling. In a key 2025 regulatory step, the President of the Civil Aviation Authority (ULC) issued Guidelines No. 7/2025 on 28 March 2025, establishing the accepted means of compliance for structuring airspace and standing up U-space infrastructure to support autonomous UAV operations.
