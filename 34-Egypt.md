# Egypt — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Entity 34 (Batch 7). MENA/Africa.*

Egypt pursues a knowledge-economy transition under **Egypt Vision 2030**, leveraging Eastern and Western partnerships (Rosatom for nuclear; multinationals for AI/chips) and a large youth demographic. Notable contrast: AI, digital, and biotech are heavily promoted, while autonomous drones are tightly securitized under military control. Flagship: the El Dabaa nuclear plant (4× VVER-1200) under construction.

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** El Dabaa Nuclear Power Plant Project (within the Vision 2030 energy mix)
- **Body:** Nuclear Power Plants Authority (NPPA), Ministry of Electricity; regulator ENRRA
- **Year:** 2017 (main Rosatom contracts); construction 2022–2030
- **URL:** https://nppa.gov.eg
- **Summary:** Four Gen III+ VVER-1200 reactors (1,200 MW each) built with Russia's Rosatom (BOO-style lifecycle fuel supply, spent-fuel management, training). Targets 9–10% of national electricity by 2030, freeing natural gas for export. First concrete poured Unit 1 (July 2022), Unit 4 (Jan 2024); first units commercial 2028–2030. Framed as "technological national security."

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** National Artificial Intelligence Strategy — Second Edition (2025–2030)
- **Body:** Ministry of Communications and IT (MCIT) / National Council for AI (NCAI)
- **Year:** 2025
- **URL:** https://ai.gov.eg
- **Summary:** Pivots toward "Sovereign AI" — local foundation models and Arabic-language data — across six pillars (governance, technology, data, infrastructure, ecosystem, talent). Targets AI+ICT at 7.7% of GDP by 2030, training 30,000 AI specialists, 250+ AI startups, and 36% daily public AI-tool use. Invests in national compute/GPU and Arabic data repositories for a National Foundation Model.

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** National Strategy for Quantum Computing (in drafting/workshop phase)
- **Body:** Academy of Scientific Research and Technology (ASRT) / Electronics Research Institute (ERI)
- **Year:** 2026 (inaugural drafting workshops Feb 2026)
- **URL:** https://eri.sci.eg
- **Summary:** Nascent — formulating a foundational roadmap via the inaugural "Workshop on the National Strategy for Quantum Computing" (Feb 2026). Heavy reliance on international capacity-building, notably with the Joint Institute for Nuclear Research (JINR) on quantum computing and machine learning, to train talent and integrate Egyptian scientists into global research networks before codifying a national strategy.

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** Egyptian Reference Genome Project (+ National Strategy for Genetic Engineering and Biotechnology)
- **Body:** ASRT / Egypt Center for Research and Regenerative Medicine (ECRRM, military-affiliated)
- **Year:** Launched 2021/2022 (Phase 1: 2022–2027)
- **URL:** https://egp.sci.eg
- **Summary:** A $62M (first five years) mega-project mapping the Egyptian population's genetic variants for precision medicine and gene therapy. Targets sequencing 100,000 healthy citizens, 8,000 with rare/genetic diseases, and DNA from 200 ancient mummies, building Africa's largest automated biobank. Led by the military-affiliated ECRRM (presidential decree, 2017) — framed as national biological-data sovereignty.

## 5. Materials Science / Critical Minerals
- **Plan:** Mining Sector Expansion Strategy / 2024–2030 Economic Strategy for Mineral Resources
- **Body:** Ministry of Petroleum and Mineral Resources / Mineral Resources and Mining Industries Authority (EMRA)
- **Year:** 2024–2030 (regulatory reforms from 2019)
- **URL:** https://petroleum.gov.eg
- **Summary:** Aims to raise mining's GDP contribution from <1% to 6% by 2030 by exploiting the Arabian-Nubian Shield. The 2019 Mineral Resources Law amendment replaced the deterrent production-sharing model with a competitive tax/royalty system to attract FDI; a first-in-40-years airborne geophysical survey de-risks exploration (gold target 800,000 oz/yr, plus copper, phosphates, REEs). Emphasizes local beneficiation (e.g. a $658M phosphoric-acid complex; 60–80% local content).

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** Egypt Makes Electronics (EME) presidential initiative
- **Body:** MCIT / Information Technology Industry Development Agency (ITIDA)
- **Year:** Launched 2015 (Phase III programs active 2023–2026)
- **URL:** https://itida.gov.eg/English/Programs/egyptmakeselectronics/
- **Summary:** Positions Egypt as a MENA/Africa hub for electronics design and embedded systems (and labor-intensive manufacturing), leveraging 700,000+ annual graduates. Offers equity-free grants up to EGP 1M (EME Incubation Program at Knowledge City), tax breaks, land discounts, and "Train to Hire." Results include a 45% rise in design centers over two years and an STMicroelectronics R&D hub in Maadi.

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** Digital Egypt (Misr Al Raqamiya) / ICT Strategy 2030
- **Body:** Ministry of Communications and Information Technology (MCIT)
- **Year:** 2017 (continuously updated; 2022–2026 offshoring strategy)
- **URL:** https://digital.gov.eg
- **Summary:** Master roadmap to digitize public services, expand broadband, and grow a digital economy (ICT is the fastest-growing sector). Flagship human-capital push: EGP 1.1B/yr training (a 22× increase over three years) to train 200,000 youth annually via the Digital Egypt Builders Initiative (fully funded master's in cybersecurity, data science). ~$3.5B in infrastructure to make Egypt Africa's premier outsourcing/data-routing hub; Creativa innovation hubs nationwide.

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** Law No. 216 of 2017 on the Regulation of the Use of Motorized or Wireless Drones
- **Body:** Ministry of Defense (with the Egyptian Civil Aviation Authority)
- **Year:** 2017
- **URL:** https://civilaviation.gov.eg
- **Summary:** A stark contrast to Egypt's other tech liberalization — one of the world's most restrictive drone regimes. Blanket prohibition on importing, manufacturing, possessing, or operating any drone without explicit prior Ministry of Defense authorization; violations carry military jurisdiction (EGP 5,000–50,000 fines, 1–7 years imprisonment, confiscation). Recreational use banned; commercial use needs rare multi-agency clearance. Ground robotics (e.g. agricultural AI robots, Egrobots) are permitted in state-sanctioned applications.
