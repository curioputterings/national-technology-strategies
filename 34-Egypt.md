# Egypt — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Entity 34 (Batch 7). MENA/Africa.*

Egypt pursues a knowledge-economy transition under **Egypt Vision 2030**, leveraging Eastern and Western partnerships (Rosatom for nuclear; multinationals for AI/chips) and a large youth demographic. Notable contrast: AI, digital, and biotech are heavily promoted, while autonomous drones are tightly securitized under military control. Flagship: the El Dabaa nuclear plant (4× VVER-1200) under construction.

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** El Dabaa Nuclear Power Plant Project (within the Vision 2030 energy mix)
- **Body:** Nuclear Power Plants Authority (NPPA), Ministry of Electricity; regulator ENRRA
- **Year:** 2017 (main Rosatom contracts); construction 2022–2030; fuel order Nov 2025; Unit 1 commercial 2026
- **URL:** https://nppa.gov.eg
- **Summary:** Four Gen III+ VVER-1200 reactors (1,200 MW each) built with Russia's Rosatom (BOO-style lifecycle fuel supply, spent-fuel management, training). Targets 9–10% of national electricity by 2030, freeing natural gas for export. Momentum accelerated sharply in late 2025–2026: Unit 1's 330-tonne reactor pressure vessel arrived October 2025, and on 19 November 2025 (Egypt's Nuclear Energy Day) Presidents El-Sisi and Putin oversaw the formal signing of the nuclear fuel purchase order, legally cementing Rosatom's lifecycle fuel supply. The largest single shipment ever for one nuclear plant reached El Dabaa in May 2026 for Unit 1 startup, keeping Unit 1 on track to begin commercial electricity generation in 2026, and the IAEA concluded a 12-day Integrated Regulatory Review Service (IRRS) mission on 25 June 2026 praising Egypt's regulatory framework. Framed as "technological national security."

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** National Artificial Intelligence Strategy — Second Edition (2025–2030) + National Guidelines for Trustworthy and Responsible AI
- **Body:** Ministry of Communications and IT (MCIT) / National Council for AI (NCAI)
- **Year:** Strategy launched 14 July 2025; Trustworthy AI Guidelines published March 2026
- **URL:** https://ai.gov.eg
- **Summary:** Officially launched by MCIT Minister Amr Talaat on 14 July 2025, the Second Edition pivots toward "Sovereign AI" — local foundation models and Arabic-language data — across six pillars (governance, technology, data, infrastructure, ecosystem, talent). Targets AI+ICT at 7.7% of GDP by 2030, training 30,000 AI specialists, 250+ AI startups, and 36% daily public AI-tool use; underpinned by a five-year IBM agreement (May 2025) for capacity-building and AI governance. In March 2026 it was supplemented by the "National Guidelines for Trustworthy and Responsible AI," mandating ethical safeguards, governance and safety audits across public-sector AI procurement. Invests in national compute/GPU and Arabic data repositories for a National Foundation Model.

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** National Quantum Computing Strategy (governance consolidated; strategy pillars unveiled)
- **Body:** MCIT / National Council for Artificial Intelligence, Quantum Computing and Emerging Technologies (formerly ASRT / Electronics Research Institute)
- **Year:** 2026 (Council mandate expanded 14 Jan 2026; pillars unveiled 21 June 2026)
- **URL:** https://mcit.gov.eg
- **Summary:** Egypt moved from inaugural drafting workshops (Feb 2026) to a consolidated national effort: on 14 January 2026 the Cabinet renamed and expanded the AI Council into the National Council for Artificial Intelligence, Quantum Computing and Emerging Technologies, formally folding quantum into MCIT's emerging-tech governance. On 21 June 2026, at the BRICS Quantum Technologies Forum, MCIT unveiled the structural pillars of the national quantum strategy — talent development, quantum-hardware research partnerships, and readiness to integrate quantum solutions into national cybersecurity and economic frameworks. The effort still leans on international capacity-building (e.g. with the Joint Institute for Nuclear Research, JINR) to train talent before codifying a full strategy.

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** Egyptian Reference Genome Project (+ National Strategy for Genetic Engineering and Biotechnology)
- **Body:** Ministry of Higher Education and Scientific Research / Egypt Center for Research and Regenerative Medicine (ECRRM, military-affiliated)
- **Year:** Launched 2021/2022 (Phase 1: 2022–2027); Phase 1 results announced April 2026
- **URL:** https://egp.sci.eg
- **Summary:** A $62M (first five years) mega-project mapping the Egyptian population's genetic variants for precision medicine and gene therapy. Targets sequencing 100,000 healthy citizens, 8,000 with rare/genetic diseases, and DNA from 200 ancient mummies, building Africa's largest automated biobank. On 7 April 2026 the Ministry of Higher Education and Scientific Research announced formal Phase 1 results — the sequencing of 1,024 citizens, establishing the country's first "national genetic reference" and uncovering roughly 17 million unique genetic variants absent from global databases — positioned as the backbone of a new biotechnology and preventive-healthcare strategy. Led by the military-affiliated ECRRM (presidential decree, 2017) — framed as national biological-data sovereignty.

## 5. Materials Science / Critical Minerals
- **Plan:** Mining Sector Expansion Strategy / 2024–2030 Economic Strategy for Mineral Resources
- **Body:** Ministry of Petroleum and Mineral Resources / Mineral Resources and Mining Industries Authority (EMRA)
- **Year:** 2024–2030 (regulatory reforms from 2019; Executive Regulations amended April 2026)
- **URL:** https://petroleum.gov.eg
- **Summary:** Aims to raise mining's GDP contribution from <1% to 6% by 2030 by exploiting the Arabian-Nubian Shield. The 2019 Mineral Resources Law amendment replaced the deterrent production-sharing model with a competitive tax/royalty system to attract FDI; a first-in-40-years airborne geophysical survey de-risks exploration (gold target 800,000 oz/yr, plus copper, phosphates, REEs). On 29 April 2026 the Cabinet approved comprehensive amendments to the Executive Regulations of the Mineral Resources Law to ease foreign-investment bottlenecks, tighten licensing, and mandate ESG/environmental-sustainability practices, with a renewed 2026 push on aerial surveying for critical minerals (copper, lithium, rare earths) to feed the energy transition. Emphasizes local beneficiation (e.g. a $658M phosphoric-acid complex; 60–80% local content).

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** Egypt Makes Electronics (EME) presidential initiative
- **Body:** MCIT / Information Technology Industry Development Agency (ITIDA)
- **Year:** Launched 2015 (Phase III programs active 2023–2026); export track added May 2026
- **URL:** https://itida.gov.eg/English/Programs/egyptmakeselectronics/
- **Summary:** Positions Egypt as a MENA/Africa hub for electronics design and embedded systems (and labor-intensive manufacturing), leveraging 700,000+ annual graduates. Offers equity-free grants up to EGP 1M (EME Incubation Program at Knowledge City), tax breaks, land discounts, and "Train to Hire," with open-standard RISC-V IC-design now central to the localization push. On 25 May 2026 the government signed a protocol explicitly folding electronics design, semiconductors and embedded systems into Egypt's official Export Development Programme for seven years (from FY 2025/2026), adding performance-based incentives tied to job creation and high-value exports to attract fabless R&D centers; this followed an October 2025 ministerial visit to Vietnam's FPT (semiconductor and automotive-electronics investment). Results include a 45% rise in design centers over two years and an STMicroelectronics R&D hub in Maadi.

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** Digital Egypt (Misr Al Raqamiya) / ICT Strategy 2030
- **Body:** Ministry of Communications and Information Technology (MCIT) / National Telecommunications Regulatory Authority (NTRA)
- **Year:** 2017 (continuously updated; 2022–2026 offshoring strategy; 5G spectrum deal Feb 2026)
- **URL:** https://digital.gov.eg
- **Summary:** Master roadmap to digitize public services, expand broadband, and grow a digital economy (ICT is the fastest-growing sector). On 9 February 2026 MCIT and the NTRA finalized a landmark $3.5B agreement allocating 410 MHz of new spectrum to mobile operators — equal to all spectrum allocated in Egypt over the prior 30 years — to drive a 5G rollout and sovereign data-center investment. The 2022–2026 offshoring strategy hit ~$4.8B in IT/BPO exports across 270+ delivery centers by late 2025 (targeting $6B), and capacity-building targets were lifted to 800,000 trainees, extending the EGP 1.1B/yr Digital Egypt Builders push (fully funded master's in cybersecurity, data science). Creativa innovation hubs continue nationwide.

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** Law No. 216 of 2017 (drone regulation); 2026 defense co-production & civil aerial-monitoring programs
- **Body:** Ministry of Defense (with the Egyptian Civil Aviation Authority); Ministries of Local Development/Environment for civil monitoring
- **Year:** 2017 (regulation); 2026 (production and monitoring programs)
- **URL:** https://civilaviation.gov.eg
- **Summary:** A stark contrast to Egypt's other tech liberalization — one of the world's most restrictive drone regimes: blanket prohibition on importing, manufacturing, possessing, or operating any drone without explicit prior Ministry of Defense authorization, with violations under military jurisdiction. The posture began shifting in 2026 toward state-controlled capability-building: in February 2026 Egypt signed a ~$350M defense-cooperation agreement with Turkey for joint autonomous drone and ammunition production, and in April 2026 the government launched a nationwide network of AI-powered, spectral-imaging civil drones to track urbanization violations, manage nature reserves, and monitor seasonal pollution. Recreational use remains banned and commercial use needs rare multi-agency clearance; ground robotics (e.g. agricultural AI robots, Egrobots) remain permitted in state-sanctioned applications.
