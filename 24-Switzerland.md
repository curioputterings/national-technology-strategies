# Switzerland — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Entity 24 (Batch 5). OECD/EFTA.*

Switzerland favors decentralized innovation, strong state funding for research (the ETH Domain), and a pragmatic, sector-specific regulatory posture (preferring Council of Europe frameworks over EU-style omnibus laws). Being outside EU programs (Horizon/Digital Europe) has driven sovereignty initiatives (SwissChips, Swiss Quantum Initiative). Two notable shifts: reversing the 2017 ban on new nuclear reactors, and pioneering autonomy laws for both roads and airspace.

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** Amendment to the Nuclear Energy Act (Kernenergiegesetz) — indirect counter-proposal to the "Stop the Blackout" initiative
- **Body:** Federal Council, DETEC, Swiss Federal Office of Energy (SFOE)
- **Year:** Draft Aug/Sep 2024; Council of States approval March 2026
- **URL:** admin.ch (DETEC/SFOE)
- **Summary:** A major reversal of the 2017 ban (Energy Strategy 2050) on building new nuclear plants, restoring "technological openness" for energy security and 2050 net-zero. Current plants (Beznau I/II, Gösgen, Leibstadt; ~30–33% of electricity) have unlimited licenses. The Council of States passed the counter-proposal (27–13) in March 2026; the National Council debates next (decision by Aug 2026), with a likely 2027 referendum as Greens/Social Democrats oppose.

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** Guidelines on Artificial Intelligence for the Confederation + adoption of the Council of Europe Framework Convention on AI
- **Body:** Federal Council, Federal Office of Communications (OFCOM), Competence Network for AI (CNAI)
- **Year:** Guidelines 2020 (reviewed 2022/2024-25); CoE Convention signed 2024/25
- **URL:** https://www.bakom.admin.ch/en/ai-guidelines
- **Summary:** Deliberately avoids an EU-style omnibus AI act, using sector-specific regulation guided by seven 2020 principles (human-centric, transparent, accountable, secure). Signed the legally binding Council of Europe AI Convention (the first international AI treaty), to be ratified and implemented primarily for public-sector actors. In Feb 2025 the Federal Council confirmed a technology-neutral, sector-specific approach to preserve Switzerland's competitiveness.

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** Swiss Quantum Initiative (SQI)
- **Body:** SERI, Swiss Quantum Commission (hosted by SCNAT)
- **Year:** Launched May 2022; funding period 2025–2028
- **URL:** https://www.sbfi.admin.ch/en/swiss-quantum-initiative-en
- **Summary:** Consolidates Switzerland's strong quantum position across computing, sensing, and communication. CHF 82.1M for 2025–2028, split among SCNAT (CHF 22.9M, strategy/talent), the Swiss National Science Foundation (CHF 29.6M, research), and Innosuisse (CHF 29.6M, commercialization). Directed by the Swiss Quantum Commission (ETH Zurich, EPFL, PSI, IBM Research); partly a response to constrained EU-research association.

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** Swiss Personalized Health Network (SPHN) — Mandate 2025–2028
- **Body:** SERI and Federal Office of Public Health (FOPH); implemented by SAMS and SIB Swiss Institute of Bioinformatics
- **Year:** Established 2017; consolidation phase 2025–2028
- **URL:** https://sphn.ch
- **Summary:** National infrastructure making clinical/health data securely interoperable (FAIR principles) across university and cantonal hospitals via the secure BioMedIT network. After CHF 135M (2017–2024), CHF 20.7M committed for 2025–2028 to permanently anchor the Data Coordination Center. By 2024 had unlocked consented data from ~700,000–800,000 patients and 60+ projects; transitions to a sustainable fee-based model.

## 5. Materials Science
- **Plan:** ETH Board Strategic Plan 2025–2028 ("Advanced Materials and Key Technologies") + NCCR Muoniverse
- **Body:** ETH Board / Swiss National Science Foundation (SNSF) / SERI
- **Year:** Strategic Plan 2022/2024 (2025–28); Muoniverse launched 2026
- **URL:** https://ethrat.ch ; https://www.muoniverse.ch
- **Summary:** Materials science driven by the ETH Domain (ETHZ, EPFL, Empa, PSI), with "Advanced Materials and Key Technologies" a top-five priority; the ETH Domain requests a CHF 12.2B ceiling for 2025–2028. The flagship NCCR Muoniverse (2026; PSI + University of Zurich) uses the world's most intense muon beams for non-destructive analysis of quantum materials, superconductors, batteries, and power semiconductors (30+ teams). Also runs the ESA-linked ESDI Centre at PSI for extreme-environment materials.

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** SwissChips Initiative
- **Body:** SERI, coordinated by ETH Zurich, EPFL, and CSEM
- **Year:** Launched 2024 (transitional 2024–2026)
- **URL:** https://swisschips.ethz.ch
- **Summary:** A transitional program (CHF 33.8M: CHF 26M SERI + CHF 7.8M CSEM/EPFL/ETHZ) to safeguard sovereignty in microelectronics and IC design, prompted by exclusion from the EU's Digital Europe program. Rather than build fabs, it focuses on Swiss strengths — advanced IC design, SoCs, 6G, biomedical circuits, Edge AI — providing shared design infrastructure to universities, training PhDs, and keeping multinational R&D in Switzerland (also the green-chip GENESIS project on PFAS removal).

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** Digital Switzerland Strategy 2026 (Strategie Digitale Schweiz)
- **Body:** Federal Council, coordinated by the Federal Chancellery (DTI), with DETEC, FDFA, FDJP
- **Year:** Adopted December 2025 (updated annually)
- **URL:** https://digital.swiss/en/
- **Summary:** The binding umbrella framework for federal digital transformation. The 2026 edition sets three focus themes: (1) digital sovereignty and crisis resilience (DDPS-led), (2) reinforcing "International Geneva" as a secure digital host state for UN/WHO/ICRC (FDFA), and (3) rollout of the state-recognized voluntary e-ID ("Swiyu" wallet, FDJP), with >$200M committed to the verification infrastructure.

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** Ordinance on Automated Driving (OAD/VAF) + AVISTRAT-CH airspace strategy & Swiss U-Space Implementation (SUSI)
- **Body:** Federal Roads Office (FEDRO) for vehicles; Federal Office of Civil Aviation (FOCA) for drones/airspace
- **Year:** OAD in force March 1, 2025; AVISTRAT-CH vision to 2035
- **URL:** https://www.astra.admin.ch ; https://www.bazl.admin.ch
- **Summary:** Among the first nations with a detailed nationwide automated-driving law (OAD, March 2025): highway autopilots (with takeover), canton-approved SAE Level-4 driverless shuttles monitored from remote control centers (e.g. Swiss Post "AmiGo," SBB "iamo"), and geofenced automated valet parking — with a mandatory "driving mode memory" data logger. For drones, FOCA's AVISTRAT-CH modernizes airspace by 2035 and SUSI (with Skyguide) builds U-Space traffic management for BVLOS and urban air mobility; Switzerland adopted EU drone rules in 2023. (Built on the legacy of NCCR Robotics, 2010–2022.)
