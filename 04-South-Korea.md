# South Korea (Republic of Korea) — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Country 4 of 5 (Batch 1). OECD bloc.*

South Korea's strategies tie tech leadership to demographic decline, energy independence, and the U.S.-China rivalry. The $471B semiconductor mega-cluster anchors the agenda; nuclear is being scaled to power AI data centers and fabs. The 2024 AI Basic Act (effective 2026) positions Korea as an AI-governance standard-bearer.

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** 11th Basic Plan for Long-Term Electricity Supply and Demand (제11차 전력수급기본계획)
- **Body:** Ministry of Trade, Industry and Energy (MOTIE)
- **Year:** Drafted 2024; confirmed February 2025
- **URL:** MOTIE policy archives (announced via MOTIE; summaries via Shin & Kim legal updates)
- **Summary:** Reverses the prior phase-out, raising nuclear to 35.2% of the energy mix by 2038 (from 31.7% in 2024). Authorizes two new APR-1400 reactors (1.4 GW each) and one domestic SMR (0.7 GW) for 2035–2038. Expressly designed to power AI data centers (4.4 GW demand by 2038) and semiconductor fabs (15.4 GW). Also decommissions 28 coal plants by 2036, targeting a 70.7% carbon-free grid; SMR demonstration intended to bolster nuclear exports (UAE Barakah, Czech Dukovany).

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** AI-Semiconductor Initiative + AI Basic Act (인공지능 기본법)
- **Body:** MSIT / National Artificial Intelligence Committee
- **Year:** April 2024 (initiative); Dec 2024 (Act passed, effective Jan 2026)
- **URL:** https://www.msit.go.kr
- **Summary:** Pledges 9.4 trillion won (~$6.7B) public investment by 2027 for next-gen AI models and low-power AI chips, plus a 1.4T won chipmaker fund; private sector adds 65T won (~$48.9B) for a national AI computing center. Aims for "G3" AI-superpower status and 10%+ of the global system-semiconductor market by 2030, fusing memory-chip dominance (HBM/NPU) with AI software. The AI Basic Act balances promotion with safety: high-impact-AI governance, transparency/labeling of AI outputs, and an AI Safety Institute (deepfake detection).

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** Quantum Science and Technology Strategy + Quantum Technology Promotion Act
- **Body:** MSIT
- **Year:** June 2023 (strategy); Nov 2023 (Act in force)
- **URL:** https://www.msit.go.kr/bbs/view.do?sCode=user&mId=84&mPid=83&bbsSeqNo=65&nttSeqNo=3017410
- **Summary:** Public-private investment of 3 trillion won (~$2.3B) by 2035 to reach 85% of leading nations' quantum capability. Targets a domestic 1,000-qubit superconducting quantum computer (early 2030s), a 100km intercity quantum network, and 10% of the global quantum market. Expands the workforce from ~500 to 2,500 core professionals and fosters 1,200 quantum firms by 2035; the Promotion Act funds "quantum innovation zones" and graduate programs (KAIST, Korea University).

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** Advanced Bio Initiative (첨단바이오 이니셔티브)
- **Body:** Cross-ministerial (MSIT, MOTIE, MOHW, MOEF)
- **Year:** 2024
- **URL:** OECD STIP Compass (national announcement)
- **Summary:** A structural reform to make Korea a global biotech leader by 2035, centered on "digital bio convergence" (AI + big data to accelerate drug discovery). Opens public biodata from 15 research institutions to the private sector from 2025 (via KISTI / National Bio Data Station, with mandated Data Management Plans). MOTIE designated five specialized bio-industrial strategic zones to attract FDI (Merck, Prestige Biopharma) and scale biopharma raw materials, vaccines, and immuno-oncology.

## 5. Materials Science / Critical Materials
- **Plan:** Strategy for Securing Reliable Critical Minerals Supply (핵심광물 확보전략)
- **Body:** MOTIE
- **Year:** 2023
- **URL:** IEA Policy Database (MOTIE strategy)
- **Summary:** Identifies 33 critical minerals, with 10 "strategic" (lithium, nickel, cobalt, manganese, graphite, 5 rare earths) prioritized for chips and batteries. Aims to cut reliance on concentrated import sources (esp. China) from ~80% to 50% by 2030. Expands public stockpiles from 54 to 100 days, launches a minerals early-warning system, and raises critical-mineral recycling from 2% to 20% by 2030; pursues the Minerals Security Partnership and bilateral MOUs to diversify.

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** Semiconductor Mega Cluster Creation Plan + K-Chips Act
- **Body:** MOTIE / MSIT
- **Year:** Jan 2024 (cluster); March 2023 (K-Chips Act revision)
- **URL:** MOTIE press releases
- **Summary:** A 622 trillion won (~$471B) private-sector investment by 2047 to build the world's largest chip mega-cluster in the Pyeongtaek-Yongin corridor (16 new fabs, 7.7M wafers/month by 2030, 10% global system-chip share). Government adds 26T won (~$19B) financing, fast-tracked power/water permits, and K-Chips Act tax credits (up to 25% for SMEs, 15% for large firms). Anchored by Samsung (~500T won, 2nm foundries) and SK Hynix (122T+ won, HBM4/321-layer NAND); requires ~10 GW power and 1.1M tons water/day.

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** Digital Strategy of Korea + K-Network 2030 Strategy
- **Body:** MSIT
- **Year:** Sept 2022 (Digital Strategy); Feb 2023 (K-Network 2030)
- **URL:** https://www.msit.go.kr
- **Summary:** Pan-government roadmap to expand the digital economy, build a "platform government," and shift software to SaaS, with public-sector cloud migration and 100,000 cybersecurity professionals. K-Network 2030 targets world-first commercial 6G by 2028–2030, 30%+ of global 6G standard-essential patents, and Open RAN ("Network Software House," K-OTIC certification). Backbone speeds to double by 2026 / quadruple by 2030; submarine cable capacity from 200 to 260 Tbps with diversified landing stations.

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** Advanced Robotics Industry Vision and Strategy (+ 4th Intelligent Robot Basic Plan) and Mobility Innovation Roadmap
- **Body:** MOTIE (robotics) / MOLIT (mobility)
- **Year:** 2023 (robotics vision); Jan 2024 (4th plan); 2022 (mobility roadmap)
- **URL:** MOTIE & MOLIT announcements
- **Summary:** "K-Robot Economy" — 3 trillion won (~$2.3B) by 2030 to deploy 1 million robots across manufacturing, agriculture, defense, and services; 80% domestic component production, 15,000 professionals, $3.9B robot exports by 2030. Mobility Roadmap targets Level 4 autonomous buses (2025), L4 passenger cars and drone delivery (2027), and V2X across 110,000 km of roads by 2030. Agile regulation: the 2023 Intelligent Robot Act reclassified delivery robots as "pedestrians" for sidewalk operation; Autonomous Vehicle Act enabled pilot zones (5,200+ km).
