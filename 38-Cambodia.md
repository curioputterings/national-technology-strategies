# Cambodia — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Entity 38 (Batch 8). ASEAN.*

Cambodia's tech posture is pragmatic and developmental, anchored in the **Pentagonal Strategy Phase I (2023–2028)** — whose fifth priority is "Technology" for the 4IR — and the **Cambodia Digital Economy and Society Policy Framework 2021–2035** (139 policy measures over 15 years). Foundational digital infrastructure, AI, and semiconductor *assembly/design* are aggressively supported as it pursues Vision 2050 high-income status; mining and biomedicine are being modernized to ESG/UHC standards. Quantum is **explicitly absent**, and autonomous systems are treated as an airspace-safety matter, not an industry. The binding constraint across all domains: severe shortage of specialized human capital and R&D funding (~0.09% of GDP).

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** Memoranda of Understanding on the Peaceful Use of Nuclear Energy + Draft Nuclear Law *(no nuclear power plants; long-horizon option)*
- **Body:** Ministry of Mines and Energy (MME) — Department of Nuclear Science and Technology (DONST)
- **Year:** MoUs 2015/2016/2017; Draft Nuclear Law ongoing
- **URL:** https://mme.gov.kh/
- **Summary:** Cambodia's constitution bars nuclear/chemical/biological weapons, but peaceful nuclear use (medicine, industry, agriculture) is permitted. There are no NPPs and nuclear is absent from the Power Development Plan 2022–2040. Still, the government has kept the door open via bilateral cooperation — MoUs with Russia's Rosatom (2015/2016: joint working group, a Nuclear Energy Information Center for public/youth awareness) and a 2017 agreement between the NCSD and China National Nuclear Corporation (CNNC) on HR development. Domestically, the Office of Atomic Energy Technology (2005) was upgraded to DONST under MME; with IAEA facilitation, Cambodia is drafting its first comprehensive nuclear law to regulate nucleonic gauges and medical radiation. Earlier ~2014 ambitions for an NPP by 2030 stalled on public resistance and feasibility; the current focus is regulatory compliance, radioactive-source/waste handling, and long-term SMR capacity-building within ASEAN frameworks. Funding relies on bilateral aid and IAEA technical-cooperation grants.

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** Draft National Artificial Intelligence Strategy (NAIS) 2025–2030
- **Body:** Ministry of Post and Telecommunications (MPTC), with UNESCO, UN-ESCAP and MISTI
- **Year:** 2025 — opened to public consultation June 2025; remains draft (not yet adopted) as of mid-2026
- **URL:** https://mptc.gov.kh/
- **Summary:** AI is a fast-developing domain driven by the Pentagonal Strategy's 4IR mandate. The NAIS sets a vision to lift productivity, quality of life, and the digital economy via **six strategic priorities and 41 measures**: citizen literacy/skills, data/infrastructure/key tech, AI for digital government, sectoral adoption (agriculture, education, health, manufacturing), ethical/responsible AI, and collaboration/R&D. Central targets: digitalize up to 80% of high-demand public services by 2028 (tied to the Digital Government Policy 2022–2035), and build a sovereign **Khmer Large Language Model** via culturally relevant datasets. The strategy mandates a National AI and Data Science Lab and a Digital Park, and — given critically low R&D funding — calls on the MEF for direct funding and tax incentives for AI startups/MSMEs. On 11 June 2025 the MPTC issued Announcement No. 1497PT opening the draft strategy to public consultation, and in July 2025 completed a joint AI Readiness Assessment with UNESCO across 26 ministries to inform finalization; despite this, the strategy had still not been formally adopted by the Council of Ministers as of mid-2026, with the government continuing to operate under draft frameworks and seeking private-sector partners and data-center investment to execute it. A regulatory sandbox (with an ethics review assistant grounded in Khmer Buddhist values of compassion/non-harm) accompanies draft laws on Personal Data Protection and Cybersecurity.

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** *Explicitly absent* — no national quantum strategy, roadmap, or funding
- **Body:** N/A
- **Year:** N/A
- **URL:** N/A
- **Summary:** Quantum is explicitly absent from Cambodia's policy agenda. Unlike ASEAN neighbors (Singapore's ~$219M National Quantum Strategy; Malaysia's National Quantum Computing Centre), Cambodia has published nothing. Under the Digital Economy and Society Policy Framework, the government prioritizes foundational digital infrastructure (broadband, 4G/5G, basic cloud, digital literacy) over capital- and talent-intensive quantum R&D. Any future entry is expected through ASEAN multilateral tech-transfer rather than sovereign R&D; international bodies (ITU "Quantum for Good") classify Cambodia as a "Developing" nation positioned to eventually *consume* quantum-safe cybersecurity standards rather than innovate them.

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** Health Strategic Plan 2025–2034 (HSP4) + National Agenda for Health Systems Research (NAHSR)
- **Body:** Ministry of Health (MoH); National Institute of Public Health (NIPH)
- **Year:** 2024/2025
- **URL:** https://moh.gov.kh/ ; https://niph.org.kh/
- **Summary:** Biomedical science sits under "Human Capital Development" (Pentagon 1), shifting from disease management toward a resilient, data-driven, preventive system targeting Universal Health Coverage by 2035. HSP4 is the 10-year roadmap aligned with the Cambodian SDGs 2030 — eliminating communicable diseases, tackling the rising NCD burden and climate-health impacts, and centering primary health care. The NIPH's NAHSR provides the governance framework for biomedical research, correcting historical fragmentation and foreign-institution dependence by setting national research priorities under the National Ethics Committee for Health Research (NECHR) and translating research into policy. Funding remains heavily ODA/WHO/NGO-subsidized, though the government is expanding domestic health financing and social protection (NSSF-H).

## 5. Materials Science / Critical Raw Materials
- **Plan:** National Policy on Mineral Resources 2018–2028 + Law on Mineral Resource Management and Exploitation (2001, amended 2018); US–Cambodia Agreement on Reciprocal Trade (critical-minerals provisions)
- **Body:** Ministry of Mines and Energy (MME); Ministry of Commerce / Council for the Development of Cambodia (CDC)
- **Year:** 2018 (Mineral Policy); 2025 (US–Cambodia Reciprocal Trade Agreement)
- **URL:** https://mme.gov.kh/ ; https://www.whitehouse.gov/
- **Summary:** Cambodia has underexplored subsoil wealth (gold, iron, bauxite, rare earths) and has overhauled its framework to attract FDI, formalize artisanal mining, and join critical-mineral supply chains. The 2018–2028 policy balances extraction with sustainability; geological work has confirmed ~350M tons of proven bauxite in the southwest, and in 2024 the MME approved >$1M for nine new exploration initiatives targeting critical raw materials. Regulatory reform shifted from a contract-based system to a standardized concession model (modeled on the West Australian Mining Act); via the Council for the Development of Cambodia (CDC), Qualified Investment Projects get 3–9 year tax holidays, import-duty exemptions, and lifetime land leases. Mining generated ~$100M in non-tax revenue in 2024 (+80% YoY), led by the Okvau Gold Mine; royalties run 3–15%, and exports of raw crushed stone/silica sand are banned to force domestic value-added processing. On 26 October 2025, Cambodia signed the **US–Cambodia Agreement on Reciprocal Trade**, whose investment article commits Cambodia to allow and facilitate US investment to explore, mine, extract, refine, process, transport and export critical minerals and energy resources on national-treatment terms, in exchange for prospective US EXIM Bank and DFC financing — elevating critical minerals from a domestic FDI matter to a formal pillar of supply-chain diplomacy.

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** Cambodia Automotive and Electronic Sectors Development Roadmap + Electronic Design Automation (EDA) Roadmap
- **Body:** Council for the Development of Cambodia (CDC); Ministry of Industry, Science, Technology & Innovation (MISTI)
- **Year:** 2022 (CDC Roadmap); 2025 (MISTI EDA Roadmap)
- **URL:** https://cdc.gov.kh/ ; https://misti.gov.kh/
- **Summary:** Facing rising labor costs and eroding garment-sector tariff preferences, Cambodia targets electronics/semiconductor assembly for industrial upgrading. The 2022 Automotive & Electronics Roadmap is phased — Phase 1 simple sub-assemblies (wire harnesses, motors), Phase 2 higher value-added assembly/design — aiming to push electrical/electronic exports beyond the $730M reached in 2023, driven by "Thailand-plus-one" Japanese/Chinese investors in Special Economic Zones. To climb the value chain, MISTI launched the **EDA Roadmap (Nov 2025)** to position Cambodia as a regional semiconductor *design-services* hub by 2030. The top operational priority is human capital: training 300+ specialized engineers in digital IC design/verification and establishing a national EDA center, supported by CDC tax holidays and import-duty exemptions to attract global firms for outsourcing/R&D.

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** Cambodia Digital Economy and Society Policy Framework 2021–2035; Digital Government Policy 2022–2035; National Strategy on Startup Development 2026–2030
- **Body:** Supreme National Economic Council (SNEC); MPTC; Techo Startup Center (TSC)
- **Year:** 2021 (CDESPF); 2022 (Digital Gov); 2026 (draft Startup Strategy)
- **URL:** https://mptc.gov.kh/ ; https://techostartup.center/
- **Summary:** The most documented and prioritized domain. The CDESPF's 139 policy directions span three phases — building digital foundations, digital adoption, and digital transformation — targeting **95% high-speed broadband population coverage by 2035**. The Digital Government Policy builds smart-government infrastructure, e-payments, and streamlined public services. State-owned Telecom Cambodia operates alongside large private players (Smart Axiata, Viettel/Metfone, Cellcard); mobile penetration exceeds 116 subscriptions per 100 people (2024), and 5G/IoT/smart-city rollouts are underway via MoUs with Huawei and Viettel. The Techo Startup Center (under MEF) implements the National Strategy on Startup Development 2026–2030, accelerating MSME digitalization (ADB-backed Digital Adoption Initiative) — aiming to bring hundreds of formal MSMEs into the digital economy by 2028, starting with tourism, and fixing funding-access and post-accelerator gaps.

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** Directives and Guidelines on the Operation of Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs) *(robotics R&D nascent/absent)*
- **Body:** State Secretariat of Civil Aviation (SSCA)
- **Year:** Continuously updated (stricter bans post-2021)
- **URL:** https://ssca.gov.kh/
- **Summary:** Cambodia's autonomous-systems policy is about airspace safety/security, not industrial promotion. Drones are "sensitive items" requiring customs declaration; any drone >250 g must register with the SSCA (passport, specs, $50–100 fee). Flight rules follow international norms: max 120 m (400 ft) altitude, mandatory VLOS, daylight-only, and no flying within 8 km of airports. Absolute no-fly zones protect national security and heritage — Angkor Archaeological Park (Apsara Authority), the Royal Palace, military installations, and borders — with confiscation, fines, and possible arrest for violations. Commercial use (aerial photography, real estate, agricultural monitoring) is permitted with SSCA and Ministry of Interior permits, but there is no state funding for domestic drone manufacturing and no dedicated robotics roadmap or R&D ecosystem.
