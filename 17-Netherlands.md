# Netherlands — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Country 17 (Batch 4). OECD/EU bloc.*

The Netherlands pursues strategic autonomy within the EU framework, with the **National Growth Fund (Nationaal Groeifonds)** as the primary scale-up engine and the **National Technology Strategy (NTS) 2024** focusing resources on ten key enabling technologies. Standout: home of ASML (EUV lithography) — Project Beethoven is a €2.5B ecosystem play to retain it. In late 2025 the Schoof cabinet sharpened this into a "focus" doctrine — the October 2025 **Nieuw industriebeleid met focus** ("Wie niet kiest, verliest") phases out the broad top-sector approach and concentrates on six strategic markets (semiconductors/ChipNL, biotech, defence/aerospace, AI, machine-building, chemistry), reflecting the EZK split into **Economic Affairs (EZ)** and **Climate & Green Growth (KGG)**. Nuclear has reversed from phase-out to new-build.

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** Amendment to the Nuclear Energy Act (Borssele life-extension) + Nuclear Energy Organisation Netherlands (NEO NL)
- **Body:** Ministry of Climate and Green Growth (KGG)
- **Year:** 2024 (amendment); NEO NL established Feb 2025; progress letter Oct 2025
- **URL:** https://www.rijksoverheid.nl/actueel/nieuws/2025/10/17/kabinet-wil-borssele-langer-open-houden
- **Summary:** A dramatic reversal from planned phase-out, positioning nuclear as complement to wind/solar for carbon-neutral electricity by 2040. Extends the 485 MWe Borssele plant from 2033 to 2054 and creates state-owned NEO NL (€45M initial capital) to build two new Gen III+ reactors (1,000–1,650 MWe each, ~2035, 9–13% of electricity). The 17 October 2025 progress letter (*Voortgangsbrief nieuwbouw kernenergie*) confirms NEO NL transitions in early 2026 into an independent policy participation (*beleidsdeelneming*) with KGG as sole shareholder, acting as commissioning authority, contract negotiator and eventual operator for the two Borssele/Sloegebied units, and reaffirms keeping the existing plant open longer. €65M for nuclear knowledge infrastructure and €20–65M for SMRs (e.g. ULC-Energy/Rolls-Royce MoU); evaluating Westinghouse and EDF.

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** AiNed National Growth Fund Programme + Actieagenda AI en Data / National AI Factory (Rijksbegroting 2026)
- **Body:** Ministry of Economic Affairs (EZ) with BZK, JenV, Netherlands AI Coalition (NL AIC)
- **Year:** 2019 (Action Plan); 2021 (AiNed); 2025 (2026 budget)
- **URL:** https://www.rijksoverheid.nl/documenten/begrotingen/2025/09/16/xiii-economische-zaken-rijksbegroting-2026
- **Summary:** Three pillars: economic opportunity, enabling conditions (infrastructure/talent), and ethical/human-centric foundations (aligned with EU trustworthy-AI). AiNed is backed by €276M from the National Growth Fund (toward a €2B public-private goal over 7 years), accelerating SME adoption and research. The 2026 budget (Prinsjesdag, 16 Sept 2025) consolidates AiNed, ROBUST and NOLAI into a broader **Actieagenda AI en Data** and earmarks a **National AI Factory** giving Dutch researchers, start-ups and SMEs priority access to an AI supercomputer, plus regulatory sandboxes for the phased EU AI Act rollout in 2026–2027. Executed via the 400+-member NL AIC; notable for an Algorithm and AI Register for transparency. AI is one of the ten NTS 2024 priority technologies and a focus market under the 2025 industrial-policy reset.

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** Quantum Delta NL Programme (National Growth Fund)
- **Body:** EZK (National Growth Fund) + Quantum Delta NL consortium
- **Year:** 2021–2027 (operational)
- **URL:** https://www.tue.nl/... (Quantum Delta NL €615M award)
- **Summary:** €615M to make the Netherlands the "Silicon Valley of the Quantum World," building on QuTech's strong science base. Five regional hubs (Delft, Eindhoven, Leiden, Twente, Amsterdam) across quantum computers, networks, and sensors. Targets training 2,000 researchers, scaling 100 startups, and €5–7B cumulative impact / 30,000 jobs by the 2040s. Synergistic with integrated photonics (PhotonDelta); a noted gap is post-quantum cryptography implementation.

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** Kabinetsvisie op biotechnologie 2025–2040 + Biotech Booster (National Growth Fund) + Health~Holland International Strategy 2024–2027
- **Body:** Ministry of Economic Affairs (EZ) / National Growth Fund / Top Sector Life Sciences & Health (Health~Holland)
- **Year:** 2025 (Cabinet Vision); 2022 (Biotech Booster); 2024 (LSH Strategy)
- **URL:** https://zoek.officielebekendmakingen.nl/kst-27428-408.html
- **Summary:** The 11 April 2025 **Kabinetsvisie op biotechnologie 2025–2040** sets a long-horizon agenda to make the Netherlands a global biotech leader by 2040 while reducing strategic dependencies, backed by National Growth Fund instruments; biotech is also one of the six focus markets in the October 2025 industrial-policy reset. It builds on the Biotech Booster, which addresses the Dutch "valley of death" — strong research, weak commercialization — allocating €246M (to 2032) to turn discoveries into investable propositions (mentorship, capital, KTO business developers) across five thematic clusters; 70+ projects admitted. Complemented by €325M Oncode-PACT (preclinical cancer-drug acceleration) and PharmaNL. Health~Holland maximizes international trade/investment impact of the LSH ecosystem.

## 5. Materials Science
- **Plan:** National Exploration Programme for Critical Raw Materials (2025) + National Technology Strategy 2024 + National Raw Materials Strategy 2022
- **Body:** Ministry of Economic Affairs (EZ)
- **Year:** 2022 (Raw Materials); 2024 (NTS); 2025 (Exploration Programme)
- **URL:** https://zoek.officielebekendmakingen.nl/kst-32852-374.html
- **Summary:** The NTS names "Energy Materials" and green chemistry among ten priority key enabling technologies; aligns with €5.7B public-private investment under the Knowledge and Innovation Covenant (KIC) for metamaterials, optical materials, and circular solutions. The 15 July 2025 **Nationaal Exploratie Programma kritieke grondstoffen** maps critical-raw-material potential in the Dutch subsurface to help meet EU Critical Raw Materials Act targets and secure high-tech and defence supply chains, building on the 2024 Netherlands Materials Observatory (NMO). The Raw Materials Strategy mitigates critical-material dependencies via circularity, diversification, and sustainable European mining; HCSS defense audits flag vulnerabilities in 21 strategic military materials, explicitly linking materials to national security.

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** Project Beethoven, succeeded by Nieuw industriebeleid met focus ("Wie niet kiest, verliest") with semiconductors as the ChipNL pillar
- **Body:** EZ, Ministry of Education (OCW), regional authorities
- **Year:** March 2024 (Beethoven); Oct 2025 (focus industrial policy)
- **URL:** https://www.rijksoverheid.nl/actueel/nieuws/2025/10/17/wie-niet-kiest-verliest-nieuw-industriebeleid-met-focus
- **Summary:** A €2.51B initiative to retain ASML (world leader in EUV lithography) and grow the Brainport Eindhoven hub. Unlike US/China direct subsidies, Beethoven funds regional infrastructure, housing, and education (EU state-aid-compliant): €1.28B National Growth Fund + €450M central education + €780M regional, targeting 33,000 additional semiconductor technicians by 2030, with TU/e deeply integrated. On 17 October 2025 the cabinet generalised this targeted method into **Nieuw industriebeleid met focus**, phasing out broad top-sector policy from 1 January 2026 and concentrating narrowed innovation funding on six strategic markets — with semiconductors pursued via a dedicated **ChipNL** track — to safeguard Dutch geopolitical autonomy. TU/e remains central (cleanrooms, PhD programs in plasma physics/optics/mechatronics).

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** Dutch Digitalisation Strategy 2.0 (Nederlandse Digitaliseringsstrategie)
- **Body:** Ministry of the Interior and Kingdom Relations (BZK) / State Secretary for Digitalisation
- **Year:** 2018 original; 2.0 (2019); ongoing updates 2024/25
- **URL:** https://www.nederlanddigitaal.nl/english/dutch-digitalisation-strategy-2.0
- **Summary:** Foundational digital policy operating on "agree centrally, implement federatively" ("One Digital Government"). Prioritizes a secure digital foundation (privacy, cybersecurity, skills) and an open data economy, targeting digital inclusion, user-centric "1 Government" services, and high-speed connectivity aligned with the EU Digital Decade 2030. On the economic side, EZ's *Strategie Digitale Economie* progress report (10 March 2025) tracks gigabit roll-out, AI and cloud, and a target of 95% SME digitalisation by 2030. Umbrella over the National Cybersecurity Strategy; uses the country as a "living lab" (digital twins, data spaces) for strategic data autonomy.

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** Unmanned Aviation Action Plan 2023–2025 (Actieplan Onbemande Luchtvaart) + Smart Mobility programmes
- **Body:** Ministry of Infrastructure and Water Management (IenW)
- **Year:** 2023 (Action Plan published April 2023)
- **URL:** https://www.rijksoverheid.nl/documenten/rapporten/2023/04/20/rapport-actieplan-onbemande-luchtvaart-2023-2025
- **Summary:** Safely integrates the growing drone sector (~€500M market by 2035) via flexible regulation and a U-Space Unmanned Traffic Management system. Smart Mobility initiatives cover autonomous port logistics (Rotterdam), self-driving transit, and Mobility-as-a-Service under public oversight. As the 2023–2025 civil action plan reaches the end of its term, the emphasis is shifting toward defence-driven uncrewed systems: in October 2025 the Ministry of Defence (with EZ) launched an industry call to scale domestic drone production and rapid prototyping, drawing on lessons from Ukraine. Applied research via TNO and NLR; maritime projects (e.g. Drobotize) deploy unmanned surface vessels with "red team" cyber testing. Amsterdam's program insists the public sector retains control of mobility data.
