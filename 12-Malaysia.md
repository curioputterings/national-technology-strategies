# Malaysia — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Country 12 (Batch 3). ASEAN bloc.*

Malaysia coordinates STI via the 10-10 MySTIE Framework and New Industrial Master Plan (NIMP) 2030, positioning itself as a neutral "middle-power broker" capturing "Plus One" FDI amid US-China rivalry while building indigenous sovereignty. Notable reversal: nuclear power is back on the agenda after years of suspension.

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** 13th Malaysia Plan (RMK13) 2026–2030, aligned with the National Energy Transition Roadmap (NETR)
- **Body:** Ministry of Economy, Ministry of Energy Transition and Water Transformation (PETRA), MyPOWER Corporation (NEPIO)
- **Year:** NETR July 2023; RMK13 tabled 2025 (implementation 2026–2030)
- **URL:** https://www.petra.gov.my/uploads/content-downloads/file_20251113113853.pdf
- **Summary:** Reverses the 2018 anti-nuclear halt, reincorporating nuclear as a clean baseload to support net-zero by 2050. MyPOWER Corporation is the Nuclear Energy Programme Implementing Organization (NEPIO), following the IAEA Milestones Approach; the Atomic Energy Licensing Act was amended (2025). Driven by data-center demand (~11 GW) and coal phase-out (7 GW by 2033, 5 GW more by 2044). Evaluating conventional 2,000MW plants, SMRs (100–300MW), and floating units; targeted grid integration ~2031.

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** AI Technology Action Plan 2026–2030 (successor to the AI Roadmap 2021–2025) + Sovereign AI Cloud; National AI Governance & Ethics Guidelines (AIGE)
- **Body:** National AI Office (NAIO) under the Ministry of Digital / MyDIGITAL Corporation; MOSTI
- **Year:** AI-Rmap 2021–2025; NAIO Aug 2024; Action Plan 2030 and Sovereign AI Cloud endorsed by MED4IRN council 2 Oct 2025 (phased rollout 2026)
- **URL:** https://naio.gov.my/
- **Summary:** As the 2021–2025 Roadmap expires, NAIO is steering a successor AI Technology Action Plan 2026–2030 organised around three execution phases (Ignite, Scale, Transform) and seven core deliverables, including an AI adoption regulatory framework and AI Code of Ethics, to push Malaysia from AI consumer to "Made by Malaysia" AI producer. On 2 October 2025 the National Digital Economy and 4IR Council (MED4IRN) endorsed the build-out of a Sovereign AI Cloud to secure national data and government services. Targets include upskilling 700,000+ workers by 2030 and a 0.8–1.2% AI-driven GDP uplift, building on the earlier AIGE (seven UNESCO-anchored principles) and the Cybersecurity Act 2024.

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** National Quantum Policy 2026–2035
- **Body:** MOSTI / MIMOS Berhad
- **Year:** Formally endorsed by the MED4IRN council / Prime Minister on 2 October 2025 (operational 2026–2035); MIMOS Quantum Intelligence Centre launched 25 Feb 2025
- **URL:** https://www.mimos.my/shaping-the-future-of-quantum-intelligence/
- **Summary:** Endorsed by the National Digital Economy and 4IR Council (MED4IRN) on 2 October 2025, the policy sets a 10-year roadmap and stands up a National Quantum Task Force to prepare Malaysia for "Q-Day." Aims to make Malaysia ASEAN's leading quantum hub by 2035 via the concept of "Quantum Intelligence" (quantum + AI). Centered on the MIMOS Quantum Intelligence Centre (Feb 2025) and the Malaysian Quantum Valley. Via partnership with South Korea's SDT Inc., targets a 20-qubit superconducting computer by 2025 scaling to 1,000+ qubits by 2030, and prioritizes Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC) against "Q-Day" (with telcos like Maxis securing the optical layer).

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** National Biotechnology Policy 2.0 (NBP 2.0 / DBN 2.0) + National Biotechnology Ethics Guidelines (2025)
- **Body:** MOSTI / Malaysian Bioeconomy Development Corporation
- **Year:** NBP 2.0 2022 (active to 2030); Ethics Guidelines launched 9 Sep 2025
- **URL:** https://www.bioeconomycorporation.my/corporate/nbp/
- **Summary:** Builds on the 2005 policy with three thrusts: agriculture/food security, healthcare/wellbeing (incl. cell & gene therapy), and industrial circular economy (palm-oil waste → bioplastics). Five 2030 targets: 5% GDP from biotech, 80% of biotech graduates micro-accredited, and three bio-innovation "unicorns." On 9 September 2025 MOSTI launched the National Biotechnology Ethics Guidelines, setting out 15 core principles governing frontier areas such as gene editing and cultured meat; in early 2026 MOSTI and Bioeconomy Corp announced an explicit AI-into-bioeconomy pivot (targeting ~RM2.2B bio-based revenue for 2026). Lead agency rebranded from "Biotechnology" to "Bioeconomy Development" Corporation, signaling a broader wealth-generation mandate.

## 5. Materials Science
- **Plan:** National Advanced Materials Technology Roadmap 2021–2030; rare-earth (REE) raw-export ban / critical-minerals policy (2025)
- **Body:** MOSTI / Malaysian Industry-Government Group for High Technology (MIGHT); MITI
- **Year:** Roadmap 2021 (active to 2030); REE raw-export ban reaffirmed Dec 2025; US–Malaysia critical-minerals MoU signed 26 Oct 2025
- **URL:** https://www.mosti.gov.my/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/Pelan-Hala-Tuju-Teknologi-Bahan-Termaju-Negara-2021-2030-versi-BI.pdf
- **Summary:** Focuses on four "game-changing" materials: rare earth elements (REE), multicrystalline nanocellulose, nitinol, and graphene. RM100M via the National Technology and Innovation Sandbox (NTIS) to speed commercialization. Targets monetizing RM800B in non-radioactive REE deposits (~16.1M tonnes), ~RM13.59B downstream REE revenue by 2030 (super-magnets, EV motors), with green extraction and circular-economy (palm biomass) principles. In late 2025 MITI sharpened the critical-minerals stance: it reaffirmed a ban on exporting raw/unprocessed REE (only value-added oxides, alloys and magnet components may be exported) and signed a critical-minerals supply-chain MoU with the United States (26 Oct 2025) that preserves Malaysia's domestic value-addition mandate. Coordinated by the National Advanced Materials Consortium (NAMC).

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** National Semiconductor Strategy (NSS) (within NIMP 2030)
- **Body:** Ministry of Investment, Trade and Industry (MITI)
- **Year:** 2024
- **URL:** https://www.miti.gov.my/miti/resources/NSS_141024.pdf
- **Summary:** A pivot from back-end assembly (Malaysia holds ~13% of global OSAT) to front-end IC design, advanced packaging, and wafer fab, backed by RM25B (~US$5.3B). Three phases (build foundations → move to frontier → innovate at frontier) aiming for RM500B total investment, 60,000 upskilled engineers, 10 local champions (RM5B revenue), and 100 companies (RM1B). Leverages neutral "middle-power" positioning for "Plus One" diversification; IC Design Park in Puchong.

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** Malaysia Digital Economy Blueprint (MyDIGITAL)
- **Body:** Economic Planning Unit (PMO) / Ministry of Economy / MyDIGITAL Corporation
- **Year:** 2021 (executing to 2030)
- **URL:** https://ekonomi.gov.my/sites/default/files/2021-02/malaysia-digital-economy-blueprint.pdf
- **Summary:** A whole-of-nation framework with six thrusts to make Malaysia a high-income digital leader by 2030. By 2025 targets: digital economy at 22.6% of GDP, 500,000 new digital jobs, 100% household internet, 100% civil-servant digital literacy, and 875,000 MSMEs in e-commerce. Includes public-sector digitalization (Chief Digital Officers, sovereign cloud) and connectivity via the parallel JENDELA network plan (broadband, 5G); cybersecurity is a core backbone.

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** National Robotics Roadmap (NRR) 2021–2030 + Malaysia Drone Technology Action Plan 2022–2030 (MDTAP30) + Low-Altitude Economy (LAE) Roadmap (2026)
- **Body:** MOSTI / MRANTI / Malaysia Robotics and Automation Society (MyRAS); Ministry of Transport / Civil Aviation Authority of Malaysia (CAAM)
- **Year:** NRR 2021; MDTAP30 2022; LAE Roadmap launched 24 Jun 2026
- **URL:** https://www.malaysia.gov.my/en/my-initiative/emerging-technology/emerging-technology/national-robotics-roadmap-nrr-2021-2030 ; https://www.caam.gov.my/
- **Summary:** NRR targets raising robot density from 55 (2019) to 195 per 10,000 workers by 2030 (a ~RM103.1B domestic market), reducing reliance on low-skilled foreign labor via "embodied AI" smart factories. MDTAP30 advances drones through 8 missions: a nationwide Unmanned Traffic Management (UTM) system, urban air mobility, and drone solutions for agriculture and rural medical logistics. On 24 June 2026 the Ministry of Transport launched the Low-Altitude Economy (LAE) Roadmap, creating the regulatory and developmental foundation for commercial cargo drones and air taxis by 2030; CAAM aims to begin limited commercial freight-drone services as early as Q1 2027, paired with a new risk-based Civil Aviation Regulation for Unmanned Aircraft Operations (replacing the 2016 framework) and a digital UAS Traffic Management System (UAS-TMS). Goal: shift from tech consumer to ASEAN value creator/exporter.
