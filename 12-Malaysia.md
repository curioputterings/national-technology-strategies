# Malaysia — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Country 12 (Batch 3). ASEAN bloc.*

Malaysia coordinates STI via the 10-10 MySTIE Framework and New Industrial Master Plan (NIMP) 2030, positioning itself as a neutral "middle-power broker" capturing "Plus One" FDI amid US-China rivalry while building indigenous sovereignty. Notable reversal: nuclear power is back on the agenda after years of suspension.

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** 13th Malaysia Plan (RMK13) 2026–2030, aligned with the National Energy Transition Roadmap (NETR)
- **Body:** Ministry of Economy, Ministry of Energy Transition and Water Transformation (PETRA), MyPOWER Corporation (NEPIO)
- **Year:** NETR July 2023; RMK13 tabled 2025 (implementation 2026–2030)
- **URL:** https://www.petra.gov.my/uploads/content-downloads/file_20251113113853.pdf
- **Summary:** Reverses the 2018 anti-nuclear halt, reincorporating nuclear as a clean baseload to support net-zero by 2050. MyPOWER Corporation is the Nuclear Energy Programme Implementing Organization (NEPIO), following the IAEA Milestones Approach; the Atomic Energy Licensing Act was amended (2025). Driven by data-center demand (~11 GW) and coal phase-out (7 GW by 2033, 5 GW more by 2044). Evaluating conventional 2,000MW plants, SMRs (100–300MW), and floating units; targeted grid integration ~2031.

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** Malaysia National AI Roadmap 2021–2025 (AI-Rmap) + National AI Governance & Ethics Guidelines (AIGE)
- **Body:** MOSTI; National AI Office (NAIO) under the Ministry of Digital
- **Year:** AI-Rmap 2021; AIGE & NAIO 2024
- **URL:** https://dig.watch/resource/malaysia-national-artificial-intelligence-roadmap-2021-2025
- **Summary:** A Quadruple-Helix blueprint with six thrusts (governance, R&D, infrastructure, talent, acculturation, ecosystem) to make Malaysia an AI producer, targeting 900 AI startups by 2026. NAIO (Aug 2024) is the central coordinating body; AIGE (built on seven principles, anchored to UNESCO) makes Malaysia an early ASEAN mover on AI ethics. Developing a sovereign AI cloud alongside the Cybersecurity Act 2024; Microsoft pledged to train 300,000 Malaysians.

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** National Quantum Policy 2026–2035
- **Body:** MOSTI / MIMOS Berhad
- **Year:** Framework approved late 2024/early 2025 (operational 2026–2035)
- **URL:** https://www.mimos.my/shaping-the-future-of-quantum-intelligence/
- **Summary:** Aims to make Malaysia ASEAN's leading quantum hub by 2035 via the concept of "Quantum Intelligence" (quantum + AI). Centered on the MIMOS Quantum Intelligence Centre (Feb 2025) and the Malaysian Quantum Valley. Via partnership with South Korea's SDT Inc., targets a 20-qubit superconducting computer by 2025 scaling to 1,000+ qubits by 2030, and prioritizes Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC) against "Q-Day" (with telcos like Maxis securing the optical layer).

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** National Biotechnology Policy 2.0 (NBP 2.0 / DBN 2.0)
- **Body:** MOSTI / Malaysian Bioeconomy Development Corporation
- **Year:** 2022 (active to 2030)
- **URL:** https://www.bioeconomycorporation.my/corporate/nbp/
- **Summary:** Builds on the 2005 policy with three thrusts: agriculture/food security, healthcare/wellbeing (incl. cell & gene therapy), and industrial circular economy (palm-oil waste → bioplastics). Five 2030 targets: 5% GDP from biotech, 80% of biotech graduates micro-accredited, and three bio-innovation "unicorns." Lead agency rebranded from "Biotechnology" to "Bioeconomy Development" Corporation, signaling a broader wealth-generation mandate.

## 5. Materials Science
- **Plan:** National Advanced Materials Technology Roadmap 2021–2030
- **Body:** MOSTI / Malaysian Industry-Government Group for High Technology (MIGHT)
- **Year:** 2021 (active to 2030)
- **URL:** https://www.mosti.gov.my/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/Pelan-Hala-Tuju-Teknologi-Bahan-Termaju-Negara-2021-2030-versi-BI.pdf
- **Summary:** Focuses on four "game-changing" materials: rare earth elements (REE), multicrystalline nanocellulose, nitinol, and graphene. RM100M via the National Technology and Innovation Sandbox (NTIS) to speed commercialization. Targets monetizing RM800B in non-radioactive REE deposits (~16.1M tonnes), ~RM13.59B downstream REE revenue by 2030 (super-magnets, EV motors), with green extraction and circular-economy (palm biomass) principles. Coordinated by the National Advanced Materials Consortium (NAMC).

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** National Semiconductor Strategy (NSS) (within NIMP 2030)
- **Body:** Ministry of Investment, Trade and Industry (MITI)
- **Year:** 2024
- **URL:** https://www.miti.gov.my/miti/resources/NSS_141024.pdf
- **Summary:** A pivot from back-end assembly (Malaysia holds ~13% of global OSAT) to front-end IC design, advanced packaging, and wafer fab, backed by RM25B (~US$5.3B). Three phases (build foundations → move to frontier → innovate at frontier) aiming for RM500B total investment, 60,000 upskilled engineers, 10 local champions (RM5B revenue), and 100 companies (RM1B). Leverages neutral "middle-power" positioning for "Plus One" diversification; IC Design Park in Puchong.

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** Malaysia Digital Economy Blueprint (MyDIGITAL)
- **Body:** Economic Planning Unit (PMO) / Ministry of Economy / MyDIGITAL Corporation
- **Year:** 2021 (executing to 2030)
- **URL:** https://ekonomi.gov.my/sites/default/files/2021-02/malaysia-digital-economy-blueprint.pdf
- **Summary:** A whole-of-nation framework with six thrusts to make Malaysia a high-income digital leader by 2030. By 2025 targets: digital economy at 22.6% of GDP, 500,000 new digital jobs, 100% household internet, 100% civil-servant digital literacy, and 875,000 MSMEs in e-commerce. Includes public-sector digitalization (Chief Digital Officers, sovereign cloud) and connectivity via the parallel JENDELA network plan (broadband, 5G); cybersecurity is a core backbone.

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** National Robotics Roadmap (NRR) 2021–2030 + Malaysia Drone Technology Action Plan 2022–2030 (MDTAP30)
- **Body:** MOSTI / MRANTI / Malaysia Robotics and Automation Society (MyRAS)
- **Year:** NRR 2021; MDTAP30 2022
- **URL:** https://www.malaysia.gov.my/en/my-initiative/emerging-technology/emerging-technology/national-robotics-roadmap-nrr-2021-2030 ; https://mranti.my/innovators/connect/my-drone-tech
- **Summary:** NRR targets raising robot density from 55 (2019) to 195 per 10,000 workers by 2030 (a ~RM103.1B domestic market), reducing reliance on low-skilled foreign labor via "embodied AI" smart factories. MDTAP30 advances drones through 8 missions: a nationwide Unmanned Traffic Management (UTM) system, urban air mobility, and drone solutions for agriculture and rural medical logistics (with CAAM; Remote Pilot Certificate RCoC-B). Goal: shift from tech consumer to ASEAN value creator/exporter.
