# Sweden — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Country 20 (Batch 4). OECD/EU bloc.*

Sweden relies on collaborative "triple-helix" (academia–industry–government) innovation, with Vinnova and the Wallenberg Foundation as major engines. Two standout shifts: a historic pro-nuclear pivot (from "100% renewable" to "100% fossil-free," targeting ~10 new reactors by 2045) and aggressive critical-minerals positioning (EU's leading mining nation; LKAB's Kiruna rare-earth find).

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** Roadmap for New Nuclear Power in Sweden (Färdplan för ny kärnkraft)
- **Body:** Government of Sweden (Ministry of Climate and Enterprise) + National Nuclear New-build Coordinator
- **Year:** 2023 (launch); 2024–25 updates
- **URL:** https://www.regeringen.se/pressmeddelanden/2023/11/regeringen-lanserar-en-fardplan-for-ny-karnkraft-i-sverige/
- **Summary:** A historic shift — the national target changed from "100% renewable" to "100% fossil-free" (Tidö Agreement), removing the cap on reactor numbers and existing-site restriction. Targets 2,500 MW (~2 large reactors) by 2035 and up to 10,000 MW (~10 reactors) by 2045, anticipating electricity demand doubling to ~300 TWh. State risk-sharing via government loans and two-way Contracts for Difference (CfDs) for up to 5,000 MW; a coordinator is compressing the ~10-year permitting timeline.

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** The AI Commission's Roadmap for Sweden (SOU 2025:12), informing the National AI Strategy
- **Body:** Swedish AI Commission (appointed by the Government / Ministry of Finance)
- **Year:** November 2024
- **URL:** https://www.sou.gov.se/globalassets/the-ai-commissions-roadmap-for-sweden.pdf
- **Summary:** 75 proposals to push Sweden into the global AI top 10 (it had slipped to ~25th). Recommends SEK 12.5B (~$1.14B) over five years for research, an EU-co-funded "AI Factory" for SMEs (using the Arrhenius supercomputer), and an "AI-verkstad" for public-sector collaboration. Notably calls for reforming Sweden's overly strict GDPR interpretation to enable secure data sharing, plus a Data Steward role at Statistics Sweden. Integrated into the Digitalisation Strategy 2025–2030.

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** Wallenberg Centre for Quantum Technology (WACQT) + forthcoming National Quantum Strategy 2025–2030
- **Body:** Knut and Alice Wallenberg Foundation (WACQT); Swedish Research Council (Vetenskapsrådet) for the national strategy
- **Year:** 2018–2029 (WACQT); 2024–25 (strategy drafting)
- **URL:** https://kaw.wallenberg.org/en/research/initiative-driving-swedens-quantum-revolution
- **Summary:** WACQT is a SEK 1.4B philanthropic program (centered at Chalmers) aiming for a domestic 100-qubit superconducting computer by 2029 (a 25-qubit processor demonstrated by 2024, open as an industry testbed for Ericsson, AstraZeneca, Volvo). Four pillars: computing (Chalmers), communication (KTH), sensors (Lund), simulation. The government tasked Vetenskapsrådet to draft a formal National Quantum Strategy with structural funding (SEK 50M in 2027, 100M in 2028), integrating with the EU Quantum Flagship/EuroQCI.

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** National Strategy for Life Science (Nationell strategi för life science), updated
- **Body:** Government of Sweden (cross-ministerial Life Science Office)
- **Year:** November 2024 (update of 2019 strategy)
- **URL:** https://www.regeringen.se/regeringens-politik/nationell-strategi-for-life-science/
- **Summary:** Positions Sweden as a leading life-sciences nation across eight priority areas, with three core outcomes: excellence, competitiveness, patient benefit. Prioritizes clinical trials (a national partnership to reverse declining European trials), precision medicine, and Advanced Therapy Medicinal Products (cell/gene therapies). Backed by SEK 1.75B into research infrastructure (SciLifeLab, biobanks, registries, HALRIC); coordinated by the Life Science Office linking regions (Skåne, Västra Götaland) with AstraZeneca, Getinge. (~40,000 employees, 1,000+ companies.)

## 5. Materials Science / Critical Minerals
- **Plan:** Sweden's Minerals Strategy + National Mapping System for Critical Raw Materials (KRRR)
- **Body:** Ministry of Enterprise; SGU (Geological Survey), Naturvårdsverket, IVL
- **Year:** 2013 (Minerals Strategy); 2024–25 (KRRR / EU CRM Act adaptation)
- **URL:** https://rmis.jrc.ec.europa.eu/uploads/legislation/Sweden_MineralsStrategy_ENGLISH.pdf
- **Summary:** Sweden produces 93% of EU iron ore and holds major REE, vanadium, and graphite deposits. The 2013 strategy promotes sustainable extraction and low mineral taxation; the new KRRR project maps secondary flows/circularity of materials locked in products and waste. LKAB's 2023 Kiruna find — Europe's largest known REE deposit (neodymium, praseodymium for magnets) — triggered permitting modernization. Vinnova coordinates the EU "RAMP" raw-materials partnership (~$470M, 32 countries).

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** Sweden's National Semiconductor Strategy 2035 (Sveriges nationella halvledarstrategi 2035)
- **Body:** Semiconductor Sweden (consortium led by Svensk Elektronik, with Vinnova, RISE, FMV)
- **Year:** 2024 (launch cited April 2026 in source)
- **URL:** https://semiconductorsweden.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/Swedens-national-semiconductor-strategy-2035_webb.pdf
- **Summary:** A niche "indispensability through specialization" strategy (not advanced-node mass fabs), proposing ~SEK 2B/yr public-private investment across five domains: chip design (RF/analog/mixed-signal), advanced packaging, power electronics (SiC/GaN), test/validation for safety-critical and defense electronics, and manufacturing equipment. Recommends a "Program for Advanced Electronics" (PAE) — with Ericsson, Saab, Mycronic — as the national gateway for EU pilot lines and IPCEI funding. (8,000+ Swedish firms depend on imported microelectronics.)

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** Sweden's Digitalisation Strategy 2025–2030
- **Body:** Government of Sweden (Ministry of Finance)
- **Year:** 2025
- **URL:** https://www.regeringen.se/rapporter/2025/05/sveriges-digitaliseringsstrategi-20252030/
- **Summary:** Five pillars: digital skills, business digitalization, public administration, public welfare, and connectivity (98% of households/businesses to 1 Gbps; rapid 5G). Integrates AI across sectors and prioritizes cybersecurity, a sovereign state e-identification (bypassing private bank IDs), and a new Swedish Cloud Policy (molnpolicy) to reduce reliance on non-European hyperscalers — reflecting NATO membership and total-defense resilience. Carries forward "A Completely Connected Sweden."

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** Drive Sweden Strategic Innovation Program (CCAM — Connected, Cooperative and Automated Mobility)
- **Body:** Drive Sweden (Lindholmen Science Park), funded by Vinnova, Swedish Energy Agency, Formas
- **Year:** Established 2015 (active 2024–25 strategic calls)
- **URL:** https://www.drivesweden.net/en
- **Summary:** A system-level (not vehicle-only) approach with 200+ partners spanning robotaxis, micromobility, automated waste collection, and drone logistics. Built on the "Drive Sweden Innovation Cloud" for B2B data sharing (spin-offs like the "Connected Traffic Tower" with Ericsson, Scania, Veoneer). Policy Labs work with the Swedish Transport Agency to adapt legislation toward fully automated vehicle fleets by 2027; pilots include the BASE AV-shuttle project (Brunnshög) and eVTOL/drone testbeds (Skellefteå).
