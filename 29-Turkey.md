# Turkey (Türkiye) — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Entity 29 (Batch 6).*

Turkey's tech policy is unified under the **Milli Teknoloji Hamlesi** (National Technology Initiative), codified via the 12th Development Plan (2024–2028) and the 2030 Industry and Technology Strategy. The 2024 **$30B HIT-30** High-Technology Investment Program is the financial centerpiece (semiconductors, EVs, green energy). Turkey leverages defense-tech success (Baykar drones), a giant Akkuyu nuclear build, and the huge Beylikova rare-earth find to position as an alternative supply-chain node.

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** Türkiye National Energy Plan 2020–2035
- **Body:** Ministry of Energy and Natural Resources
- **Year:** December 2022 (aligned to 2053 net-zero)
- **URL:** https://enerji.gov.tr/Media/Dizin/EIGM/tr/Raporlar/TUEP/T%C3%BCrkiye_National_Energy_Plan.pdf
- **Summary:** Targets 4.8 GW nuclear by 2030 and 7.2 GW by 2035. The flagship $20B Akkuyu plant (Rosatom, Build-Own-Operate; 4× VVER-1200 = 4.8 GW; Unit 1 commissioning 2025/26) will supply ~10% of electricity. Additional large plants planned at Sinop and Thrace; 5 GW of SMRs targeted by 2050 (long-term goal ~20 GW). TENMAK coordinates domestic R&D and technology transfer; Turkey joined the COP29 "triple nuclear by 2050" declaration. **Update (2025–26):** Akkuyu Unit 1 entered final pre-commissioning (400 kV switchyard energised Dec 2025, dummy-fuel loading June 2026) with a commercial-operation target of end-2026. Following a US–Türkiye Strategic Civil Nuclear Cooperation MoU (September 2025), Ankara declared 2026 the "year of decision" for the 4.8 GW Sinop plant — running trilateral talks with South Korea's KEPCO and US vendors and targeting 50–55% localization — while in June 2026 Turkish firm Nuclean signed an SMR cooperation pact with South Korea's i-SMRDA, alongside parallel talks with TerraPower, advancing the 5 GW SMR goal.

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** National AI Strategy 2021–2025 (+ 2024–2025 Action Plan)
- **Body:** Ministry of Industry and Technology + Presidential Digital Transformation Office
- **Year:** August 2021; Action Plan update 2024
- **URL:** https://cbddo.gov.tr/SharedFolderServer/Genel/File/TRNationalAIStrategy2021-2025.pdf
- **Summary:** Six priorities, 24 objectives, 119 measures spanning public administration, talent, and ethics. The 2024–25 Action Plan pivots to generative AI — sovereign Turkish-language LLMs and scaling national data-center capacity from 250 MW to 1 GW (~$10B). Operates via "soft law" (KVKK guidance) while preparing hard regulation aligned with the EU AI Act; TÜBİTAK is building a "Trusted AI" certification.

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** 2030 Industry and Technology Strategy (establishing the National Quantum Institute under TÜBİTAK)
- **Body:** Ministry of Industry and Technology / TÜBİTAK (BİLGEM)
- **Year:** 2025
- **URL:** https://www.sanayi.gov.tr/assets/pdf/plan-program/2030IndustryAndTechnologyStrategyENG.pdf
- **Summary:** Elevates quantum to a tier-one priority (computing, sensing for defense radar, post-quantum cryptography). Centralizes dispersed efforts into a National Quantum Institute (from TÜBİTAK BİLGEM's Quantum Technologies Department). Validated by "QuanT," Turkey's first domestic quantum computer (TOBB University). PQC research underway (no formal migration roadmap yet); maintains EuroHPC membership for access to systems like MareNostrum 5.

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** National Biotechnology Strategy and Action Plan 2023–2028 + Smart Life and Health Products & Technologies Roadmap
- **Body:** Presidency / Ministry of Industry and Technology / Health Institutes of Türkiye (TÜSEB)
- **Year:** 2022 (Roadmap); 2023 (Biotech Strategy)
- **URL:** https://www.tuseb.gov.tr/en/
- **Summary:** Aims for strategic autonomy by localizing high-value biopharmaceuticals and medical devices, targeting net-exporter status. ~$200M for R&D plus ~$250M in tax incentives/grants; uses public procurement (Ministry of Health buying domestic biosimilars/devices) to de-risk startups. Geographic clusters: Istanbul (corporate/academic), Ankara (policy/national labs), Izmir (startups). Targets a larger share of the ~$1.8B domestic biopharma R&D market.

## 5. Materials Science / Rare Earths
- **Plan:** Critical Raw Materials Strategy (Kritik Ham Maddeler Stratejisi) + Beylikova Rare Earth Elements program (Eti Maden / TENMAK)
- **Body:** Ministry of Energy and Natural Resources; Eti Maden; TENMAK
- **Year:** April 2026 (Critical Raw Materials Strategy); program ongoing 2022–2026
- **URL:** https://enerji.gov.tr/
- **Summary:** On 28 April 2026, Energy Minister Alparslan Bayraktar unveiled a comprehensive Critical Raw Materials Strategy at the OECD Critical Minerals Forum in Istanbul, framing supply security for the "Electric Age" and stressing deep processing and high-tech industrial production over raw extraction, with Beylikova as its cornerstone. Aims to challenge China's REE monopoly via the Beylikova deposit (Eskişehir) — ~694M tonnes of ore (~12.5M tonnes of oxides), claimed as the world's 2nd-largest reserve. Backed by ~$600M: scaling from a 1,200-tonne pilot plant to an industrial facility processing 570,000 tonnes/yr by 2027 (~10,000 tonnes of REO, plus barite, fluorite, and thorium feeding nuclear goals). TENMAK builds separation and permanent-magnet R&D; key bottleneck is China-dominated separation tech. Seen by the West as a supply-chain diversification node.

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** HIT-30 High Technology Investment Program — Semiconductor Call (HIT-Çip)
- **Body:** Ministry of Industry and Technology
- **Year:** July 2024
- **URL:** https://www.trmonitor.net/high-tech-incentive-package-unveiled-usd-30-billion/
- **Summary:** A $5B subsidy (within the $30B HIT-30 program) for semiconductor sovereignty. Target: ≥1 million wafers/yr at 65nm-or-better, covering the full value chain (ingot, wafer, ATP). Incentives include up to 30% capital contribution, 80% tax reductions, and land allocation to attract foundries. Domestic fabless ecosystem: Yongatek (with Beko) toward MCU serial production by 2026 (30–50M units/yr), plus FPGAs for defense and 12nm AI inference chips (TSMC) for 2027–28.

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** 12th Development Plan 2024–2028 + National Broadband Strategy and Action Plan + 5G Authorization Tender
- **Body:** Presidency of Strategy and Budget; Ministry of Transport and Infrastructure; Information and Communication Technologies Authority (BTK)
- **Year:** 2023 (plan); October 2025 (5G tender)
- **URL:** https://www.sbb.gov.tr/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/Twelfth-Development-Plan_2024-2028.pdf
- **Summary:** Mandates universal high-speed broadband (toward 100% household coverage at 100 Mbps) and structured 5G rollout (overseen by BTK). BTK completed Turkey's long-awaited 5G spectrum auction on 16 October 2025 — Turkcell, Vodafone Türkiye and Türk Telekom all won packages (incl. 700 MHz and 3.5 GHz bands), raising over $3.5B — with commercial 5G service mandated to launch across all 81 provinces on 1 April 2026. Deepens the highly successful "e-Devlet" e-government gateway into a "Digital State" (cloud, big data, AI decision-support) with data-localization for sovereignty. Targets a 4× increase in national data-center capacity (to 1 GW, ~$10B) and subsidizes SME digital transformation (€300M EBRD financing).

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** Mobility Vehicles and Technologies Strategy Roadmap (TOGG) + Defense Industry Agency (SSB) 2024–2028 Strategic Plan
- **Body:** Ministry of Industry and Technology; Defense Industry Agency (SSB)
- **Year:** 2022 (Mobility Roadmap); 2024 (SSB plan)
- **URL:** https://www.sanayi.gov.tr/assets/pdf/plan-program/MobiliteAracveTeknolojileriYolHaritasi.pdf
- **Summary:** A dual-track civilian/defense strategy. Civilian: the Mobility Roadmap positions Turkey as a hub for electric, connected, and autonomous vehicles, led by the state-backed TOGG project. Defense (the most globally visible success of the Milli Teknoloji Hamlesi): the SSB plan targets 85% domestic content, prioritizing uncrewed combat aerial vehicles (e.g. Bayraktar Kızılelma) and autonomous swarming to sustain Turkey's multi-billion-dollar defense exports — with growing civil-military technological synergy.
