# Argentina — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Entity 35 (Batch 7). Latin America.*

Argentina is undergoing a dramatic paradigm shift under the Milei administration — pivoting from state-led sovereign technological development toward frameworks that aggressively incentivize foreign direct investment, privatization, and deregulation. The signature instruments are **RIGI** (30-year fiscal stability for mega-investments) and the proposed "Super RIGI" for frontier industries. Distinctive tensions: the historic domestic SMR (CAREM) is being defunded in favor of foreign commercial SMRs to power AI data centers; lithium governance balances provincial ownership against FDI; and drone rules were radically liberalized in 2024/2025.

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** Plan Nuclear Argentino (2025) / Lineamientos de la Política Nuclear Argentina 2026
- **Body:** Ministry of Economy (Secretaría de Asuntos Nucleares); Presidencia (Consejo de Asesores); CNEA; Nucleoeléctrica Argentina S.A. (NA-SA)
- **Year:** Announced December 2024; 2025/2026 budget and guidelines
- **URL:** https://www.argentina.gob.ar/noticias/el-presidente-javier-milei-al-anunciar-el-plan-nuclear-argentino-la-energia-nuclear-tendra
- **Summary:** A dramatic pivot toward international commercialization and partial privatization of NA-SA (up to 44% of shares can be sold; state keeps 51%, 5% for employees, via Ley Bases). It targets deployment of foreign-designed SMRs (the ACR-300, four modules at the Atucha complex) to provide baseload power for regional AI data centers — pitching Argentina as a "Stargate" tech hub leveraging cold southern climates. The historic sovereign SMR project CAREM (CNEA, ~80% complete, ~$750M spent since the 1980s) has been operationally defunded and "de-hierarchized" as a mere demonstration reactor. The regulator ARN was allocated ARS 21.27B for 2025. Argentina retains its legacy of three operating PHWRs (Atucha I, II, Embalse) and full fuel-cycle mastery.

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** Recomendaciones para una Inteligencia Artificial Fiable (Disposición 2/2023) + Guía AAIP para el uso responsable de la IA
- **Body:** Jefatura de Gabinete de Ministros (Subsec. de Tecnologías de la Información) / Agencia de Acceso a la Información Pública (AAIP)
- **Year:** 2023 (Disp. 2/2023); 2024 (AAIP Guidelines)
- **URL:** https://www.argentina.gob.ar/normativa/nacional/disposici%C3%B3n-2-2023-384656
- **Summary:** Argentina's baseline AI governance centers on human-centered, transparent, ethical adoption — building on UNESCO and OECD principles. The 2023 Recomendaciones (the first national-public-sector AI regulatory act) and the 2024 AAIP Guide mandate that a designated human remains legally responsible for algorithmic decisions (algorithms cannot hold liability), require Data Protection Impact Assessments before deployment, and enforce an "explainability" principle plus public algorithmic-transparency registries. The framework traces back to the 2019 Plan Nacional de IA under Agenda Digital 2030. Proposed 2025/2026 legislation would go further: a National Strategy for Sovereign AI funding local deep-tech startups via a venture fund (FONVIA) and tax credits for foundation models trained on Argentine Spanish.

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** Programa Interinstitucional para el Fortalecimiento de las Ciencias y Tecnologías Cuánticas (within the Plan Nacional de Ciencia, Tecnología e Innovación 2030)
- **Body:** Secretariat of Innovation, Science and Technology (formerly MINCyT) / CONICET / national universities
- **Year:** 2023–2025
- **URL:** https://www.argentina.gob.ar/normativa/nacional/resoluci%C3%B3n-282-2025-419597/texto
- **Summary:** Quantum is designated a disruptive strategic priority under PNCTI 2030 for technological sovereignty. The interinstitutional program centralizes funding across two lines — strengthening existing experimental infrastructure and helping new researchers establish labs — with grants up to ~$1.2M USD per project for specialized equipment. Primary beneficiaries include Centro Atómico Bariloche, Universidad Nacional del Sur, and CONICET institutes (superconducting circuits, bio-simulation). A historic milestone in late 2025: Universidad Nacional de Hurlingham (UNAHUR) installed Argentina's first university quantum computer (a 3-qubit room-temperature NMR SpinQ system), federated nationally via an inter-university consortium and linked to Amazon Braket cloud. Proposed 2025/2026 legislation seeks a sovereign, federal legal framework mandating post-quantum cryptography for critical infrastructure.

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** Ley de Promoción del Desarrollo y Producción de la Biotecnología Moderna y la Nanotecnología (Ley 27685)
- **Body:** Honorable Congreso de la Nación / Ministry of Economy / AFIP
- **Year:** Enacted September 2022 (valid until 31 December 2034)
- **URL:** https://www.boletinoficial.gob.ar/detalleAviso/primera/271855/20220916
- **Summary:** Law 27685 modernizes and extends prior biotech legislation (Law 26.270) to include nanotechnology, offering aggressive fiscal incentives for R&D and industrial production within Argentina through 2034. Benefits for firms in the National Registry: accelerated depreciation of capital goods (lowering income-tax liability), early VAT refunds, and — critically — a non-transferable fiscal credit bond equal to 50% of the cost of hiring R&D services from public institutions in the National Science-Technology System (SNCTI: CONICET, INTA, INTI). That third pillar deliberately bridges private industry and public labs, ensuring state-funded research financially benefits from commercial product development. The law leverages Argentina's strong agri-biotech (GM crops, bio-fertilizers) and CONICET-led human-health research.

## 5. Materials Science / Critical Raw Materials (Lithium)
- **Plan:** Régimen de Incentivo para Grandes Inversiones (RIGI, Ley 27742) + Mesa Nacional del Litio
- **Body:** Congress of the Nation / Ministry of Economy / Provincial Governments (Jujuy, Salta, Catamarca)
- **Year:** RIGI enacted July 2024 (project approvals ongoing 2025/2026)
- **URL:** https://www.argentina.gob.ar/normativa/nacional/resoluci%C3%B3n-431-2026-424514
- **Summary:** Argentina's critical-minerals strategy (the "Lithium Triangle," plus copper and potassium) rests on RIGI, embedded in the Ley de Bases (Law 27.742). RIGI ring-fences mega-investments (minimum $200M USD) with 30 years of fiscal, customs and exchange-rate stability: reduced income tax, import-duty exemptions, eventual zero export duties, free profit repatriation, and ICSID arbitration recourse. It has triggered an investment wave — Rio Tinto's $2.29B Rincón project, the $251M Fénix expansion in Catamarca, the $891M San Jorge copper project in Mendoza. Because the constitution grants provinces original domain over resources, the Mesa Nacional del Litio (governors of Jujuy/Salta/Catamarca + SAE + YPF's Y-TEC) coordinates policy, pushing downstream industrialization (cathodes, batteries) and decreeing that miners reserve a production quota for domestic industry. Critics warn RIGI's generous terms risk "reprimarization" — an extractive model with weak tech spillover and ecological harm in the arid Puna.

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** Régimen de Incentivo para Grandes Inversiones en Nuevas Industrias ("Súper RIGI") + Programa Nacional de Diseño de Semiconductores para IA (CENDIA) — both proposed
- **Body:** Executive Branch / Ministry of Economy / National Congress
- **Year:** Proposed May 2026
- **URL:** https://rest.hcdn.gob.ar/web/tramites-parlamentarios/render/adjunto/69b8338fc5c1c.pdf
- **Summary:** Acknowledging that domestic end-to-end fabs are economically unrealistic, Argentina pursues a bifurcated strategy: attract global giants via fiscal incentives while fostering local fabless chip-design IP. The "Super RIGI" bill (submitted May 2026) targets mega-investments over $1B USD (20% in the first two years) in frontier industries — explicitly semiconductor manufacturing, AI infrastructure, and SMRs — with incentives even steeper than standard RIGI: 15% income tax (vs 35%), zero import/export tariffs, employer social-security contributions slashed to 10%, locked for 30 years. In parallel, the proposed Programa Nacional de Diseño de Semiconductores para IA would create CENDIA (Centro Nacional de Diseño de Chips para IA) with INTI and CONICET — equipped with EDA tools and IP libraries, funding scholarships and engineer training in ASIC/FPGA design, and agreements with foreign foundries (TSMC, GlobalFoundries) to prototype Argentine-designed chips abroad.

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** Agenda Digital Argentina 2030 (Decreto 996/2018) + Plan Nacional de Telecomunicaciones "Argentina Conectada"
- **Body:** Jefatura de Gabinete de Ministros / Secretaría de Gobierno de Modernización / ARSAT
- **Year:** 2018 (continuous implementation)
- **URL:** https://www.argentina.gob.ar/noticias/el-gobierno-presento-la-nueva-agenda-digital-2030
- **Summary:** The master framework for digital transformation, aligned with OECD/G20 best practices and Industry 4.0, aiming to universalize internet access, digitize government, and promote digital literacy. Infrastructure is channeled through state-owned ARSAT, which operates the Red Federal de Fibra Óptica (REFEFO) national backbone and satellites (ARSAT-1, ARSAT-2, built by domestic champion INVAP, now integrating into LEO strategies). Targets: 20+ Mbps fixed broadband nationwide, 93% 4G coverage on national routes, connecting 1,600+ rural schools and 3,000 security centers, and full paperless public administration (the Trámites a Distancia platform). Workforce programs like Argentina Programa upskill software developers and IT professionals.

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** Reglamento para la Aviación Civil No Tripulada (Decreto 663/2024 & Resolución ANAC 550/2025)
- **Body:** Administración Nacional de Aviación Civil (ANAC) / Secretaría de Transporte
- **Year:** Decree July 2024; ANAC Resolution 2025
- **URL:** https://www.anac.gob.ar/anac/nuevo-marco-normativo-para-la-operacion-de-drones
- **Summary:** Driven by a deregulation mandate (using DNU 70/23 emergency powers), Argentina entirely overhauled its drone regime, abrogating the restrictive 2019 VANT rules (which capped operations at visual line of sight, ≤200m). The new Reglamento (RAAC Parts 100/101/102) adopts ICAO terminology (RPA/RPAS), legalizes autonomous and Beyond Visual Line of Sight (BVLOS) operations on a risk-mitigation basis rather than blanket bans, and introduces a UTM (Unmanned Aircraft System Traffic Management) framework for integration into controlled airspace. In the terrestrial domain, proposed legislation would establish a Plan Nacional de Robótica e Industria 4.0 (with a Federal Council COFERI and a fund, FONARI) for digital-fabrication centers, advanced 3D printing, and automated logistics. INVAP leads sovereign development of complex autonomous systems for civil and defense use (radar, patrol UAVs).
