# European Union — Supranational Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Entity 21 (Batch 4). EU-level (not member states).*

The EU pursues "Open Strategic Autonomy" via the "Twin Transition" (green + digital), shifting from a free-market model to interventionist industrial policy through binding "Acts" (Chips, AI, CRMA) — the "Brussels Effect" setting global standards. Funding blends central EU instruments (Horizon Europe, Digital Europe, EuroHPC) with relaxed State Aid rules and private capital. Recurring tension: ambitious targets vs. matching US/China financial scale.

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Instrument:** European Industrial Alliance on Small Modular Reactors (SMRs) + Strategic Action Plan (within the Euratom framework; nuclear's transitional inclusion in the EU Taxonomy)
- **Body:** European Commission (DG GROW, DG ENER, DG RTD)
- **Year:** 2024 (Alliance launched Feb 2024; Action Plan Sept 2024)
- **URL:** https://single-market-economy.ec.europa.eu/industry/industrial-alliances/european-industrial-alliance-small-modular-reactors_en
- **Summary:** Aims to accelerate the first European SMR projects by the early 2030s, revitalizing the nuclear supply chain (advanced fuel cycles, critical materials) and a Nuclear Skills Academy. Reflects a more pragmatic supranational stance — nuclear controversially recognized as transitional in the EU Taxonomy. Direct funding is bound by Euratom/Horizon Europe; the Alliance mainly unlocks blended finance and public-private partnerships to overcome investment barriers.

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Instrument:** Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 (Artificial Intelligence Act) + AI Factories Initiative (EuroHPC)
- **Body:** European Parliament & Council; European Commission (European AI Office)
- **Year:** 2024 (AI Act in force Aug 1, 2024, phased to Aug 2026)
- **URL:** https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj
- **Summary:** The world's first binding, risk-based AI law — bans "unacceptable" practices (social scoring, certain biometrics), imposes obligations on high-risk and general-purpose models, with penalties up to €35M or 7% of global turnover. To counterbalance regulation, the AI Factories initiative leverages the €7B EuroHPC budget (covering 50% of AI-supercomputer costs) to give EU startups compute for frontier models.

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Instrument:** European Declaration on Quantum Technologies + Quantum Flagship Strategic Research and Industry Agenda (SRIA 2030)
- **Body:** Council of the EU + European Commission
- **Year:** 2023 (Declaration Dec 2023); 2024 (SRIA update)
- **URL:** https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/library/european-declaration-quantum-technologies
- **Summary:** Aims to make Europe the world's leading "quantum valley" across computing, simulation, sensing, and secure communications. €1B+ committed over a decade (Quantum Flagship from 2018), supplemented by national pools and Horizon Europe. 2030 targets: align national R&D, fast-track lab-to-industry, and build pan-European infrastructure (EuroQCI for secure comms; quantum computers integrated into EuroHPC centers).

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Instrument:** Communication "Building the future with nature: Boosting Biotechnology and Biomanufacturing in the EU" (blueprint for a coming EU Biotech Act)
- **Body:** European Commission (Health, DG GROW, DG RTD)
- **Year:** 2024 (adopted March 20, 2024)
- **URL:** https://ec.europa.eu/commission/presscorner/detail/en/statement_24_1610
- **Summary:** Targets dismantling regulatory barriers to make Europe a leader in health and industrial biomanufacturing, preparing the ground for an EU Biotech Act. Funds generative-AI drug discovery, Regional Innovation Valleys, and addresses dual-use biosecurity. Channels capital via HERA Invest, the EIC accelerator, and the Circular Bio-based Joint Undertaking, with regulatory sandboxes for novel biotech.

## 5. Materials Science / Critical Raw Materials
- **Instrument:** Critical Raw Materials Act (Reg. 2024/1252) + Communication "Advanced Materials for Industrial Leadership" (toward an Advanced Materials Act)
- **Body:** European Parliament & Council; European Commission
- **Year:** 2024 (CRMA in force May 2024; Advanced Materials Feb 2024)
- **URL:** https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1252/oj/eng
- **Summary:** Binding 2030 targets for strategic raw materials: ≥10% domestic extraction, 40% processing, 25% recycling of annual consumption, and no single third country supplying >65%. Streamlines permitting for "Strategic Projects" (24 months extraction / 12 months processing). The Advanced Materials strategy proposes a €500M Horizon Europe public-private partnership and a shared digital "materials commons."

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Instrument:** European Chips Act (Regulation (EU) 2023/1781)
- **Body:** European Parliament & Council
- **Year:** 2023 (in force Sept 21, 2023)
- **URL:** https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/european-chips-act
- **Summary:** Binding target to double the EU's global chip market share from ~10% to 20% by 2030. Unlocks ≥€43B in public-private investment (€4.5B from the EU budget via Horizon Europe/Digital Europe; ~€35B reliant on Member State State Aid for "first-of-a-kind" mega-fabs). Three pillars: R&D leadership, production capacity, and a crisis-monitoring/coordination mechanism.

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Instrument:** Digital Decade Policy Programme 2030 (Decision (EU) 2022/2481)
- **Body:** European Parliament & Council
- **Year:** 2022 (in force early 2023)
- **URL:** https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/digital-decade-policy-programme
- **Summary:** A monitoring/cooperation mechanism with four 2030 targets: digital skills, secure/sustainable infrastructure (universal 5G + gigabit), business digital transformation, and 100% digital public services. Mandates 100% citizen access to e-health records and a universal eID by 2030. Member States submit national roadmaps; "multi-country projects" pool investment for cross-border initiatives (5G corridors, data infrastructure, secure space comms).

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Instrument:** "A Drone Strategy 2.0" (Communication) + Cooperative, Connected and Automated Mobility (CCAM) Partnership
- **Body:** European Commission (DG MOVE, DG RTD, EASA)
- **Year:** 2022 (Drone Strategy 2.0 adopted Nov 2022; CCAM ongoing via Horizon Europe 2023–27)
- **URL:** https://transport.ec.europa.eu/transport-themes/smart-mobility/cooperative-connected-and-automated-mobility-ccam_en
- **Summary:** Drone Strategy 2.0 targets a large-scale commercial drone and urban-air-mobility market by 2030 via the automated "U-space" traffic management system, with civil-defense counter-drone synergies. CCAM integrates shared, highly automated vehicles with digital road infrastructure to cut congestion, emissions, and road deaths. ~€500M committed via Horizon Europe for trustworthy-AI connected/automated driving R&D and cross-border testing.
