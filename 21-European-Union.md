# European Union — Supranational Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Entity 21 (Batch 4). EU-level (not member states).*

The EU pursues "Open Strategic Autonomy" via the "Twin Transition" (green + digital), shifting from a free-market model to interventionist industrial policy through binding "Acts" (Chips, AI, CRMA) — the "Brussels Effect" setting global standards. Funding blends central EU instruments (Horizon Europe, Digital Europe, EuroHPC) with relaxed State Aid rules and private capital. Recurring tension: ambitious targets vs. matching US/China financial scale.

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Instrument:** EU Strategy for Small Modular Reactors (SMRs) and Advanced Modular Reactors (AMRs) (COM/2026/117) + updated Nuclear Illustrative Programme/PINC (COM/2026/120); building on the SMR Industrial Alliance and its Strategic Action Plan (adopted Sept 2025)
- **Body:** European Commission (DG ENER, DG GROW, DG RTD)
- **Year:** 2026 (Strategy adopted 10 March 2026; Alliance launched Feb 2024, Strategic Action Plan Sept 2025)
- **URL:** https://energy.ec.europa.eu/topics/nuclear-energy/small-modular-reactors_en
- **Summary:** A formal Commission strategy (nine actions) to take SMRs from research to first operational European projects by the early 2030s, following a November 2025 call for evidence and the Alliance's 2025 Strategic Action Plan. It projects EU SMR capacity of 17–53 GW by 2050 and targets decarbonization of power, district heating, hydrogen, and high-demand users such as data centres. Reflects a more pragmatic supranational stance — nuclear is recognized as transitional in the EU Taxonomy — while the updated PINC sets out long-term nuclear investment needs. Direct funding remains bound by Euratom/Horizon Europe; the Alliance mainly unlocks blended finance and public-private partnerships to overcome investment barriers.

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Instrument:** AI Continent Action Plan + Apply AI Strategy (industrial-uptake layer atop Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 / AI Act and the AI Factories–EuroHPC initiative)
- **Body:** European Commission (European AI Office, DG CNECT); AI Act adopted by Parliament & Council
- **Year:** 2025 (AI Continent Action Plan 9 April 2025; Apply AI Strategy 8 October 2025; AI Act in force Aug 2024, phased to 2026)
- **URL:** https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/library/ai-continent-action-plan
- **Summary:** The 2025 strategies pivot from regulation to acceleration. The AI Continent Action Plan sets five objectives — large-scale compute (AI gigafactories), better data access, sector adoption, talent/skills, and smoother AI Act implementation — to make the EU a global AI hub. The Apply AI Strategy adds an "AI First" doctrine for industry and the public sector, with an Apply AI Alliance and an AI Observatory to drive and monitor real-world uptake. These sit atop the AI Act — the world's first binding, risk-based AI law (penalties up to €35M or 7% of global turnover) — and the AI Factories initiative, which leverages the EuroHPC budget to give EU startups frontier-model compute.

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Instrument:** Quantum Europe Strategy (building on the European Declaration on Quantum Technologies and the Quantum Flagship)
- **Body:** European Commission (DG CNECT)
- **Year:** 2025 (Strategy adopted 2 July 2025)
- **URL:** https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/quantum
- **Summary:** The first dedicated Commission quantum strategy, aiming to make Europe the world's leading "quantum valley" by 2030 across computing, simulation, sensing, and secure communications. It structures action across five areas: research and innovation; quantum infrastructure (including a pilot facility for a European Quantum Internet); ecosystem building (funding six quantum-chip pilot lines); space and dual-use technologies; and skills, with a European Quantum Skills Academy to launch in 2026. It consolidates the earlier European Declaration on Quantum Technologies and the €1B+ Quantum Flagship, supplemented by national pools and Horizon Europe, and continues pan-European infrastructure (EuroQCI for secure comms; quantum computers integrated into EuroHPC centres).

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Instrument:** European Biotech Act (legislative proposal) + Life Sciences ("Choose Europe for Life Sciences") Strategy; superseding the 2024 "Boosting Biotechnology and Biomanufacturing" Communication
- **Body:** European Commission (DG SANTE/Health, DG GROW, DG RTD)
- **Year:** 2025/26 (Life Sciences Strategy 2 July 2025; Biotech Act proposal 16 December 2025)
- **URL:** https://health.ec.europa.eu/biotechnology_en
- **Summary:** The EU has moved from blueprint to binding legislation. The Life Sciences Strategy (July 2025) aims to make the EU the most attractive global hub for life-sciences innovation by 2030, mobilizing over €10B annually across health, biotechnology, food systems, and green manufacturing to speed lab-to-market transition. The European Biotech Act (proposed Dec 2025) streamlines regulatory frameworks to cut time-to-market for health biotech such as cell and gene therapies, simplifies clinical trials, incentivizes biomanufacturing to stay in Europe, and launches a health-biotech investment pilot with the EIB Group. Together they build on the 2024 "Boosting Biotechnology and Biomanufacturing" Communication while continuing support via HERA Invest, the EIC, and the Circular Bio-based Joint Undertaking, and addressing dual-use biosecurity.

## 5. Materials Science / Critical Raw Materials
- **Instrument:** Critical Raw Materials Act (Reg. 2024/1252) — first Strategic Projects designations + forthcoming Advanced Materials Act (call for evidence/consultation launched 21 Oct 2025)
- **Body:** European Commission; CRMA adopted by Parliament & Council
- **Year:** 2025 (47 EU Strategic Projects designated 25 March 2025; 13 third-country projects 4 June 2025; Advanced Materials Act consultation Oct 2025)
- **URL:** https://single-market-economy.ec.europa.eu/sectors/raw-materials/areas-specific-interest/critical-raw-materials/strategic-projects_en
- **Summary:** 2025 marks the CRMA's move into implementation: the Commission designated its first Strategic Projects — 47 inside the EU (March 2025) and 13 in third countries/overseas (June 2025), spanning extraction, processing, and recycling of lithium, nickel, cobalt, and rare earths — granting them accelerated permitting and facilitated financing. The CRMA's binding 2030 targets remain: ≥10% domestic extraction, 40% processing, 25% recycling of annual consumption, and no single third country supplying >65%. In parallel, the Commission opened a call for evidence (21 Oct 2025, closing Jan 2026) for an Advanced Materials Act under the Clean Industrial Deal, with a SAM scientific opinion due April 2026 and a legislative proposal expected later in 2026 — moving beyond the Feb 2024 "Advanced Materials for Industrial Leadership" strategy toward scaled manufacturing and reduced CRM dependencies.

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Instrument:** Chips Act 2.0 (legislative proposal) — revising the European Chips Act (Reg. (EU) 2023/1781) within the European Technological Sovereignty package
- **Body:** European Commission (DG CNECT); original Chips Act adopted by Parliament & Council
- **Year:** 2026 (Chips Act 2.0 proposal 3 June 2026; consultation/evaluation opened 5 Sept 2025; 27 Member States' declaration 29 Sept 2025)
- **URL:** https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/library/proposal-chips-act-20
- **Summary:** After a September 2025 evaluation and a unanimous Member State call for a bolder approach, the Commission adopted Chips Act 2.0 (June 2026) to close manufacturing gaps for advanced nodes, reduce strategic dependencies on external suppliers, and secure dual-use chip supply chains. New measures include a B2B semiconductor supply-chain platform, demand stimulation, and simplified rules for investment. It retains the original Act's binding goal — doubling the EU's global chip market share from ~10% to 20% by 2030 — and its three pillars (R&D leadership, production capacity, crisis monitoring), which unlocked ≥€43B in public-private investment largely via Member State State Aid for "first-of-a-kind" mega-fabs.

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Instrument:** Digital Networks Act (legislative proposal) — single regulation modernizing EU telecoms rules, under the Digital Decade Policy Programme 2030 (Decision (EU) 2022/2481)
- **Body:** European Commission (DG CNECT)
- **Year:** 2026 (Digital Networks Act proposal 21 January 2026; Digital Decade Programme in force 2023)
- **URL:** https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/digital-networks-act
- **Summary:** The Digital Networks Act (proposed Jan 2026) consolidates and simplifies sector-specific EU telecoms rules into a single regulation to boost cross-border connectivity, streamline spectrum assignment, phase out legacy copper for full-fibre networks, and create investment-friendly conditions for the EU's 2030 gigabit targets. It operationalizes the connectivity pillar of the Digital Decade Policy Programme 2030, whose four targets (digital skills, secure/sustainable infrastructure with universal 5G + gigabit, business digital transformation, 100% digital public services) remain the overarching monitoring framework. The Digital Decade still mandates 100% citizen access to e-health records and a universal eID by 2030, with Member State roadmaps and "multi-country projects" pooling investment for cross-border initiatives (5G corridors, data infrastructure, secure space comms).

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Instrument:** EU Action Plan on Drone and Counter-Drone Security (superseding the mid-term review of Drone Strategy 2.0); continued CCAM Partnership for connected/automated road mobility
- **Body:** European Commission (DG HOME, DG DEFIS, DG CNECT, DG MOVE, EASA)
- **Year:** 2026 (Action Plan adopted 11 February 2026; CCAM ongoing via Horizon Europe; Drone Strategy 2.0 from Nov 2022)
- **URL:** https://ec.europa.eu/commission/presscorner/detail/en/ip_26_364
- **Summary:** The 2026 Action Plan pivots EU drone policy toward security and defence, effectively replacing the planned mid-term review of Drone Strategy 2.0 and complementing the European Drone Defence Initiative. Its four pillars: preparedness via rapid technological development; detection using AI software and 5G networks; coordinated EU counter-drone emergency response; and defence readiness through mass production and civil-military synergies. The civilian commercial agenda continues — Drone Strategy 2.0's large-scale drone/urban-air-mobility market by 2030 via the automated "U-space" traffic system — alongside the CCAM Partnership, which integrates shared, highly automated vehicles with digital road infrastructure (~€500M via Horizon Europe for trustworthy-AI connected/automated driving R&D and cross-border testing).
