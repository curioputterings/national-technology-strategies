# Ukraine — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Entity 43 (Batch 9). Europe (at war).*

The full-scale Russian invasion (since Feb 2022) is the inescapable context for every domain. The impact is sharply asymmetric: domains with battlefield or survival applications — **autonomous systems/defense-tech, digital e-governance, applied microelectronics** — have hyper-accelerated, while basic science, biomedicine, and energy infrastructure have been disrupted by brain drain, budget reallocation, and kinetic strikes. The government consolidates ambitions under the **WINWIN Global Innovation Strategy until 2030** ("Defense Valley") and the globally-celebrated **Diia** "State in a Smartphone." Defining features: drone warfare coordinated by the **Brave1** cluster, a US/EU-courted critical-minerals endowment, and a Westinghouse-led nuclear pivot away from Russia.

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** Energy Strategy of Ukraine until 2050 (+ Energoatom–Westinghouse AP1000 program)
- **Body:** Ministry of Energy; Cabinet of Ministers; Energoatom
- **Year:** Approved 21 April 2023
- **URL:** https://mev.gov.ua/en/reforma/energy-strategy
- **Summary:** Nuclear supplied ~55% of power pre-invasion; Russia's occupation of the Zaporizhzhia NPP (Europe's largest, 6× VVER-1000) since March 2022 is an unprecedented militarization of civilian nuclear infrastructure. The 2050 strategy targets **24 GWe nuclear capacity by 2050**, with a 2035 mix of 50% renewables / 50% nuclear and full coal phase-out. To replace Soviet-era plants and decouple from Russia, Energoatom partnered with Westinghouse to build **nine AP1000 reactors** (+11,250 MWe), prioritizing Khmelnytskyi Units 3 & 4 (using equipment from Bulgaria's incomplete Belene project), and localized VVER-440 fuel production to end Russian fuel dependence. The strategy (developed with UK/KPMG assistance) aligns with the EU Association Agreement and aims to fully integrate Ukraine into ENTSO-E as a decentralized, climate-neutral energy hub — though all of it remains vulnerable to ongoing strikes.

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** Roadmap for the Regulation of AI in Ukraine; White Paper on AI Regulation
- **Body:** Ministry of Digital Transformation (MDT)
- **Year:** October 2023 (Roadmap); June 2024 (White Paper)
- **URL:** https://ai.thedigital.gov.ua/en/vision
- **Summary:** Despite the war, the AI sector is robust (5,200+ specialists, 243 startups, concentrated in DefenseTech/GovTech/cyber). The MDT deliberately rejected hardline wartime legislation for a **"bottom-up" approach**: Stage 1 (2023–2026) fosters self-regulation via voluntary codes, recommendations, and the White Paper to prepare developers for EU standards without punitive constraints; Stage 2 drafts a comprehensive law analogous to the EU AI Act (draft bill planned Q4 2026). Roadmap milestones include the HUDERIA pilot (rights/democracy impact assessment), a regulatory sandbox (launching 2025–2026), and sector-specific guidelines. Goals: protect rights/data, ready Ukrainian IT for the EU market, and meet European-integration requirements. The WINWIN strategy aspires to a global top-three position in AI development/public-sector integration by 2030.

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** WINWIN Global Innovation Strategy until 2030 (broad mandate) + NATO Science for Peace and Security (SPS) quantum initiatives
- **Body:** MDT / Ministry of Education and Science / NATO SPS
- **Year:** 2024 (WINWIN)
- **URL:** https://winwin.gov.ua/en
- **Summary:** Highly nascent and war-disrupted — Ukraine lacks the capital infrastructure (cleanrooms cost $15–30M) for sovereign quantum hardware, so the domain runs on international collaboration, talent retention, and NATO alignment. There is no standalone funded national quantum strategy; QIS is embedded in WINWIN 2030 and Horizon Europe. The most structured funding comes from **NATO SPS**, where Ukrainian scientists co-lead six quantum activities (the most of any NATO partner): hybrid spin-photonic quantum frequency converters (2021–2024), single-microwave-photon counters from superconducting flux qubits (with France/Slovakia/Sweden), and US-Ukraine quantum-networks/thin-film research. The strategic goal is to leverage Ukraine's theoretical-physics and materials-science strength to become an R&D node for NATO's "quantum-ready Alliance" ambition. *(Note: the firm "Quantum Systems" active in Ukraine works on drones, not quantum computing — see Domain 8.)*

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** WINWIN Global Innovation Strategy — MedTech & BioTech Sectoral Strategies
- **Body:** Ministry of Digital Transformation
- **Year:** 2024–2025
- **URL:** https://winwin.gov.ua/en
- **Summary:** War has forged a militarized MedTech branch focused on mass-casualty needs. The MedTech strategy prioritizes domestic production of bionic prostheses, endoprostheses, and rehabilitation equipment; 3D modeling/bioengineering for severe burns and complex wounds; and telemedicine + AI clinical decision-making linking frontline areas to international teams. The BioTech Sectoral Strategy aims to make Ukraine a biotechnology hub (medicine, agriculture, environmental restoration) by 2030 — building clusters, advancing bioinformatics, and using agri-biotech for food security and ecosystem remediation, while addressing weak patenting/commercialization. To counter severe underfunding, the US National Academy of Sciences launched the Science and Innovation Fund for Ukraine (>$13M) for grants, fellowships, and commercialization training.

## 5. Materials Science / Critical Raw Materials
- **Plan:** Draft Critical Raw Materials Strategy + EU-Ukraine Strategic Partnership on Raw Materials
- **Body:** Ministry of Economy; Ministry of Environmental Protection and Natural Resources
- **Year:** 2021 (EU MoU); 2024 (Draft Strategy)
- **URL:** https://mepr.gov.ua
- **Summary:** Ukraine holds some of Europe's largest reserves of critical raw materials (lithium, titanium, graphite, manganese, rare earths), though many deposits sit in war-affected east/south. Geopolitically, CRMs are an economic lifeline — offering the EU/US a secure non-China/Russia supply chain and intertwining recovery with Western clean-tech/defense manufacturing. The foundation is the 2021 EU MoU (aligned with the EU CRM Act's 10% extraction / 40% processing / 25% recycling 2030 targets). The 2024 Draft Strategy pushes governance reform to move from raw exporter to deep-processing hub: digitized open geological databases (ending Soviet-era secrecy), single-contact-point permitting, EU-aligned tax incentives, flagship titanium/lithium/graphite projects, and "Net-Zero Valleys"/battery clusters. A US-Ukraine minerals and reconstruction investment fund (2025) is being negotiated to de-risk private capital via war-risk insurance.

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** Microelectronics Development Strategy + Chip Act UA (draft)
- **Body:** Ministry of Digital Transformation / Cabinet of Ministers
- **Year:** 2023 (Strategy); 2024 (Chip Act draft; Strategy approved early 2024)
- **URL:** https://digitalstate.gov.ua
- **Summary:** Battlefield demand for drone and electronic-warfare components has elevated microelectronics to a national-security priority. The Microelectronics Development Strategy sets an ambitious goal: a global top-15 position and **1% of world chip production by 2030**. Ukraine also holds raw materials critical for chipmaking (silicon quartzites, neon gas, germanium, gallium arsenide), favoring upstream integration. Implementation: a first dedicated microelectronics R&D **clean room launching in 2026** (Innovation Development Fund, with the Kyiv School of Economics and Swiss universities); a long-term domestic chip plant targeting 50,000 chips/month within three years of launch; and the **Chip Act UA** (in drafting) to harmonize with the European Chips Act (with Spanish and EU4Digital support).

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** Diia ecosystem + Diia City (within the WINWIN GovTech sector)
- **Body:** Ministry of Digital Transformation
- **Year:** 2020 (Diia launch); 2024 (WINWIN)
- **URL:** https://diia.gov.ua / https://digitalstate.gov.ua
- **Summary:** Ukraine's most globally recognized tech success. Launched under Zelenskyy's 2019 "State in a Smartphone" promise, **Diia** ("Action") — an integrated app/portal with 22M+ users — became a resilient wartime lifeline rather than collapsing. Ukraine was the first country to give digital passports full legal equivalence. Wartime modules rapidly deployed: eRecovery (compensation for destroyed property), eEnemy (citizen reporting of Russian troop movements to the Armed Forces), and war-bond/IDP-support functions. **Diia City** (launched weeks before the invasion) is a virtual free economic zone with competitive tax/IP terms supporting 1,700+ IT companies, preventing capital flight. In April 2024 Diia was open-sourced on GitHub (USAID-backed) for international replication. Ukraine rose to 5th globally in the UN E-Government Development Index (Online Services).

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** WINWIN Global Innovation Strategy (DefenseTech) + Brave1 mandate
- **Body:** Ministry of Digital Transformation; Ministry of Defense; Brave1
- **Year:** 2023 (Brave1); 2024 (WINWIN)
- **URL:** https://brave1.gov.ua / https://winwin.gov.ua/en
- **Summary:** The hyper-growth domain that has reshaped modern warfare. Facing a superior conventional force, Ukraine pivoted to asymmetric, cheap, rapidly-iterated drones — FPVs, naval drones (Magura), UGVs, AI interceptors, and decoy-saturation fleets. **Brave1** is the government defense-tech cluster connecting military, startups, and investors: 3,500+ registered developments, 260+ codified to NATO standards, 470 grants totaling 1.3B+ UAH. Procurement was decentralized to ~700 military units (commanders buy COTS drones directly), and an "Army of Drones Bonus" (ePoints) gamifies warfare — units earn points for verified target destruction redeemable for more drones. Defense-industry production capacity exceeded €10B in 2024. International JVs scale output — e.g., Germany's Quantum Systems' "Build with Ukraine" (Quantum WIY Industries: 15,000 interceptor drones; Quantum Tencore: UGVs). A key target is autonomous interceptors (e.g., Strila, ~350 km/h) to down Shaheds cheaply, plus AI trained on Ukrainian datasets to recognize targets and beat Russian EW jamming.
