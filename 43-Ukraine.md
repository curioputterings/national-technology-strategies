# Ukraine — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Entity 43 (Batch 9). Europe (at war).*

The full-scale Russian invasion (since Feb 2022) is the inescapable context for every domain. The impact is sharply asymmetric: domains with battlefield or survival applications — **autonomous systems/defense-tech, digital e-governance, applied microelectronics** — have hyper-accelerated, while basic science, biomedicine, and energy infrastructure have been disrupted by brain drain, budget reallocation, and kinetic strikes. The government consolidates ambitions under the **WINWIN Global Innovation Strategy until 2030** ("Defense Valley") — formally adopted by the Cabinet of Ministers in 2025 with an Operational Plan (2025–2027) — and the globally-celebrated **Diia** "State in a Smartphone," now pivoting toward an AI-driven "Agentic State." Defining features: drone warfare coordinated by the **Brave1** cluster and the scaled **"Drone Line"** program, a US/EU-courted critical-minerals endowment now anchored by an enacted 20-year strategy and a US–Ukraine investment fund, and a Westinghouse-led nuclear pivot away from Russia.

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** Energy Strategy of Ukraine until 2050 (+ Energoatom–Westinghouse AP1000 program)
- **Body:** Ministry of Energy; Cabinet of Ministers; Energoatom
- **Year:** Approved 21 April 2023; AP1000 design package milestone 2026
- **URL:** https://mev.gov.ua/en/reforma/energy-strategy / https://energoatom.com.ua
- **Summary:** Nuclear supplied ~55% of power pre-invasion; Russia's occupation of the Zaporizhzhia NPP (Europe's largest, 6× VVER-1000) since March 2022 is an unprecedented militarization of civilian nuclear infrastructure. The 2050 strategy targets **24 GWe nuclear capacity by 2050**, with a 2035 mix of 50% renewables / 50% nuclear and full coal phase-out. To replace Soviet-era plants and decouple from Russia, Energoatom partnered with Westinghouse to build **nine AP1000 reactors** (+11,250 MWe), prioritizing Khmelnytskyi Units 3 & 4 (using equipment from Bulgaria's incomplete Belene project), and localized VVER-440 fuel production to end Russian fuel dependence. By 2026 the AP1000 reactor design package for Khmelnytskyi was reported finalized and undergoing localization to map Ukrainian suppliers into Westinghouse's global chain, accelerating the deployment timeline; energy/nuclear assets also feature in the US–Ukraine Reconstruction Investment Fund. The strategy (developed with UK/KPMG assistance) aligns with the EU Association Agreement and aims to fully integrate Ukraine into ENTSO-E as a decentralized, climate-neutral energy hub — though all of it remains vulnerable to ongoing strikes.

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** Roadmap for the Regulation of AI in Ukraine; White Paper on AI Regulation; Action Plan for the Concept for the Development of AI in Ukraine 2025–2026
- **Body:** Ministry of Digital Transformation (MDT)
- **Year:** October 2023 (Roadmap); June 2024 (White Paper); October 2025 (Action Plan)
- **URL:** https://ai.thedigital.gov.ua/en/vision / https://thedigital.gov.ua
- **Summary:** Despite the war, the AI sector is robust (5,200+ specialists, 243 startups, concentrated in DefenseTech/GovTech/cyber). The MDT deliberately rejected hardline wartime legislation for a **"bottom-up" approach**: Stage 1 (2023–2026) fosters self-regulation via voluntary codes, recommendations, and the White Paper to prepare developers for EU standards without punitive constraints; Stage 2 drafts a comprehensive law analogous to the EU AI Act (draft bill planned Q4 2026). In October 2025 the government adopted an **Action Plan implementing the Concept for the Development of AI for 2025–2026**, sequencing the voluntary transitional regime, the AI regulatory sandbox, and the HUDERIA-based impact methodology ahead of the EU-harmonized law. Institutionally, MDT stood up a **WINWIN AI Centre of Excellence** (2025) to deploy national LLMs and oversee the sandbox, and is steering toward an **"Agentic State"** — deploying AI agents (e.g. Diia.AI) across public administration. The WINWIN strategy aspires to a global top-three position in AI development/public-sector integration by 2030.

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** WINWIN Global Innovation Strategy until 2030 (broad mandate) + NATO Science for Peace and Security (SPS) quantum initiatives
- **Body:** MDT / Ministry of Education and Science / NATO SPS
- **Year:** 2024 (WINWIN)
- **URL:** https://winwin.gov.ua/en
- **Summary:** Highly nascent and war-disrupted — Ukraine lacks the capital infrastructure (cleanrooms cost $15–30M) for sovereign quantum hardware, so the domain runs on international collaboration, talent retention, and NATO alignment. There is no standalone funded national quantum strategy; QIS is embedded in WINWIN 2030 and Horizon Europe. The most structured funding comes from **NATO SPS**, where Ukrainian scientists co-lead six quantum activities (the most of any NATO partner): hybrid spin-photonic quantum frequency converters (2021–2024), single-microwave-photon counters from superconducting flux qubits (with France/Slovakia/Sweden), and US-Ukraine quantum-networks/thin-film research. The strategic goal is to leverage Ukraine's theoretical-physics and materials-science strength to become an R&D node for NATO's "quantum-ready Alliance" ambition. *(Note: the firm "Quantum Systems" active in Ukraine works on drones, not quantum computing — see Domain 8.)*

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** WINWIN Global Innovation Strategy — MedTech & BioTech Sectoral Strategies
- **Body:** Ministry of Digital Transformation
- **Year:** 2024–2025
- **URL:** https://winwin.gov.ua/en
- **Summary:** War has forged a militarized MedTech branch focused on mass-casualty needs. The MedTech strategy prioritizes domestic production of bionic prostheses, endoprostheses, and rehabilitation equipment; 3D modeling/bioengineering for severe burns and complex wounds; and telemedicine + AI clinical decision-making linking frontline areas to international teams. The BioTech Sectoral Strategy aims to make Ukraine a biotechnology hub (medicine, agriculture, environmental restoration) by 2030 — building clusters, advancing bioinformatics, and using agri-biotech for food security and ecosystem remediation, while addressing weak patenting/commercialization. To counter severe underfunding, the US National Academy of Sciences launched the Science and Innovation Fund for Ukraine (>$13M) for grants, fellowships, and commercialization training.

## 5. Materials Science / Critical Raw Materials
- **Plan:** Strategy for the Development of Industries Based on Strategic and Critical Raw Materials (to 2056) + Operational Action Plan 2026–2028; Resolution No. 845 strategic-minerals lists; US–Ukraine Reconstruction Investment Fund; EU-Ukraine Strategic Partnership on Raw Materials
- **Body:** Cabinet of Ministers; Ministry of Economy; Ministry of Environmental Protection and Natural Resources
- **Year:** 2021 (EU MoU); July 2025 (Resolution No. 845); 2025 (US–Ukraine Fund); June 2026 (2056 Strategy)
- **URL:** https://mepr.gov.ua / https://me.gov.ua
- **Summary:** Ukraine holds some of Europe's largest reserves of critical raw materials (lithium, titanium, graphite, manganese, rare earths), though many deposits sit in war-affected east/south. Geopolitically, CRMs are an economic lifeline — offering the EU/US a secure non-China/Russia supply chain and intertwining recovery with Western clean-tech/defense manufacturing. The foundation is the 2021 EU MoU (aligned with the EU CRM Act's 10% extraction / 40% processing / 25% recycling 2030 targets). The earlier draft has now hardened into adopted policy: **Cabinet Resolution No. 845 (14 July 2025)** approved the official lists of strategic and critical minerals and a transparent framework for subsoil auctions and Production Sharing Agreements; and in **June 2026 the Cabinet approved a 20-year Strategy (to 2056) with an Operational Action Plan for 2026–2028** that restricts raw-ore export and steers state support toward domestic deep processing, recycling of mining waste, and finished advanced products. Investment is anchored by the **US–Ukraine Reconstruction Investment Fund** (Mineral Resources Agreement signed 30 April 2025; co-governed limited partnership seeded by the US DFC, with a 2026 World Bank MIGA political-risk-insurance framework to de-risk private capital). Implementation also advances digitized open geological databases (ending Soviet-era secrecy), single-contact-point permitting, and flagship titanium/lithium/graphite and battery-cluster projects.

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** Microelectronics Development Strategy + Chip Act UA (draft)
- **Body:** Ministry of Digital Transformation / Cabinet of Ministers
- **Year:** 2023 (Strategy); 2024 (Chip Act draft; Strategy approved early 2024); 2025 (MDT mandate expanded)
- **URL:** https://digitalstate.gov.ua
- **Summary:** Battlefield demand for drone and electronic-warfare components has elevated microelectronics to a national-security priority. The Microelectronics Development Strategy sets an ambitious goal: a global top-15 position and **1% of world chip production by 2030**. In April 2025 a Cabinet of Ministers resolution formally expanded the MDT's mandate to set policy for semiconductors, robotics and AI, consolidating institutional ownership of the agenda. Ukraine also holds raw materials critical for chipmaking (silicon quartzites, neon gas, germanium, gallium arsenide), favoring upstream integration. Implementation: a first dedicated microelectronics R&D **clean room launching in 2026** (Innovation Development Fund, with the Kyiv School of Economics and Swiss universities); a long-term domestic chip plant targeting 50,000 chips/month within three years of launch; and the **Chip Act UA** (in drafting) to harmonize with the European Chips Act (with Spanish and EU4Digital support).

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** Diia ecosystem + Diia City + WINWIN Global Innovation Strategy until 2030 (Cabinet-approved) + Diia.AI ("Agentic State")
- **Body:** Ministry of Digital Transformation
- **Year:** 2020 (Diia launch); 2025 (WINWIN adoption + Operational Plan 2025–2027; Diia.AI)
- **URL:** https://diia.gov.ua / https://digitalstate.gov.ua
- **Summary:** Ukraine's most globally recognized tech success. Launched under Zelenskyy's 2019 "State in a Smartphone" promise, **Diia** ("Action") — an integrated app/portal with 24M+ users — became a resilient wartime lifeline rather than collapsing. Ukraine was the first country to give digital passports full legal equivalence. Wartime modules rapidly deployed: eRecovery (compensation for destroyed property), eEnemy (citizen reporting of Russian troop movements to the Armed Forces), and war-bond/IDP-support functions. **Diia City** (launched weeks before the invasion) is a virtual free economic zone with competitive tax/IP terms supporting 1,700+ IT companies, preventing capital flight. The umbrella **WINWIN Global Innovation Strategy until 2030** was formally adopted by the Cabinet of Ministers in 2025 with an Operational Plan (2025–2027), and an MDT resolution (April 2025) expanded the ministry's mandate to cover AI, robotics and semiconductor policy. Ukraine is now moving from "Digital State" to **"Agentic State"**: from September 2025 it integrated **Diia.AI**, an AI government-services assistant, with the goal of 100% of public services online, complemented by a WINWIN **EdTech Centre of Excellence** (September 2025, with Estonia) and national AI-literacy programs. In April 2024 Diia was open-sourced on GitHub (USAID-backed) for international replication; Ukraine ranks among the world's top performers in the UN E-Government Development Index (Online Services).

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** WINWIN Global Innovation Strategy (DefenseTech) + Brave1 mandate + "Drone Line" program
- **Body:** Ministry of Digital Transformation; Ministry of Defense; Brave1
- **Year:** 2023 (Brave1); 2024 (WINWIN); 2025 ("Drone Line")
- **URL:** https://brave1.gov.ua / https://mil.gov.ua
- **Summary:** The hyper-growth domain that has reshaped modern warfare. Facing a superior conventional force, Ukraine pivoted to asymmetric, cheap, rapidly-iterated drones — FPVs, naval drones (Magura), UGVs, AI interceptors, and decoy-saturation fleets. **Brave1** is the government defense-tech cluster connecting military, startups, and investors: 3,500+ registered developments, 260+ codified to NATO standards, 470 grants totaling 1.3B+ UAH. The Ministry of Defense's **"Drone Line" program** (scaled through 2025–2026) restructured force-generation and funding around elite UAV brigades, fusing reconnaissance, autonomous strike and electronic warfare with ground assault to create deep (10–15 km) frontline "killzones." Procurement was decentralized to ~700 military units (commanders buy COTS drones directly), and an "Army of Drones Bonus" (ePoints) gamifies warfare — units earn points for verified target destruction redeemable for more drones. Defense-industry production capacity has surged toward a multi-tens-of-billions (~$40B) annual scale, reinforcing both Ukrainian and European industrial bases. International JVs scale output — e.g., Germany's Quantum Systems' "Build with Ukraine" (Quantum WIY Industries: 15,000 interceptor drones; Quantum Tencore: UGVs). A key target is autonomous interceptors (e.g., Strila, ~350 km/h) to down Shaheds cheaply, plus AI trained on Ukrainian datasets to recognize targets and beat Russian EW jamming.
