# Spain — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Country 19 (Batch 4). OECD/EU bloc.*

Spain runs a historic, EU-financed modernization via NextGenerationEU and its "PERTEs" (Strategic Projects for Economic Recovery and Transformation), under the "España Digital 2026" umbrella. Novel institutions: SETT (a sovereign tech fund for chips) and AESIA (Europe's first national AI regulator). Nuclear is the most contested area — a legislated 2027–2035 phase-out now under intense pressure to reverse.

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** Integrated National Energy and Climate Plan 2021–2030 (PNIEC)
- **Body:** Ministry for the Ecological Transition (MITECO)
- **Year:** 2019/20 original; updated 2023–2030; phase-out actively challenged through 2025–26
- **URL:** https://www.miteco.gob.es (PNIEC 2023 update)
- **Summary:** Mandates a gradual phase-out of Spain's seven reactors between 2027 and 2035, replacing baseload with renewables and up to 22 GW of storage; ENRESA-managed funds (>€20B) cover dismantling and waste. But the April 28, 2025 Iberian blackout — which exposed weak synchronous generation and Spain's <3% interconnection vs. the 15% EU target — sharply intensified pressure to reverse course: after a non-binding Congress motion (Feb 12, 2025), the Almaraz owners formally requested a lifetime extension (to ~June 2030) on November 2, 2025, and in June 2026 the Senate passed an opposition-led amendment to delay the scheduled shutdowns of Almaraz, Ascó and Cofrentes. The government must now weigh a structural rewrite of the PNIEC closure calendar, leaving the phase-out — once firmly legislated — increasingly contingent. Nuclear currently provides ~20% of electricity.

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** Artificial Intelligence Strategy 2024 (Estrategia de IA 2024, updating the 2020 ENIA), now paired with the **Anteproyecto de Ley de Inteligencia Artificial** (draft AI Law)
- **Body:** Ministry for Digital Transformation and Civil Service / Secretariat of State for Digitalization and AI (SEDIA); AESIA as supervisor
- **Year:** Strategy May 2024; draft AI Law approved by Council of Ministers March 11, 2025
- **URL:** http://espanadigital.gob.es/estrategia-de-inteligencia-artificial-2024 ; draft law: https://digital.gob.es/comunicacion/notas-prensa/mtdfp/2025/03/2025-03-11
- **Summary:** The 2024 Strategy commits €1.5B (on top of a prior €600M) across three axes: AI levers (supercomputing/data centers), public+private adoption, and humanistic governance — including €90M to upgrade the MareNostrum 5 supercomputer (BSC) and, distinctively, **ALIA**, an open-source foundational model trained in Castilian Spanish and the co-official languages (Catalan, Basque, Galician) to counter anglocentric bias. In a major 2025 step, the Council of Ministers approved (March 11, 2025) a draft AI Law that nationalizes the EU AI Act: it grants full sanctioning powers to AESIA (Europe's first national AI regulator), bans manipulative/biometric-categorization practices, and mandates labelling of AI-generated content and deepfakes. The bill advanced through advisory review (Economic and Social Council) toward parliamentary approval, while "Green AI" remains a cross-cutting theme.

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** Quantum Spain
- **Body:** SEDIA, coordinated by the Barcelona Supercomputing Center (BSC)
- **Year:** Launched 2021 (strategic reports 2023–24)
- **URL:** https://quantumspain-project.es
- **Summary:** >€60M state investment to build a sovereign quantum ecosystem, installing a quantum computer at BSC scaling to 20 qubits by end-2025, integrated into the Spanish Supercomputing Network (RES) and aligned with EuroHPC. Milestone: a 5-qubit chip from the Qilimanjaro-GMV consortium tested remotely with BSC. Funds quantum algorithms for AI, cybersecurity, and logistics; Spain joined the OECD Global Technology Forum. Bottlenecks remain in qubit stability and error correction.

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** Programa Estratégico Salud de Vanguardia 2025–2027 (PESV) — direct continuation of the PERTE para la Salud de Vanguardia (+ Complementary Plan for Biotechnology Applied to Health, PCBAS)
- **Body:** Ministry of Science, Innovation and Universities (MICIU) and Ministry of Health
- **Year:** PERTE approved late 2021; PESV 2025–2027 successor framework presented 2025–26
- **URL:** https://planderecuperacion.gob.es
- **Summary:** The original PERTE (~€982M+ public-private) modernized healthcare via biotech and data science across four pillars: advanced/cell therapies (CAR-T), precision genomic medicine (IMPaCT-GENóMICA), digital health/"digital twins," and domestic biotech manufacturing, with the PCBAS decentralizing R&D across autonomous communities (e.g. Galicia's national drug-screening lead). Building on >€2.8B mobilized, the government rolled the effort into the Programa Estratégico Salud de Vanguardia 2025–2027 (PESV) to ensure continuity: it consolidates advanced-biotech capacity, finances national gene/cell-therapy networks (e.g. CERTERA), scales precision medicine, and launches biotech and digital-health hubs. The programme continues to build anonymized health "data lakes" to train predictive-health AI; 46+ specialized advanced-cell-therapy entities operate nationwide (per AseBio).

## 5. Materials Science / Critical Minerals
- **Plan:** I Plan de Acción para la Gestión Sostenible de las Materias Primas Minerales 2026–2030 (operationalizing the 2022 Hoja de Ruta)
- **Body:** MITECO
- **Year:** Roadmap August 2022; first Action Plan 2026–2030 approved March 10, 2026
- **URL:** https://www.miteco.gob.es (materias-primas-minerales); https://www.lamoncloa.gob.es
- **Summary:** The 2022 Roadmap (aligned with the EU Critical Raw Materials Act) set 46 measures to secure strategic minerals and cut dependence on China — promoting domestic extraction (e.g. lithium in Extremadura), refining and circular economy, including recovery of critical minerals from legacy tailings (escombreras). In March 2026 the government approved its first concrete Action Plan 2026–2030, endowed with ~€414M, to safeguard strategic autonomy in raw materials for the digital and green transitions (lithium, copper, rare earths). Its centerpiece is the Programa Nacional de Exploración Minera (PNEM) 2026–2030 — Spain's first national mining-exploration strategy in roughly 50 years — boosting geological mapping, extraction and circularity (including e-waste recovery of critical minerals), while still navigating tension between extraction and local environmental opposition.

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** PERTE Chip (Strategic Project for Microelectronics and Semiconductors)
- **Body:** Ministry for Digital Transformation / Spanish Society for Technological Transformation (SETT, formerly SEMyS)
- **Year:** May 2022 (SETT established by Royal Decree July 2024)
- **URL:** https://www.pertechip.com/
- **Summary:** Spain's largest recovery project — €12.25B through 2027 for an end-to-end sovereign chip value chain. €9.35B subsidizes cutting-edge (<5nm) and mid-range (>5nm) fabs; the rest funds R&D/design, leveraging Spanish strengths in RISC-V (BSC), integrated photonics (Valencia, Vigo), and quantum chips, plus a €200M startup equity fund. Through 2025–26 SETT evolved from a pure grant body into an active sovereign fund executing direct co-investments (taking equity stakes of up to 49.9% in strategic projects), with a September 2025 IPCEI funding window offering up to ~70% support for local microelectronics. "Cátedras Chip" train 1,000+ specialists; the programme remains aligned with the EU Chips Act.

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** España Digital 2026
- **Body:** Ministry for Digital Transformation
- **Year:** July 2022 (updating the 2020/2025 agenda)
- **URL:** https://avance.digital.gob.es/programas-avance-digital/Paginas/Espana_Digital_2026.aspx
- **Summary:** Master framework with eight sub-plans (AI, 5G, cybersecurity, etc.) coordinating gigabit connectivity, 5G, and digital literacy. Spain already has one of Europe's strongest fiber networks and a strategic position for transatlantic/Africa subsea cables. Focus shifted to SME digitization (the "Digital Kit," subsidizing cloud/e-commerce for 98%+ SME fabric) and public-administration modernization. In a 2025 mid-term pivot, emphasis moved from physical infrastructure toward the cyber ecosystem and sovereign AI/data spaces: at the ENISE convention (October 2025) the government announced a revision of the Estrategia Nacional de Ciberseguridad and the creation of a new Centro Nacional de Ciberseguridad attached to the Presidency, complementing INCIBE's ongoing Plan Estratégico Plurianual 2023–2026 (incl. the €235M CIBERINNOVA plan, €224M via Innovative Public Procurement).

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** PERTE VEC (Strategic Project for Electric and Connected Vehicles), now in successive rounds (VEC IV / VEC V)
- **Body:** Ministry of Industry and Tourism
- **Year:** 2021; VEC IV modifications January 2026, VEC V launched March 2026
- **URL:** https://www.mintur.gob.es ; https://planderecuperacion.gob.es
- **Summary:** A >€4.5B industrial overhaul of Spain's auto sector (EU's 2nd-largest) toward electric, connected, and autonomous mobility — co-financing battery gigafactories, AV testing labs (Spanish Network of Labs for Autonomous & Connected Vehicles), and deep factory robotization. Anchors investment from VW/SEAT/Stellantis; firms like GKN (Vigo) tripled robotic workforces with AI/computer-vision (RobotVEC). The Ministry accelerated the framework into 2026 — modifying the VEC IV value-chain line (BOE, January 13, 2026) and announcing the launch of PERTE VEC V (March 19, 2026, an initial €100M, expandable, under a more flexible EU state-aid framework) targeting EV battery tech, material recycling and autonomous/connected-vehicle R&D. Robotic/battery IP is expected to seed cross-sector growth in drones, UAS, and defense aerospace. (Note: no standalone civil AV/drone strategy; autonomy rides on PERTE VEC + industry.)
