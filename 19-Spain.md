# Spain — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Country 19 (Batch 4). OECD/EU bloc.*

Spain runs a historic, EU-financed modernization via NextGenerationEU and its "PERTEs" (Strategic Projects for Economic Recovery and Transformation), under the "España Digital 2026" umbrella. Novel institutions: SETT (a sovereign tech fund for chips) and AESIA (Europe's first national AI regulator). Nuclear is the most contested area — a legislated 2027–2035 phase-out now under intense pressure to reverse.

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** Integrated National Energy and Climate Plan 2021–2030 (PNIEC)
- **Body:** Ministry for the Ecological Transition (MITECO)
- **Year:** 2019/20 original; updated 2023–2030 (debate active early 2025)
- **URL:** https://www.miteco.gob.es (PNIEC 2023 update)
- **Summary:** Mandates a gradual phase-out of Spain's seven reactors between 2027 and 2035, replacing baseload with renewables and up to 22 GW of storage; ENRESA-managed funds (>€20B) cover dismantling and waste. But citing grid-stability concerns (highlighted by an April 2025 Iberian blackout and Spain's <3% interconnection vs. the 15% EU target), Congress passed a non-binding motion (Feb 12, 2025) urging reversal and lifetime extensions — leaving nuclear's future highly contingent. Nuclear currently provides ~20% of electricity.

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** Artificial Intelligence Strategy 2024 (Estrategia de IA 2024, updating the 2020 ENIA)
- **Body:** Ministry for Digital Transformation / Secretariat of State for Digitalization and AI (SEDIA)
- **Year:** May 2024
- **URL:** http://espanadigital.gob.es/estrategia-de-inteligencia-artificial-2024
- **Summary:** €1.5B (on top of a prior €600M) across three axes: AI levers (supercomputing/data centers), public+private adoption, and humanistic governance. Includes €90M to upgrade the MareNostrum 5 supercomputer (BSC) and — distinctively — develops **ALIA**, an open-source foundational model trained in Castilian Spanish and co-official languages (Catalan, Basque, Galician) to counter anglocentric bias. Entrenches AESIA (Europe's first national AI regulator) and promotes "Green AI."

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** Quantum Spain
- **Body:** SEDIA, coordinated by the Barcelona Supercomputing Center (BSC)
- **Year:** Launched 2021 (strategic reports 2023–24)
- **URL:** https://quantumspain-project.es
- **Summary:** >€60M state investment to build a sovereign quantum ecosystem, installing a quantum computer at BSC scaling to 20 qubits by end-2025, integrated into the Spanish Supercomputing Network (RES) and aligned with EuroHPC. Milestone: a 5-qubit chip from the Qilimanjaro-GMV consortium tested remotely with BSC. Funds quantum algorithms for AI, cybersecurity, and logistics; Spain joined the OECD Global Technology Forum. Bottlenecks remain in qubit stability and error correction.

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** PERTE para la Salud de Vanguardia (+ Complementary Plan for Biotechnology Applied to Health, PCBAS)
- **Body:** Ministry of Science, Innovation and Universities (MICIU) and Ministry of Health
- **Year:** Approved late 2021 (mid-term evaluation 2024)
- **URL:** https://planderecuperacion.gob.es
- **Summary:** ~€982M+ public-private investment to modernize healthcare via biotech and data science. Four pillars: advanced/cell therapies (CAR-T), precision genomic medicine (IMPaCT-GENóMICA), digital health/"digital twins," and domestic biotech manufacturing. The PCBAS decentralizes R&D across autonomous communities (e.g. Galicia leads national drug-screening). Builds anonymized health "data lakes" to train predictive-health AI; 46+ specialized advanced-cell-therapy entities (per AseBio).

## 5. Materials Science / Critical Minerals
- **Plan:** Roadmap for the Sustainable Management of Mineral Raw Materials (Hoja de Ruta)
- **Body:** MITECO
- **Year:** August 2022
- **URL:** https://www.miteco.gob.es (materias-primas-minerales)
- **Summary:** Aligned with the EU Critical Raw Materials Act, 46 measures to secure strategic minerals and cut dependence on China. Promotes domestic extraction (e.g. lithium in Extremadura), refining, and circular economy — aiming to raise the raw-materials circularity rate from 11.2% — including recovery of critical minerals from legacy mining tailings (escombreras). Navigates tension between extraction and local environmental opposition.

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** PERTE Chip (Strategic Project for Microelectronics and Semiconductors)
- **Body:** Ministry for Digital Transformation / Spanish Society for Technological Transformation (SETT, formerly SEMyS)
- **Year:** May 2022 (SETT established by Royal Decree July 2024)
- **URL:** https://www.pertechip.com/
- **Summary:** Spain's largest recovery project — €12.25B through 2027 for an end-to-end sovereign chip value chain. €9.35B subsidizes cutting-edge (<5nm) and mid-range (>5nm) fabs; the rest funds R&D/design, leveraging Spanish strengths in RISC-V (BSC), integrated photonics (Valencia, Vigo), and quantum chips, plus a €200M startup equity fund. SETT operates like a sovereign fund for co-investment; "Cátedras Chip" train 1,000+ specialists. Aligned with the EU Chips Act.

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** España Digital 2026
- **Body:** Ministry for Digital Transformation
- **Year:** July 2022 (updating the 2020/2025 agenda)
- **URL:** https://avance.digital.gob.es/programas-avance-digital/Paginas/Espana_Digital_2026.aspx
- **Summary:** Master framework with eight sub-plans (AI, 5G, cybersecurity, etc.) coordinating gigabit connectivity, 5G, and digital literacy. Spain already has one of Europe's strongest fiber networks and a strategic position for transatlantic/Africa subsea cables. Focus shifted to SME digitization (the "Digital Kit," subsidizing cloud/e-commerce for 98%+ SME fabric) and public-administration modernization. Cybersecurity via INCIBE, incl. a €235M CIBERINNOVA plan (€224M via Innovative Public Procurement).

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** PERTE VEC (Strategic Project for Electric and Connected Vehicles)
- **Body:** Ministry of Industry and Tourism
- **Year:** 2021 (funding tranches through 2024/25)
- **URL:** https://www.mintur.gob.es
- **Summary:** A >€4.5B industrial overhaul of Spain's auto sector (EU's 2nd-largest) toward electric, connected, and autonomous mobility — co-financing battery gigafactories, AV testing labs (Spanish Network of Labs for Autonomous & Connected Vehicles), and deep factory robotization. Anchors investment from VW/SEAT/Stellantis; firms like GKN (Vigo) tripled robotic workforces with AI/computer-vision (RobotVEC). Robotic/battery IP is expected to seed cross-sector growth in drones, UAS, and defense aerospace. (Note: no standalone civil AV/drone strategy; autonomy rides on PERTE VEC + industry.)
