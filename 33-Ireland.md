# Ireland — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Entity 33 (Batch 7). OECD/EU.*

A small, highly globalized economy that functions as a localized instantiation of EU technology sovereignty (Chips Act, CRMA, AI Act). Research funding was just consolidated: the Irish Research Council and Science Foundation Ireland merged into **Taighde Éireann – Research Ireland** (Research and Innovation Act 2024) under the **Impact 2030** agenda. Distinctive: a legal *prohibition* on nuclear power, and a deep-tech anchor in the Tyndall National Institute.

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** Electricity Regulation Act 1999 (s.18(6)) + Planning and Development Act (s.182) — legal prohibition
- **Body:** Oireachtas / Dept of Environment, Climate and Communications / Commission for Regulation of Utilities
- **Year:** 1999/2000 (active ministerial reviews 2026)
- **URL:** https://www.irishstatutebook.ie/eli/1999/act/23/enacted/en/print
- **Summary:** Electricity generation via nuclear fission is strictly prohibited by law — no state funding, targets, or plans for domestic plants. Focus is renewables toward a net-zero electricity grid by 2050. Ireland does consume nuclear power imported via UK interconnectors, and debate on repealing the ban is active (polls ~33–43% support), but officials (Min. Dooley, 2026) say a "social licence" and legislative change would need a long national conversation — nuclear is absent from current decarbonization budgets.

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** National AI Strategy "AI – Here for Good" + Refresh 2024
- **Body:** Department of Enterprise, Trade and Employment
- **Year:** July 2021 (original); November 2024 (refresh)
- **URL:** https://enterprise.gov.ie/en/publications/national-ai-strategy-refresh-2024.html
- **Summary:** Aims for Ireland as a leader in ethical, people-centered AI for productivity and public services. The 2024 refresh pivots to generative AI and full EU AI Act alignment, including a national AI regulatory sandbox and an AI Ambassador. Funds upskilling (Skillnet, Springboard+) and Research Ireland centers; targets 75% enterprise take-up of cloud/AI/big data by 2030 (AI adoption already ~91%, potentially +€250B by 2035).

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** Quantum 2030: A National Quantum Technologies Strategy for Ireland
- **Body:** Department of Further and Higher Education, Research, Innovation and Science (DFHERIS)
- **Year:** November 2023
- **URL:** https://www.gov.ie/en/department-of-further-and-higher-education-research-innovation-and-science/publications/quantum-2030-a-national-quantum-technologies-strategy-for-ireland/
- **Summary:** Aims to make Ireland an internationally competitive quantum hub by 2030, leveraging existing semiconductor and photonics strengths. Five pillars (research, talent, collaboration, innovation, awareness). Centerpiece: €100M+ to the Tyndall National Institute (UCC) to double quantum/chip experimental space; commercialization via the Disruptive Technologies Innovation Fund and Research Ireland, with IPIC (photonics) and CONNECT (networks) as academic engines.

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** Impact 2030: Ireland's Research and Innovation Strategy (via Taighde Éireann – Research Ireland)
- **Body:** DFHERIS / Taighde Éireann (Research Ireland)
- **Year:** May 2022 (Research and Innovation Act 2024)
- **URL:** https://www.gov.ie/en/publication/27c78-impact-2030-irelands-research-and-innovation-strategy/
- **Summary:** Governs biomedical/biopharma via the holistic Impact 2030 framework (no single biotech strategy). The Research and Innovation Act 2024 merged SFI and the Irish Research Council into Taighde Éireann – Research Ireland (€810M capital allocation) to streamline interdisciplinary health/tech funding. Supports 15 Research Ireland Centres, biopharma commercialization, and all-island clusters like the All-Island Cancer Research Institute (AICRI).

## 5. Materials Science / Critical Raw Materials
- **Plan:** EU Critical Raw Materials Act (national implementation) + CIRCLE project
- **Body:** Dept of Environment, Climate and Communications / EPA / Geological Survey Ireland (GSI)
- **Year:** May 2024 (CRMA in force); 2024 (CIRCLE report)
- **URL:** https://www.epa.ie/publications/research/circular-economy/
- **Summary:** Materials policy driven by the EU CRMA (securing lithium, cobalt, etc. for the twin transition). The EPA/GSI CIRCLE project built Ireland's first bespoke criticality-assessment methodology, identifying 42 raw materials critical to Irish industry. A GSI-led National Exploration Programme will map domestic CRM potential (incl. mine waste); "Single Points of Contact" streamline permitting, with a strong circular-economy/recycling emphasis.

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** Silicon Island: Ireland's National Semiconductor Strategy
- **Body:** Department of Enterprise, Trade and Employment
- **Year:** May 2025
- **URL:** https://enterprise.gov.ie/en/publications/publication-files/silicon-island-a-national-semiconductor-strategy.pdf
- **Summary:** Aligned with the EU Chips Act (Ireland hosts 15 of the top 30 global semiconductor firms; ~€13.5B chip exports/yr). Targets growing the workforce from 20,000 to 34,500 by 2040. Commits €70M+ for Tyndall's role in three EU Pilot Lines (plus IPCEI microelectronics) and explicitly seeks a leading-edge fab (regional), two trailing-edge foundries, and an advanced packaging facility. I-C3 is the national Chips Act competence center.

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** Harnessing Digital – The Digital Ireland Framework
- **Body:** Department of the Taoiseach
- **Year:** February 2022 (progress reports to Jan 2024)
- **URL:** https://www.gov.ie/en/department-of-the-taoiseach/publications/harnessing-digital-the-digital-ireland-framework/
- **Summary:** Master framework mapped to the EU Digital Decade across four pillars (enterprise, infrastructure, skills, public services). Targets: gigabit coverage to all households/businesses by 2028, 5G to all populated areas by 2030, 90% of public services online and 90% SME basic digital intensity by 2030. Funded via the National Broadband Plan and an €85M Digital Transition Fund; regulatory role via Coimisiún na Meán (Online Safety, DSA).

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** National Policy Framework for Unmanned Aircraft Systems (UAS)
- **Body:** Department of Transport
- **Year:** August 2025
- **URL:** https://www.gov.ie/en/department-of-transport/publications/national-policy-framework-for-unmanned-aircraft-systems/
- **Summary:** A blueprint to integrate drones and air mobility into low-level airspace, aligned with EU Drone Strategy 2.0 and U-space regulations (built on the success of delivery firm Manna). Three pillars: airspace/planning (IAA-led UAS Geographical Zones, a National U-space Steering Group; Dublin targeting foundational U-space in 1–3 years), compliance/enforcement (new fixed-charge offenses; IAA + Gardaí + DPC), and enterprise innovation (test beds like the SESAR-funded ÉALÚ-AER at Shannon). EU UAS services market projected at €15B/yr by 2030.
