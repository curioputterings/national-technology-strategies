# Chile — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Entity 41 (Batch 9). Latin America.*

Chile is deliberately pivoting "from the cochayuyo [seaweed] to the photon" — from raw-material extraction toward a knowledge economy — leveraging unmatched natural endowments (lithium, copper, solar radiation, dark skies). A burst of 2024–2025 policymaking is striking: a 2025 update to the National AI Policy plus the *simultaneous* December 2025 launch of 2025–2035 strategies for **Quantum** and **Biotechnology**, all funded off the fiscal leverage of the **National Lithium Strategy** and the landmark Codelco–SQM deal. A political inflection then arrived: **José Antonio Kast** (Partido Republicano) won the 14 December 2025 runoff and took office 11 March 2026, opening a more deregulatory, private-investment-led posture that is recasting both lithium governance and the AI agenda. Maturity is uneven — AI, data centers, and lithium are heavily structured and funded; semiconductors are explicitly absent. A distinctive ecosystem loop: astronomy (>50% of global infrastructure) generates data → data centers → AI/quantum computing.

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** Plan Estratégico de la CCHEN (2015–2025) / CCHEN 360°: Plan de Fortalecimiento / upcoming Plan Estratégico 2026–2030 *(no commercial nuclear power)*
- **Body:** Comisión Chilena de Energía Nuclear (CCHEN), Ministerio de Energía
- **Year:** 2017 (current plan); 2024–2025 (CCHEN 360°); 2026–2030 in co-design
- **URL:** https://www.cchen.cl/?page_id=2963
- **Summary:** CCHEN (created by Law 16.319 in 1965) runs Chile's peaceful-nuclear mandate around the RECH-1 research reactor at La Reina/Lo Aguirre. Given extreme seismicity and abundant renewables, Chile has stayed cautious on commercial nuclear power but keeps the door open: its 2017 strategic plan names six priority sectors — sustainability/food, lithium (linked to the National Lithium Strategy), human health (radioisotopes/radiopharmaceuticals), industry/mining, **"nucleoelectricity,"** and transversal capabilities. "Nucleoelectricity" signals active research and implicit openness to SMRs without a committed build. CORFO funds the CCHEN 360° modernization (connecting nuclear R&D to industrial development and technological sovereignty), and CCHEN is co-designing a 2026–2030 plan with the Laboratorio de Gobierno. It aligns with the IAEA and Ibero-American dosimetry networks.

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** Política Nacional de Inteligencia Artificial (2021; updated Decreto N°12) + Artificial Intelligence Bill + Latam-GPT
- **Body:** Ministerio de Ciencia, Tecnología, Conocimiento e Innovación (MinCiencia)
- **Year:** 2021 original; update published in the Diario Oficial 28 January 2025; AI Bill introduced May 2024 (still pending in the Senate as of mid-2026); Latam-GPT operational early 2026
- **URL:** https://www.minciencia.gob.cl/areas/inteligencia-artificial/politica-nacional-de-inteligencia-artificial/
- **Summary:** Chile leads Latin America on the CENIA Latin American AI Index. The original 2021 policy set a 10-year horizon; the explosive rise of generative AI prompted a fundamental revision (Decree No. 12, Jan 2025), built on three axes — Enabling Factors (talent, 5G infrastructure, data, plus CENIA and ANID doctoral scholarships), Development & Adoption (R&D, public/private integration), and the heavily-updated Governance & Ethics axis. The 2025 governance update used UNESCO's Readiness Assessment Methodology and citizen consultation, adding human-centric principles and addressing environmental impact, gender equity, child protection, labor, and copyright in generative models. MinCiencia reported ~78% of the policy's ~177 action items implemented by April 2025, then shifted toward mass adoption and sovereignty: Chile launched **Latam-GPT** (a CENIA-led, regional open Spanish-language large language model trained on Latin American data) and expanded the "Chile Aprende IA" training platform in early 2026. The comprehensive AI Bill (introduced May 2024, risk-tiered on the EU model) advanced past the Chamber's Science Commission but did **not** become law in 2025–26, remaining in its second constitutional procedure in the Senate. Under the incoming Kast administration (in office 11 March 2026), AI is framed as "infrastructure for development," but an April 2026 economic mega-reform proposing to let AI platforms use lawfully-published content without authorizing or paying creators drew sharp pushback from press and cultural sectors.

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** Estrategia Nacional de Tecnologías Cuánticas 2025–2035
- **Body:** MinCiencia / ANID
- **Year:** Launched December 2025 (with the UN International Year of Quantum Science)
- **URL:** https://minciencia.gob.cl/uploads/filer_public/a9/2b/a92b6e0d-40ae-48dc-9b48-2da0ba5c7585/tecnologiascuanticas.pdf
- **Summary:** Launched Dec 2025 to pre-empt technological dependency and build on Chile's deep strength in theoretical/applied physics (tied to its astronomy leadership). It rests on three pillars mirroring the AI policy: Governance/Institutional Framework (a permanent state coordinating body across state/academia/industry, including defense, security, and sovereignty), Enabling Factors (human capital from school to postdoc; inserting MSc/PhDs into tech-based firms; ANID/CORFO support for startups), and Development/Adoption (quantum sensors, quantum communications/cryptography, materials simulation for mining/lithium, and computing). Chile already hosts centers of excellence — Millennium Institutes MIRO and SAPHIR, CCTVal, CCHEN — and startups like Sequre Quantum and Maqui, and emphasizes international cooperation (Europe, US).

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** Estrategia Nacional de Biotecnología 2025–2035
- **Body:** MinCiencia (with ANID, CORFO; IDB technical assistance)
- **Year:** 17 December 2025
- **URL:** https://minciencia.gob.cl/uploads/filer_public/01/f6/01f62b43-87ad-4b5b-b763-b700249a1ea8/estrategianacionalbiotecnologia.pdf
- **Summary:** Launched alongside the Quantum Strategy under the "Del Cochayuyo al Fotón" narrative, it positions biotech as a pillar for diversification, environmental sustainability, and technological sovereignty. It modernizes Chile's trajectory (2004 National Biotech Policy → 2018 CORFO scaling push) and aims to support/scale the 200+ existing biotech firms (hubs in Metropolitan, Biobío, Valparaíso, Los Lagos). Objectives: regulatory innovation (clear IP/tech-transfer frameworks, possible European-style regulatory sandboxes); sectoral impact in public health, food security, agriculture, and notably mining/energy (bio-leaching, bioremediation); and infrastructure for advanced genetic/health research and genomic surveillance (building on post-COVID WHO guidelines). It stresses regional adoption — bridging frontier science to industry where most needed.

## 5. Materials Science / Critical Raw Materials (Lithium & Copper)
- **Plan:** Estrategia Nacional del Litio + Acuerdo de Asociación Codelco–SQM (Novandino Litio)
- **Body:** Ministerio de Minería / Ministerio de Economía / Codelco / Corfo
- **Year:** April 2023 (Strategy); May 2024 (SQM–Codelco agreement); JV constituted December 2025; protected-salars decrees January 2026
- **URL:** https://www.gob.cl/litioparachile/ ; https://acuerdocodelcosqm.cl/
- **Summary:** As a copper/lithium superpower, Chile's April 2023 National Lithium Strategy maximizes national wealth via state-led public-private partnerships: the state leads through Codelco/Enami, a dedicated National Lithium Company is to be created, a Network of Protected Salt Flats will shield ~30% of high-altitude salars, exploited flats must use low-impact tech (direct lithium extraction), and a public Lithium/Salt-Flats research institute (the **Instituto Nacional de Litio y Salares, ILiSa**, in Antofagasta) anchors the science governance. 12 prioritized areas are defined for Special Lithium Operation Contracts (CEOLs) in Tarapacá/Antofagasta, with a second phase tendering salars such as Coipasa, Ollagüe, Ascotán and Laguna Verde (Indigenous consultations closing in 2025). The landmark Codelco–SQM joint venture cleared its final hurdles in late 2025 — China's SAMR antitrust approval (November) and the Contraloría's *toma de razón* of the Corfo contracts (19 December 2025) — and the state-majority company materialized at year-end 2025 (first board late December), controlling Salar de Atacama refining through 2060 with the state capturing ~70% of operating margin (2025–2030), rising to 85% from 2031. The outgoing Boric government decreed a Red de Salares Protegidos in January 2026; the incoming Kast administration (in office 11 March 2026) pivoted sharply toward a liberalized, private-investment model, withdrawing dozens of pending environmental protection decrees from the Contraloría to accelerate extraction. These revenues fund Chile's AI/quantum/biotech transitions.

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** *Explicitly absent* — no national semiconductor/microelectronics strategy
- **Body:** N/A
- **Year:** N/A
- **URL:** N/A
- **Summary:** Unlike Brazil (which launched public funding and a "Brasil Semicon" program with Synopsys), Chile has no official strategy for domestic semiconductor manufacturing. The pragmatic consensus is that building a fab industry from scratch would be a multi-decade, monumental effort Chile is unequipped for, lacking specialized human capital and a risk-tolerant financial ecosystem (and remaining structurally extractivist). Instead its implicit strategy leverages comparative advantages: (1) supplying critical raw materials (copper, lithium) to the global chip/hardware supply chain, and (2) using cheap, abundant renewable energy to attract downstream industries that *consume* microelectronics — data centers and AI processing hubs. Chile thus sits at the very start (supplier) and very end (consumer) of the chip value chain.

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** Plan Nacional de Data Centers (PDATA) 2024–2030 + SEA environmental-evaluation criterion for data centers
- **Body:** MinCiencia / Gabinete Pro Crecimiento y Empleo (multisectoral); Servicio de Evaluación Ambiental (SEA)
- **Year:** December 2024 (PDATA); environmental criterion published January 2026
- **URL:** https://www.minciencia.gob.cl/areas/Plan-Nacional-Data-Centers/ ; https://www.sea.gob.cl
- **Summary:** Chile is emerging as Latin America's premier digital-infrastructure hub — stable climate, low-cost renewables, 62,000+ km of domestic fiber (Fibra Óptica Nacional/Austral), 69,000 km of submarine cables, and wide 5G. PDATA (Dec 2024) manages explosive growth (35 MW in 2013 → ~200 MW in 2023), with the National Electrical Coordinator projecting +270% data-center demand to ~1,207 MW by 2030, backed by ~US$4B in imminent private investment. A multi-ministry committee provides regulatory certainty to hyperscalers (AWS, Google, Microsoft) and operators (ODATA, Ascenty, Equinix) via: a geospatial tool (IDE Chile) for low-impact siting; a unified critical-permits guide; and strict sustainability mandates given chronic drought (100% renewable PPAs, advanced/liquid cooling to cut water footprint). In January 2026 the Servicio de Evaluación Ambiental (SEA) issued an official environmental-evaluation criterion specific to data-center projects, codifying water-footprint reduction (efficient/low-water cooling) and renewable-supply expectations within the SEIA permitting process. This infrastructure is the physical backbone of the National AI Policy.

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** Norma DAN 151 & DAN 91 (drones/UAS); State Policy on Astronomy (dark-skies protection)
- **Body:** Dirección General de Aeronáutica Civil (DGAC); MinCiencia (astronomy)
- **Year:** DAN 151 Ed. 3 (May 2024); Ed. 4 anticipated (ICAO harmonization)
- **URL:** https://www.dgac.gob.cl/como-operar-un-dron-en-chile/
- **Summary:** Chile has no overarching general-robotics industrial policy (robotics sits in academia/education), but drone/RPAS regulation is advanced and enforced by the DGAC via two standards: **DAN 151** (populated areas — registration, remote-pilot credential exam, JAC-approved third-party insurance, ~9 kg max takeoff weight, AOC/ROC for commercial ops) and **DAN 91** (unpopulated areas — no 9 kg cap, enabling heavy industrial/agricultural drones, critical for mining surveys and agriculture). The forthcoming DAN 151 Edition 4 harmonizes with ICAO risk-based categories (Open <120 m VLOS; Specific requiring SORA for BVLOS; Certified). Separately, **astronomy** is a decades-long state priority: prime Atacama sites, tax exemptions, and diplomatic status for international observatories (ESO, ALMA, ELT), with Chile hosting >50% of global astronomical infrastructure. Protective policy centers on dark-skies/light-pollution laws and securing observation time for Chilean scientists — whose data output feeds the PDATA and AI ecosystems.
