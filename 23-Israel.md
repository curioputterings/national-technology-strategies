# Israel — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Entity 23 (Batch 5).*

Israel is pivoting from a software-dominated "Startup Nation" toward infrastructure-heavy "deep-tech," treating quantum, bio-convergence, and AI as national-security/macroeconomic priorities. Coordination runs through the Israel Innovation Authority (IIA), the TELEM forum, and defense R&D (MAFAT). Distinctive: civil nuclear is essentially absent (strategic "deliberate ambiguity," non-NPT). A persistent theme is a "build-to-exit vs. build-to-last" tension and public-coordination/execution gaps.

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** No civil-nuclear-power plan — policy of "deliberate ambiguity" (נgרעינית) under the Israel Atomic Energy Commission (IAEC) mandate
- **Body:** Prime Minister's Office / IAEC
- **Year:** Established 1952; continuous policy
- **URL:** https://nuke.fas.org/guide/israel/agency/iaec.htm
- **Summary:** Israel operates zero commercial power reactors. Its nuclear posture is strategic deterrence under "nuclear opacity"; as a non-signatory to the NPT, standard civilian nuclear commerce and full IAEA safeguards are effectively blocked. The IAEC runs the Soreq (limited safeguards) and Negev/Dimona research centers, focused on isotopes, materials testing, and radiation safety. Past dual-purpose reactor (power + desalination) studies were shelved due to cost and the NPT blockade; the grid relies on natural gas and solar.

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** National Artificial Intelligence Program (Phase II acceleration — "Israel Will Lead the Next Generation of Innovation and Technology")
- **Body:** National AI Directorate (Prime Minister's Office), TELEM Forum, Israel Innovation Authority (IIA), Ministry of Innovation, Science and Technology (MOST)
- **Year:** 2021 (Phase 1); 2024/25 (Phase 2; Nagel Committee recommendations); government approval 16 June 2026
- **URL:** https://aiisrael.org.il/ ; https://www.gov.il/en/departments/news/ ; https://www.gov.il/BlobFolder/news/most-news20231218/en/Israel%20National%20AI%20Program%202024.pdf
- **Summary:** A multi-year, multi-billion-shekel program to close execution gaps and secure global AI standing via coordinated talent, R&D, and regulation. On 16 June 2026 the government formally approved an accelerated Phase II, now spearheaded by a new National AI Directorate inside the Prime Minister's Office, covering sovereign AI compute (including the Nebius-powered supercomputer), a national quantum computer, national R&D data repositories, updated regulation, and deep public-sector embedding. Includes NIS 1.3B (2027–2032) to subsidize 5,000+ advanced GPUs (e.g. Nvidia B200) for academia and firms, building sovereign supercomputing. Develops Hebrew/Arabic NLP models, applied-AI acceleration centers, public-sector AI, and a flexible regulatory-sandbox approach; a 2025 State Comptroller audit pressed the government to finalize a binding national AI regulatory framework. Talent pipeline fed by elite military units (e.g. 8200).

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** National Quantum Science & Technologies Program / National Quantum Initiative (+ Israeli Quantum Computing Center)
- **Body:** TELEM Forum (Ministry of Defense/MAFAT, IIA, Council for Higher Education/PBC, Finance)
- **Year:** Launched 2019/2020; Computing Center awarded 2022
- **URL:** https://www.gov.il/en/pages/iia-to-establish-israeli-quantum-computing-center-17-jul-2022
- **Summary:** ~NIS 1.25B (~$388M) for independence in quantum computing, sensing, and algorithms. Committed NIS 200M to the first applied quantum computing center (Quantum Machines + Classiq), uniquely hosting a "full stack" with three paradigms simultaneously — superconducting, cold ions, and optical — giving domestic industry/academia sovereign access (protecting IP, advancing dual-use R&D) without foreign cloud reliance.

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** National Bio-Convergence Program
- **Body:** TELEM, IIA, MOST, Ministry of Health
- **Year:** 2021/2022 (status report 2026)
- **URL:** https://innovationisrael.org.il/en/article/bio-convergence-israels-next-growth-engine/
- **Summary:** "Bio-convergence" — integrating life sciences with engineering, AI, and nanotech — positioned as Israel's "next growth engine," with NIS 1.3B committed through 2030. ~NIS 1B (public+private) injected into infrastructure, growing the sector to ~200 deep-tech startups. Builds the Bio-Devices Center (NIS 113M) and National Multi-Omics Center, and uses regulatory sandboxes (e.g. world-first approvals for cultivated dairy/beef) to commercialize biotherapeutics, AI drug discovery, and cultured meat.

## 5. Materials Science
- **Plan:** National Strategic Plan for Advanced Manufacturing + MOFET/MATX accelerators + Metamaterials MAGNET consortium
- **Body:** Ministry of Economy and Industry, IIA (Technological Infrastructure Division)
- **Year:** Advanced Manufacturing 2018; Metamaterials/MATX 2023–2025
- **URL:** https://imt.technion.ac.il/matx_accelerator/
- **Summary:** Lacking raw critical minerals, Israel leverages chemical engineering, advanced manufacturing, and urban mining (recycling). The MATX accelerator (Technion/MOFET) transitions traditional metal/ceramic/polymer factories into R&D powerhouses; ~NIS 150M funds high-risk consortiums including the Metamaterials & Metasurfaces MAGNET consortium (stealth, optics, 5G), bridging academia with defense and telecom.

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** Law for the Encouragement of Capital Investments + IIA semiconductor consortiums (silicon photonics, EU CHIPS JU) + Integrated Photonics pilot-line initiative
- **Body:** Ministry of Economy (Investment Promotion Center), IIA, Directorate of Defense R&D (DDR&D/MAFAT), Ministry of Finance
- **Year:** 1959 (reforms 2011/2013; ongoing appropriations incl. CHIPS JU 2025); Integrated Photonics initiative June 2026
- **URL:** https://www.israeltechinsider.com/p/read-my-chips-israel-emerges-as-wests ; https://innovationisrael.org.il/en/page/news-press-releases/
- **Summary:** Israel is a global chip-design/R&D hub (Intel, Nvidia, Amazon, Apple; ~45,000 employees). Strategy: move from "build-to-exit" (Mellanox→Nvidia, Mobileye→Intel) to "build-to-last" independent silicon firms. The capital-investment law grants lucrative tax breaks (as low as 6% in periphery) and capital grants to attract fabs (Intel, Tower). IIA funds fabless R&D and silicon-photonics consortiums, integrating with the EU CHIPS JU. In June 2026 the IIA and DDR&D (MAFAT) launched a grant of up to NIS 150M (covering ~55–66% of approved budget) to establish a national open-access integrated-photonics R&D and small-scale pilot-manufacturing line, accelerating design, testing, packaging, and volume manufacturing of next-generation photonic chips for academia, high-tech, and defense.

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** The National Digital Program of the Government of Israel ("Digital Israel")
- **Body:** Digital Israel Bureau (Ministry for Social Equality), National Digital Agency, Government ICT Authority
- **Year:** Established 2013; National Program 2017 (2017–2020 framework, ongoing)
- **URL:** https://www.gov.il/BlobFolder/news/digital_israel_national_plan/en/The%20National%20Digital%20Program%20of%20the%20Government%20of%20Israel.pdf
- **Summary:** A cross-ministerial program (>NIS 1.1B) to narrow a stark domestic digital divide (tech wealth concentrated in Tel Aviv; periphery, Arab and ultra-Orthodox communities lagging). Enforces "Digital by Default" across ministries, expanding fiber, telemedicine, smart classrooms, and cloud e-governance, and treats the public sector as both provider and a demand driver for domestic high-tech.

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** Israel National Drone Initiative (INDI, formerly Naama; Phase 3 / INDI3) + Smart Mobility
- **Body:** IIA, Ministry of Transport (Ayalon Highways), Civil Aviation Authority (CAAI), C4IR Israel
- **Year:** Phase 1 2019/20; Phase 2 (systemic demonstration) 2023–2026; Phase 3 (INDI3) 2026
- **URL:** https://www.c4irisrael.org/israel-national-drone-initiative
- **Summary:** A globally unprecedented regulatory sandbox designating airspace over dense urban centers (Tel Aviv, Jerusalem, Hadera) for autonomous drone testing. Develops AI-driven Uncrewed-aircraft Traffic Management (UTM) systems (High Lander, Airwayz) to coordinate hundreds of simultaneous drones without human controllers. Has run 30,000+ autonomous sorties (medical/blood deliveries, cargo, GPS-jamming tests), scaling toward urban air mobility — positioning Israel as a premier autonomous-mobility laboratory. The 2026 third phase (INDI3) moves from demonstration toward a unified national aerial-route network for dense urban logistics and heavy cargo, with UTM orchestrator Airwayz selected to coordinate high-density BVLOS operations (hundreds of weekly logistics flights across multiple operators).
