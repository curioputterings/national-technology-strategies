# South Africa — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Entity 28 (Batch 6).*

South Africa frames tech around the Fourth Industrial Revolution and the Just Energy Transition, coordinated largely by the Department of Science and Innovation (DSI) and Department of Communications and Digital Technologies (DCDT) under the **Decadal Plan on STI (2022–2032)**. Core thrust: domestic value-addition (beneficiation) of its mineral wealth, plus niche bets (quantum, compound semiconductors) leveraging existing strengths rather than competing on mass manufacturing. Constraints: energy deficit, water scarcity, and rail logistics recur across domains.

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** Integrated Resource Plan 2025 (IRP 2025) — supersedes IRP 2019 / Draft IRP 2023
- **Body:** Department of Electricity and Energy (Minister Dr. Kgosientsho Ramokgopa)
- **Year:** October 2025 (Cabinet approved 15 Oct 2025; gazetted 28 Oct 2025, Gov. Gazette No. 53596 / Notice 6767)
- **URL:** www.gov.za
- **Summary:** The finalized IRP 2025 (~R2.23 trillion) commits to adding ~105,000 MW of new generation capacity by 2039, the first plan in which clean sources (nuclear, wind, solar, hydro) are set to overtake coal. For nuclear it firms up a **5,200 MW new-build commitment** — up from the contested 2,500 MW the Draft IRP 2023 envisaged and whose procurement had been paused in Aug 2024 over consultation legal challenges. The plan continues extension of the Koeberg plant (1,860 MW — Africa's only commercial reactor) to ~2045 and pairs the build with an accompanying **Nuclear Industrial Plan** aimed not just at electricity but at resuscitating domestic expertise and localizing manufacturing across the nuclear fuel cycle for jobs and industrialization. Vendor geopolitics (e.g. Russia) and affordability remain contested.

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** National AI Policy Framework (the comprehensive Draft National AI Policy 2026 was withdrawn)
- **Body:** Department of Communications and Digital Technologies (DCDT)
- **Year:** August 2024 (Framework)
- **URL:** www.dcdt.gov.za
- **Summary:** A blueprint to use AI for growth, inequality reduction, and public services, built on twelve human-centered pillars (data sovereignty, bias mitigation, digitizing all 12 official languages). The April 2026 Draft Policy proposed a National AI Commission, AI Ethics Board, AI Regulatory Authority, Ombudsperson, and an "AI Insurance Superfund" (modeled on the Road Accident Fund) — but was withdrawn weeks later after it was found to contain AI-fabricated ("hallucinated") academic citations, leaving the sector in an interim phase under POPIA.

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** South African Quantum Technology Initiative (SA QuTI)
- **Body:** Department of Science, Technology and Innovation (DSI)
- **Year:** 2021
- **URL:** saquti.org
- **Summary:** A national consortium (Wits as hub, plus Stellenbosch, UKZN, CPUT, Zululand) deliberately "side-stepping" superpower competition — focusing on niches across quantum communication, computing, and metrology/sensing where SA has physics/optics strengths. Seed funding ~ZAR 54M (Phase 1), ~ZAR 142M projected for Phase 2; an "Emerging Leaders" program funds early-career PhDs (ZAR 1M/yr). Wits is the first African partner on the IBM Q Network (50-qubit access).

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** The Bio-economy Strategy
- **Body:** Department of Science and Innovation (DSI)
- **Year:** 2013 (active foundational policy)
- **URL:** www.dsti.gov.za
- **Summary:** Shifts from lab "technology-push" to a "market-pull" bio-economy across health, agriculture, and industry — targeting up to 5% of GDP by 2050. Health aims to localize up to 25% of pharmaceutical-ingredient imports (via programs like SHIP); agriculture pursues drought-tolerant crops and bio-fertilizers (ABIPP). Distinctively formalizes Indigenous Knowledge Systems (IKS) — commercializing traditional medicines via community "Ubuntu-inspired" benefit-sharing. Funded via the Technology Innovation Agency (TIA).

## 5. Materials Science / Critical Minerals
- **Plan:** Critical Minerals and Metals Strategy (+ Decadal Plan on STI 2022–2032)
- **Body:** Department of Mineral and Petroleum Resources (DMPR) / DSI
- **Year:** May 2025
- **URL:** www.dmpr.gov.za
- **Summary:** Pivots from raw-ore exporter to a beneficiation/advanced-manufacturing hub, prioritizing 21 critical minerals (high-criticality: PGMs, manganese, vanadium, chrome, iron ore). Six pillars (exploration, value addition, R&D/skills, infrastructure/energy, financial instruments like the Junior Mining Exploration Fund, AfCFTA regional integration). Flagship: the Hydrogen Valley corridor using domestic PGMs for fuel cells. Government cites potential for 2.3M jobs — but faces severe energy/water/rail-logistics headwinds.

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** Embedded in the Decadal Plan on STI + Digital Economy Masterplan (no standalone "chips act")
- **Body:** DSI / DCDT (CSIR Microelectronics and Nanotechnology Centre)
- **Year:** 2021 (DEMP) / 2022 (Decadal Plan)
- **URL:** www.dsti.gov.za
- **Summary:** Acknowledging it can't fund leading-edge logic fabs (power/water/capital constraints), SA targets specialized niches — compound semiconductors (GaN, SiC) for EV drivetrains, solar inverters, and power management, aligned with its automotive sector and renewable push — plus a regional packaging-and-testing hub (Durban ports) serving Africa. R&D incubated at the CSIR (Pretoria); market ~$1.85B (2025) → ~$2.55B (2030). Relies on FDI, joint ventures, and the Innovation Fund.

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** SA Connect (National Broadband Policy) Phase 2 + Digital Economy Masterplan (DEMP)
- **Body:** Department of Communications and Digital Technologies (DCDT)
- **Year:** 2013 (base); 2022 (Phase 2); 2021 (DEMP)
- **URL:** www.dcdt.gov.za
- **Summary:** Flagship infrastructure push to 100% broadband access by 2030. The accelerated Phase 2 (2023/24–2025/26) targets 5.5M households via 32,055 community Wi-Fi hotspots (≥5 Mbps) and 14,700+ government sites (≥10 Mbps). Funded via the Universal Service and Access Fund, telco spectrum social obligations (the March 2022 ICASA auction raised R14.4B), and SOEs (Broadband Infraco, Sentech, SITA). Phase 1 badly underdelivered due to budget cuts; theft/vandalism remain risks.

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** Civil Aviation Regulations (CAR) Part 101 for drones/RPAS + Draft Airfreight Strategy 2025; Automated Vehicle policy in development
- **Body:** South African Civil Aviation Authority (SACAA) / Department of Transport (DoT)
- **Year:** 2015 (CAR Part 101); 2025 (Draft Airfreight Strategy)
- **URL:** www.caa.co.za ; www.transport.gov.za
- **Summary:** A continental leader on drones — CAR Part 101 (2015) rigorously licenses commercial RPAS (RPL, ROC, RLA; VLOS limits; penalties up to R50,000 / 10 years). The 2025 Draft Airfreight Strategy treats drones as a logistics "critical enabler," proposing a mandatory "RICA-type" tracking register and dedicated BVLOS "drone corridors" with UTM. On the ground, the National Road Traffic Act still requires a human driver (only Level 1 autonomy permitted); the DoT aims to finalize AV regulations by ~2027.
