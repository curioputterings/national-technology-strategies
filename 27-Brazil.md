# Brazil — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Entity 27 (Batch 6).*

Brazil is executing an aggressive "neo-industrialization" anchored by **Nova Indústria Brasil (NIB)** — ~R$300B in financing (BNDES, Finep, Embrapii) across six missions to 2033 — with technological sovereignty as the recurring motif (e.g. reversing the CEITEC chip privatization, R$23B AI plan). Financing engines: BNDES (industrial scaling), Finep/CNPq (research), and aggressive fiscal incentives (PADIS, Mover).

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** Brazilian Nuclear Policy / Brazilian Nuclear Program (PNB)
- **Body:** Presidency / Institutional Security Cabinet (GSI) / MME / MCTI / National Nuclear Energy Commission (CNEN)
- **Year:** 2018 (Decree 9.600/2018)
- **URL:** http://www.planalto.gov.br/ccivil_03/_ato2015-2018/2018/decreto/d9600.htm
- **Summary:** Directs peaceful, autonomous mastery of the full nuclear fuel cycle (including uranium enrichment) under a constitutional state monopoly. Nuclear is ~3% of the matrix (Angra 1 & 2). Goals: complete the long-delayed Angra 3; build the Brazilian Multipurpose Reactor (RMB) for radioisotope self-sufficiency; and fund the Navy's LABGENE propulsion lab for a nuclear-powered submarine (PROSUB). On 1 Oct 2025 the National Energy Policy Council (CNPE) resolved to have Eletronuclear and BNDES refresh the economic-financial and technical studies needed to finish Angra 3; the studies concluded in late October 2025. As of April 2026 the final resumption/tariff decision remained pending (a collective review request stalled the CNPE vote, and a TCU audit flagged budget inconsistencies) — completion stays financially contentious.

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** Plano Brasileiro de Inteligência Artificial (PBIA) 2024–2028 — "IA para o Bem de Todos"
- **Body:** MCTI / National Council of Science and Technology
- **Year:** 2024
- **URL:** https://www.gov.br/mcti/pt-br/acompanhe-o-mcti/transformacaodigital/plano-brasileiro-de-inteligencia-artificial
- **Summary:** R$23B over four years to make Brazil a reference in sustainable/inclusive AI. Headline target: a top-5-globally supercomputer powered by renewable energy. Develops advanced Portuguese-language models for digital sovereignty, requalifies 200,000+ workers, and dedicates 60% of investment to business/industrial innovation (linked to LNCC's AI Institute). Through 2025–2026 implementation accelerated: LNCC's Santos Dumont machine was expanded and a multi-city contest (Petrópolis, Campinas) emerged to host the new flagship AI supercomputer, while Decree 12.912/2026 authorized a Finep capital increase of up to R$3.5B for sovereign-AI infrastructure. The horizontal AI-regulation bill (Marco Legal da IA, PL 2338/2023) — Senate-approved December 2024 — remained unsanctioned, still pending a final Chamber of Deputies vote as of mid-2026; experts note R$23B is modest vs. US/China.

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** Iniciativa Brasileira para Tecnologias Quânticas (IBQuântica) — Portaria MCTI Nº 8.194
- **Body:** MCTI / Finep
- **Year:** 2024
- **URL:** https://antigo.mctic.gov.br/mctic/opencms/legislacao/portarias/Portaria_MCTI_n_8194_de_19052024.html
- **Summary:** Targets sovereignty in quantum computing, communication, and sensing — up to R$5B by 2034, starting with a R$300M Finep subvention edict. Flagship: the International Center for Quantum Computing in Paraíba (Ciquanta, ~R$150M) hosting Brazil's first 20- and 100-qubit machines. Builds on SENAI's NMR educational computer and CBPF's Quantum Technologies Lab (superconducting qubits, diamond sensors); explicitly aims to combat brain drain.

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** Genomas Brasil (National Program for Genomics and Precision Health) + NIB Mission 2 (Health Economic-Industrial Complex)
- **Body:** Ministry of Health (DECIT) / MCTI
- **Year:** 2020 (Ordinance GM/MS 1,949); redesigned under NIB (Decree 11,715/2023)
- **URL:** https://www.gov.br/saude/pt-br/composicao/sectics/decit/genomas-brasil
- **Summary:** Builds precision public health into the universal SUS system by linking clinical data with genetic profiles. Core target: sequence 100,000 complete genomes to map Brazil's highly admixed population (existing reference data skews to other populations). Aligned with NIB's goal of 70% domestic production of medicines/vaccines/devices by 2033; funds advanced (cell/gene) therapies and projects like Onco-Genomas Brasil.

## 5. Materials Science / Strategic Minerals
- **Plan:** National Policy for Strategic Minerals (Decree 10.657/2021) → National Policy for Critical & Strategic Minerals (PNMCE, PL 2.780/2024)
- **Body:** Ministry of Mines and Energy (MME) / Investment Partnerships Program (PPI) / National Congress
- **Year:** 2021; PNMCE approved by Chamber of Deputies 6 May 2026 (pending Senate)
- **URL:** https://www.camara.leg.br/proposicoesWeb/fichadetramitacao?idProposicao=2447259
- **Summary:** Secures Brazil's position in critical-mineral supply chains (rare earths, lithium, niobium, graphite), streamlining environmental licensing for priority projects and moving up from raw-ore export toward domestic beneficiation/transformation feeding semiconductors, energy, and defense. On 6 May 2026 the Chamber of Deputies passed PL 2.780/2024 instituting the Política Nacional de Minerais Críticos e Estratégicos (PNMCE), sending it to the Senate; it creates the Mineral Activity Guarantee Fund (Fgam, ~R$2B federal backing) and ~R$5B in fiscal credits over five years for firms that process critical minerals on Brazilian soil, plus a 0.5%-of-revenue levy split between Fgam and R&D. The federal government tied any bilateral critical-minerals deal to passing this domestic framework first; frustrated by delays, the US in March 2026 signed subnational MOUs directly with the states of Goiás (rare earths) and Minas Gerais (lithium), triggering an MDIC jurisdictional pushback, while USA Rare Earth moved to acquire Goiás producer Serra Verde.

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** Programa Brasil Semicondutores (Brasil Semicon) — Law 14.968/2024, modernizing/extending PADIS; + re-establishment of CEITEC
- **Body:** Presidency / Ministry of Development, Industry, Commerce and Services (MDIC) / MCTI
- **Year:** 2024 (Law 14.968 of 11 Sep 2024; in force 1 Jan 2025)
- **URL:** https://www.planalto.gov.br/ccivil_03/_ato2023-2026/2024/Lei/L14968.htm
- **Summary:** Law 14.968/2024 created the Programa Brasil Semicondutores (Brasil Semicon) as an overarching structuring initiative and substantially modernized PADIS (originally Law 11.484/2007), which offers zero-rated federal taxes for semiconductor/display R&D and manufacturing. The reform widened eligibility to services such as IC design/software, dropped the prior government list for incentivized input imports, and re-based financial credits on a firm's total revenue; the program entered into force on 1 Jan 2025 and was operationalized through 2026 (e.g. interministerial Portaria nº 162 of 22 Jan 2026 updating the Basic Productive Process). The government also reversed the planned privatization/liquidation of CEITEC — Latin America's only commercial IC foundry — repositioning it as a sovereignty anchor (it championed the agenda at SemiCon-LAC 2026). Brazil still faces major capex/competition hurdles to climb the value chain.

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** E-Digital (Brazilian Strategy for Digital Transformation)
- **Body:** MCTI (interministerial)
- **Year:** 2018 (Decree 9.319); updated Decree 10.782/2022; new 2026–2031 cycle in consultation (Feb 2026)
- **URL:** https://www.gov.br/mcti/pt-br/acompanhe-o-mcti/transformacaodigital/estrategia-digital
- **Summary:** Master policy harmonizing federal digital initiatives across enabling axes (infrastructure, R&D, cyber-trust, education) and transformation axes (data economy, IoT, digital government), targeting digitization of 90% of industries. Legal basis for the National System for Digital Transformation (SinDigital). On 10 Feb 2026 the government (Casa Civil / interministerial CITDigital / MGI) opened a public consultation via the Brasil Participativo platform (running to April 2026, with a "Futuro Digital" seminar on 9–10 Apr 2026) to build the new 2026–2031 E-Digital cycle as the umbrella plan articulating the PBIA AI plan and the National Cybersecurity Strategy around digital sovereignty. The TCU audit court has critiqued implementation depth.

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** Programa Mover (Mobilidade Verde e Inovação)
- **Body:** MDIC
- **Year:** 2024 (Law 14.902/2024; Decree 12.435/2025)
- **URL:** https://www.gov.br/mdic/pt-br/assuntos/sdic/setor-automotivo/programa-mover
- **Summary:** R$19.3B in credits to 2028 to decarbonize and modernize the auto/logistics sector (successor to Rota 2030). For autonomy, mandates the "InTec" index requiring ADAS (automatic emergency braking, lane-departure, stability control) in all new vehicles by 2027 — the building blocks for full autonomy. Links large R&D tax benefits (50–320% return) to "well-to-wheel" efficiency and connectivity (favoring Brazil's ethanol-hybrid matrix); spurred R$100B+ in announced investment.
