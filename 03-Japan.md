# Japan — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Country 3 of 5 (Batch 1). OECD bloc.*

Japan's posture has shifted to techno-nationalism tied to economic security, with unprecedented state subsidies (esp. semiconductors and biotech). Demographic decline (hyper-aging, labor shortages) is a primary catalyst for automation/AI/DX. The Cabinet Office's "Integrated Innovation" paradigm cross-links AI, semiconductors, materials, and energy.

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** Seventh Strategic Energy Plan (第7次エネルギー基本計画)
- **Body:** METI / Cabinet Office
- **Year:** Drafted Dec 2024; Cabinet Decision Feb 2025
- **URL:** https://www.enecho.meti.go.jp/category/others/basic_plan/pdf/2025_strategic_energy_plan.pdf
- **Summary:** Aims for carbon neutrality by 2050 and a 73% GHG cut by FY2040 under the S+3E principles. Targets nuclear at ~20% of the energy mix by 2040 (from 8.5% in 2023), requiring restarts of idle reactors plus next-gen SMRs and fast reactors. Funds safety frameworks, international R&D (NuScale, GE Hitachi BWRX-300) via NEXIP, and GX supply chains. The 20% target is seen as highly challenging — nearly all 36 operable reactors would need to be online.

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** Integrated Innovation Strategy 2024 (統合イノベーション戦略) + AI Guidelines for Business (AI事業者ガイドライン)
- **Body:** Cabinet Office (Integrated Innovation Strategy Promotion Council) / METI / MIC
- **Year:** June 2024 (strategy); April 2024 (guidelines)
- **URL:** https://www8.cao.go.jp/cstp/tougosenryaku/togo2024_honbun_eiyaku.pdf
- **Summary:** Elevates AI to a "key technology," aiming for a virtuous cycle of generative-AI innovation with risk mitigation. Commits subsidies (via Economic Security Promotion Act) for domestic compute and sovereign foundation models trained on Japanese data. Uses the non-binding AI Guidelines for Business as "soft law" while an AI Institutional Research Group studies harder rules. Positions Japan as a global mediator (Hiroshima AI Process) between EU and US approaches.

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** Quantum Future Society Vision (量子未来社会ビジョン) + Quantum Future Industry Creation Strategy
- **Body:** Cabinet Office
- **Year:** April 2022 (Vision); April 2023 (Industry strategy); April 2024 (measures)
- **URL:** https://www8.cao.go.jp/cstp/english/strategy_2022.pdf
- **Summary:** Roadmap to embed quantum computing, sensing, and cryptography into society, with a long-term goal of a fault-tolerant general-purpose quantum computer by 2050. Funded via the Moonshot R&D Program and MEXT's Q-LEAP. Pragmatically commercializes near-term quantum annealing (AIST, NEDO, NEC) and builds quantum-security testbeds (NICT) against "Q-Day." Invests in quantum-literate workforce (e.g. QA4U).

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** Bioeconomy Strategy (バイオエコノミー戦略)
- **Body:** Cabinet Office
- **Year:** June 2024 (update of 2019/2020 Bio Strategy)
- **URL:** https://www8.cao.go.jp/cstp/bio/bio_economy_en.pdf
- **Summary:** Renamed from "Bio Strategy" to signal a shift from basic research to commercialization, targeting a 100 trillion JPY (~$694B) bioeconomy market by 2030. Five market areas including biomanufacturing, regenerative medicine, and sustainable primary production. ~1 trillion JPY mobilized since FY2022 (incl. 300B JPY to NEDO, 2023–2032) for biofoundries; fosters regional "biocommunities" (Greater Tokyo, Kansai BiocK) to rival Boston/Cambridge.

## 5. Materials Science
- **Plan:** Materials Innovation Strategy (マテリアル革新力強化戦略)
- **Body:** Cabinet Office
- **Year:** April 2021 (2024/2025 expert-meeting updates)
- **URL:** https://www8.cao.go.jp/cstp/material/material_honbun_en.pdf ; https://www8.cao.go.jp/cstp/material/matstrategy2025.pdf
- **Summary:** Protects Japan's dominance in specialty materials (photoresists, advanced polymers, alloys), treating them as enablers of AI/semiconductors/quantum. Core mechanism is a "Knowledge Value Chain" with shared data platforms accelerating Materials Informatics (AI-driven discovery). Targets economic security via supply-chain diversification, rare-metal recycling ("urban mining"), and a circular economy (e.g. 100% plastics reuse/recycling by 2035).

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** Strategy for Semiconductors and the Digital Industry (半導体・デジタル産業戦略)
- **Body:** METI
- **Year:** June 2021 (revised June 2023)
- **URL:** https://www.meti.go.jp/english/press/2023/0606001.html ; https://www.meti.go.jp/english/press/2021/pdf/0604_005a.pdf
- **Summary:** Aims to revive Japan's chip base (share fell from ~50% in the 1980s to ~10% by 2020), targeting 15 trillion JPY (~$100B) domestic sales by 2030. Three steps: (1) secure domestic production via $13B+ subsidies attracting TSMC to Kumamoto; (2) next-gen via state-backed Rapidus targeting 2nm mass production by 2027 with IBM/imec; (3) future tech like photoelectric fusion for low-power data centers. Phase 2 is widely viewed as technologically ambitious (skipping multiple node generations).

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** Priority Policy Program for Realizing a Digital Society (デジタル社会の実現に向けた重点計画)
- **Body:** Digital Agency (デジタル庁) / Cabinet
- **Year:** June 2023 (updated annually)
- **URL:** https://www5.cao.go.jp/keizai-shimon/kaigi/cabinet/honebuto/2023/2023_basicpolicies_en.pdf
- **Summary:** Operating framework for the Digital Agency to digitize public administration and counter demographic decline. Targets ubiquitous "My Number" digital ID, standardized local-government systems by 2025, and the "Digital Garden City Nation" infrastructure (5G, data centers, fiber; 5.7T+ JPY). Applies "Digital 3 Principles" and builds cross-sectoral data linkage (Society 5.0). Uniquely embraces Web 3.0 (stablecoins, NFTs, DAOs).

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** Mobility DX Strategy (モビリティDX戦略) + RoAD to the L4 Project
- **Body:** METI / MLIT
- **Year:** May 2024 (Mobility DX, updated June 2025); 2021–2025 (RoAD to the L4)
- **URL:** https://www.mobilitydx.go.jp/en/about/ ; https://www.road-to-the-l4.go.jp/en/
- **Summary:** Targets 30% global market share for Japanese Software-Defined Vehicles (SDVs) by 2030/2035, with subsidies for automotive semiconductors, simulation, and the "Ouranos Ecosystem" data infrastructure. RoAD to the L4 funds Level 4 deployment — driverless services in 40+ municipalities by 2025 and L4 trucking on the Shin-Tomei Expressway by 2026 — directly addressing the logistics "2024 Problem" and rural depopulation. Built on the 2022 Road Traffic Act amendment permitting L4.
