# Japan — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Country 3 of 5 (Batch 1). OECD bloc.*

Japan's posture has shifted to techno-nationalism tied to economic security, with unprecedented state subsidies (esp. semiconductors and biotech). Demographic decline (hyper-aging, labor shortages) is a primary catalyst for automation/AI/DX. The Cabinet Office's "Integrated Innovation" paradigm cross-links AI, semiconductors, materials, and energy.

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** Seventh Strategic Energy Plan (第7次エネルギー基本計画)
- **Body:** METI / Cabinet Office
- **Year:** Drafted Dec 2024; Cabinet Decision Feb 2025
- **URL:** https://www.enecho.meti.go.jp/category/others/basic_plan/pdf/2025_strategic_energy_plan.pdf
- **Summary:** Aims for carbon neutrality by 2050 and a 73% GHG cut by FY2040 under the S+3E principles. Targets nuclear at ~20% of the energy mix by 2040 (from 8.5% in 2023), requiring restarts of idle reactors plus next-gen SMRs and fast reactors. Funds safety frameworks, international R&D (NuScale, GE Hitachi BWRX-300) via NEXIP, and GX supply chains. The 20% target is seen as highly challenging — nearly all 36 operable reactors would need to be online.

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** AI Promotion Act (AI法 / 人工知能関連技術の研究開発及び活用の推進に関する法律) + AI Basic Plan (人工知能基本計画), set above the Integrated Innovation Strategy 2025 and the AI Guidelines for Business
- **Body:** Cabinet Office — AI Strategy Headquarters (PM-led) / METI / MIC
- **Year:** Act promulgated June 2025, in force Sept 2025; AI Basic Plan Cabinet Decision Dec 2025; Integrated Innovation Strategy 2025 (June 2025)
- **URL:** https://www8.cao.go.jp/cstp/ai/ ; https://laws.e-gov.go.jp/law/507AC0000000053
- **Summary:** The AI Promotion Act is Japan's first framework law dedicated to AI — a deliberately "promotion-first" (soft-law) counterpoint to the penalty-heavy EU AI Act, relying on existing statutes and government guidance for risk while establishing a PM-led AI Strategy Headquarters and mandating an AI Basic Plan. The Basic Plan (Cabinet Decision 23 Dec 2025) sets the goal of making Japan "the world's most AI-friendly country for development and utilization," built on four pillars: use AI, create AI (securing domestic compute, data, and talent), enhance AI reliability/governance (via the AI Safety Institute and Hiroshima AI Process), and foster an AI-collaborating workforce. A flagship measure is the government-wide generative-AI system "Gennai" (源内), rolled out to 100,000+ civil servants from May 2026. The Integrated Innovation Strategy 2025 (June 2025), the final annual plan under the 6th S&T Basic Plan, cross-links AI with quantum, fusion, and semiconductors and frames the labor shortage as the core driver. Commits continued subsidies (via the Economic Security Promotion Act) for sovereign foundation models trained on Japanese data.

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** Quantum Future Society Vision (量子未来社会ビジョン) + Quantum Ecosystem Construction Promotion Measures (量子エコシステム構築に向けた推進方策)
- **Body:** Cabinet Office (Integrated Innovation Strategy Promotion Council)
- **Year:** April 2022 (Vision); April 2023 (Industry strategy); May 2025 (latest ecosystem promotion measures)
- **URL:** https://www8.cao.go.jp/cstp/english/strategy_2022.pdf ; https://www8.cao.go.jp/cstp/ryoshigijutsu/ryoshigijutsu.html
- **Summary:** Roadmap to embed quantum computing, sensing, and cryptography into society, with a long-term goal of a fault-tolerant general-purpose quantum computer by 2050. The May 2025 ecosystem-construction promotion measures are the most recent government-wide policy addendum, pivoting toward ecosystem building, international partnerships, and supply chains. Funded via the Moonshot R&D Program and MEXT's Q-LEAP. Pragmatically commercializes near-term quantum annealing (AIST, NEDO, NEC) and builds quantum-security testbeds (NICT) against "Q-Day." Invests in a quantum-literate workforce (e.g. QA4U; a Q-STAR "Quantum Literacy Standard ver 1.0" appeared in Feb 2026).

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** Bioeconomy Strategy (バイオエコノミー戦略)
- **Body:** Cabinet Office
- **Year:** June 2024 (update of 2019/2020 Bio Strategy)
- **URL:** https://www8.cao.go.jp/cstp/bio/bio_economy_en.pdf
- **Summary:** Renamed from "Bio Strategy" to signal a shift from basic research to commercialization, targeting a 100 trillion JPY (~$694B) bioeconomy market by 2030. Five market areas including biomanufacturing, regenerative medicine, and sustainable primary production. ~1 trillion JPY mobilized since FY2022 (incl. 300B JPY to NEDO, 2023–2032) for biofoundries; fosters regional "biocommunities" (Greater Tokyo, Kansai BiocK) to rival Boston/Cambridge.

## 5. Materials Science
- **Plan:** Materials Innovation Strategy (マテリアル革新力強化戦略) + Promotion Measures (推進方策)
- **Body:** Cabinet Office (Integrated Innovation Strategy Promotion Council)
- **Year:** April 2021 (strategy; revised June 2025); June 2026 (latest promotion measures)
- **URL:** https://www8.cao.go.jp/cstp/material/material_honbun_en.pdf ; https://www8.cao.go.jp/cstp/material/material.html
- **Summary:** Protects Japan's dominance in specialty materials (photoresists, advanced polymers, alloys), treating them as enablers of AI/semiconductors/quantum. The June 2026 promotion measures sharpen the focus on "AI for Materials," standing up autonomous self-driving labs (closed-loop synthesis plus AI analysis, led by NIMS and AIST) with the goal of speeding up materials development roughly tenfold. The core mechanism remains a "Knowledge Value Chain" with shared data platforms accelerating Materials Informatics. Targets economic security via supply-chain diversification, rare-metal recycling ("urban mining"), and a circular economy (e.g. 100% plastics reuse/recycling by 2035).

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** Strategy for Semiconductors and the Digital Industry (半導体・デジタル産業戦略)
- **Body:** METI
- **Year:** June 2021 (revised June 2023)
- **URL:** https://www.meti.go.jp/english/press/2023/0606001.html ; https://www.meti.go.jp/policy/mono_info_service/joho/kondankai/handotai_digital/
- **Summary:** Aims to revive Japan's chip base (share fell from ~50% in the 1980s to ~10% by 2020), targeting 15 trillion JPY (~$100B) domestic sales by 2030. Three steps: (1) secure domestic production via $13B+ subsidies attracting TSMC to Kumamoto; (2) next-gen via state-backed Rapidus targeting 2nm mass production by 2027 with IBM/imec; (3) future tech like photoelectric fusion for low-power data centers. A 2026 revision is underway — a skeleton draft was presented on 18 Mar 2026 (formal adoption targeted for summer 2026) that pivots heavily to "Physical AI" (AI controlling robotics, autonomous vehicles, and manufacturing), AI-ready data infrastructure, and stretch targets of a 30% global Physical-AI share and 40 trillion JPY domestic semiconductor sales by 2040. Phase 2 is widely viewed as technologically ambitious (skipping multiple node generations).

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** Priority Policy Program for Realizing a Digital Society (デジタル社会の実現に向けた重点計画)
- **Body:** Digital Agency (デジタル庁) / Cabinet
- **Year:** June 2025 (Cabinet Decision 13 June 2025; updated annually)
- **URL:** https://www.digital.go.jp/policies/priority-policy-program
- **Summary:** Operating framework for the Digital Agency to digitize public administration and counter demographic decline. The 2025 edition sets aggressive timelines for nationwide AI social implementation in local governments, targets integrating the My Number card with foreign residence cards by FY2026, and pushes to eliminate remaining analog regulatory roadblocks. Continues ubiquitous "My Number" digital ID, standardized local-government systems, and the "Digital Garden City Nation" infrastructure (5G, data centers, fiber). Applies "Digital 3 Principles" and builds cross-sectoral data linkage (Society 5.0); uniquely embraces Web 3.0 (stablecoins, NFTs, DAOs).

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** Mobility DX Strategy (モビリティDX戦略) + RoAD to the L4 Project
- **Body:** METI / MLIT
- **Year:** May 2024 (Mobility DX, updated June 2025); 2021–2025 (RoAD to the L4)
- **URL:** https://www.mobilitydx.go.jp/en/about/ ; https://www.road-to-the-l4.go.jp/en/
- **Summary:** Targets 30% global market share for Japanese Software-Defined Vehicles (SDVs) by 2030/2035, with subsidies for automotive semiconductors, simulation, and the "Ouranos Ecosystem" data infrastructure. RoAD to the L4 funds Level 4 deployment — driverless services in 40+ municipalities by 2025 and L4 trucking on the Shin-Tomei Expressway by 2026 — directly addressing the logistics "2024 Problem" and rural depopulation. Built on the 2022 Road Traffic Act amendment permitting L4.
