# Singapore — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Country 5 of 6 (Batch 1). ASEAN bloc.*

Singapore's tech strategy is highly centralized and well-funded, anchored by the **Research, Innovation and Enterprise (RIE) 2025** plan — a S$25B budget (~1% of GDP) that funds most domains. Statutory boards (A*STAR, NRF, IMDA, EDB, EMA, LTA) deploy sector blueprints beneath it. Key pivots: semiconductor translation (NSTIC), economy-wide AI (NAIS 2.0), and serious SMR nuclear feasibility studies.

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** Energy 2050 Committee Report + "Building Singapore's Capabilities to Assess Nuclear Energy"
- **Body:** Energy Market Authority (EMA)
- **Year:** 2022 (report); 2024–2025 (nuclear feasibility/tenders)
- **URL:** https://www.ema.gov.sg/resources/industry-reports/energy-2050-committee-report ; https://www.ema.gov.sg/resources/corporate-publications/building-sg-capabilities-to-assess-nuclear-energy
- **Summary:** Evaluates advanced nuclear (esp. SMRs) as a pathway to net-zero by 2050, given ~95% reliance on imported gas and land limits capping solar at ~10% of 2050 demand. Not yet committed to deployment; funding feasibility studies (Mott MacDonald appointed late 2025) to assess SMR safety and commercial readiness. Building indigenous capability and partnerships, including a US "123 Agreement" (July 2024) and MOUs with Battelle and Idaho National Laboratory (2025).

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** National AI Strategy 2.0 (NAIS 2.0)
- **Body:** Smart Nation Group / National AI Council / IMDA
- **Year:** December 2023 (update issued 2026)
- **URL:** https://www.smartnation.gov.sg/initiatives/national-ai-strategy/
- **Summary:** Shifts from isolated pilots to economy-wide AI integration ("AI for the Public Good"). Funding envelope >S$1B for national compute, talent, and AI Centres of Excellence across nine priority sectors (healthcare, finance, transport, smart cities). Includes a S$150M Enterprise Compute Initiative; governance via IMDA's AI Verify framework and Project Moonshot (open-source LLM evaluation/red-teaming). Governed by the National AI Council chaired by the PM.

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** National Quantum Strategy (NQS)
- **Body:** NRF / National Quantum Office (hosted by A*STAR)
- **Year:** May 2024
- **URL:** https://nqo.sg/nqs/ ; https://www.cqt.sg/highlight/2024-05-singapore-national-quantum-strategy/
- **Summary:** ~S$300M (under RIE2025) to make Singapore a global quantum hub. Four thrusts: scientific excellence (elevating CQT to a flagship national center), engineering translation (QEP 3.0, National Quantum Processor Initiative, National Quantum Federated Foundry), talent (National Quantum Scholarships — 100 PhD + 100 Master), and enterprise. Deploys the National Quantum-Safe Network (Plus) — SE Asia's first quantum-safe network infrastructure.

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** RIE2025 — "Human Health and Potential" (HHP) domain
- **Body:** NRF / Ministry of Health / A*STAR
- **Year:** December 2020 (2021–2025)
- **URL:** https://www.nrf.gov.sg/rie2025-plan
- **Summary:** Expanded the biomedical domain into "Human Health and Potential" to tackle aging and low birth rates. Funded from the S$25B RIE2025 budget; prioritizes early childhood development, healthy longevity, and the National Precision Medicine Programme (mapping the population genome for Asian-phenotype-targeted therapies). The PREPARE programme funds epidemic preparedness post-COVID. ~S$500M earmarked for biomedical R&D infrastructure under RIE2030.

## 5. Materials Science
- **Plan:** RIE2025 — Manufacturing, Trade and Connectivity (MTC) domain, Advanced Materials sector
- **Body:** NRF / A*STAR
- **Year:** December 2020 (2021–2025; criteria updated 2025)
- **URL:** https://www.a-star.edu.sg (IAF-PP MTC grant materials)
- **Summary:** Applied materials R&D feeding aerospace, semiconductor, and automotive supply chains via the Industry Alignment Fund (IAF-PP / IAF-ICP). Targets continuous fibre-reinforced polymers, metallic additive materials, and high-entropy metals. Hallmark "Corporate Lab" model (e.g. Applied Materials-NUS Advanced Materials Corporate Lab, Phase 2 from late 2024; AUMOVIO/Continental-NTU) co-funds MNC R&D to de-risk discovery and embed local expertise.

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** RIE Flagship in Semiconductors + Manufacturing 2030 Vision
- **Body:** EDB / A*STAR / MTI
- **Year:** 2021 (Manufacturing 2030); 2024–2025 (Flagship, NSTIC)
- **URL:** https://www.edb.gov.sg/en/our-industries/semiconductor.html ; https://nstic.sg/
- **Summary:** Singapore holds ~10% of global chip output and ~20% of semiconductor equipment manufacturing (~6% of GDP). Rather than a subsidy arms race, a targeted S$800M RIE Flagship focuses on advanced packaging, heterogeneous integration, silicon photonics, and wide-bandgap (GaN). A*STAR invests S$500M in the NSTIC R&D Fab (JTC nanoSpace @ Tampines, opening 2027; 8"/12" wafers) plus NSTIC (GaN), launched June 2025. Addresses talent via ITE/polytechnic curricula and the Shortage Occupation List.

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** Smart Nation 2.0 + Digital Connectivity Blueprint (DCB)
- **Body:** Smart Nation Group / IMDA / Ministry of Digital Development and Information (MDDI)
- **Year:** June 2023 (DCB); October 2024 (Smart Nation 2.0)
- **URL:** https://file.go.gov.sg/smartnation2-report.pdf ; https://www.imda.gov.sg/how-we-can-help/digital-connectivity-blueprint
- **Summary:** Three pillars — Trust, Growth, Community. DCB commits to seamless 10 Gbps domestic connectivity, doubling submarine cable capacity over a decade, Wi-Fi 6E / 5G Standalone spectrum, and a green-data-center standard for tropical climates. The forthcoming Digital Infrastructure Act would regulate cloud/data-center operators as critical infrastructure (resilience, concentration risk, baseline security). Builds the Singapore Digital Utility Stack for secure e-transactions.

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** National Robotics Programme (NRP) + LTA Autonomous Vehicles / Smart Mobility
- **Body:** A*STAR / NRF (robotics); LTA / MOT (mobility)
- **Year:** 2016 (NRP); 2024 (S$60M RoboCluster); NRP 2.0 anticipated 2026
- **URL:** https://www.lta.gov.sg/content/ltagov/en/industry_innovations/technologies/autonomous_vehicles.html
- **Summary:** Automation driven by labor constraints and aging. NRP (>S$450M historical funding + S$60M 2024 "RoboCluster") accelerates robotics in facilities management, manufacturing, and healthcare; produced the open-source Robotics Middleware Framework (Open-RMF). Domestic robotics market projected at S$2.1B by 2026. For AVs, the CETRAN test circuit (LTA-NTU) gates public-road deployment; fixed-route autonomous shuttles/buses operate in Punggol (expanding to Tengah), with safety operators on board and worker reskilling into AV remote-operator/fleet-tech roles.
