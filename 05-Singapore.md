# Singapore — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Country 5 of 6 (Batch 1). ASEAN bloc.*

Singapore's tech strategy is highly centralized and well-funded, now anchored by the **Research, Innovation and Enterprise (RIE) 2030** plan — a record S$37B budget (~1% of GDP, a 32% jump over RIE2025) endorsed at the 15th RIEC meeting on 5 December 2025 and committed at Budget 2026 (12 February 2026). RIE2030 succeeds the S$25B RIE2025 and funds most domains via thematic plans and RIE Flagships (AI, advanced semiconductor packaging, quantum, decarbonisation). Statutory boards (A*STAR, NRF, IMDA, EDB, EMA, LTA) deploy sector blueprints beneath it. Key 2025–26 pivots: a refreshed National AI Strategy (Update to NAIS, May 2026) plus a new National AI Council, the RIE2030 Quantum Thematic Plan, a forthcoming Digital Infrastructure Act, an AV legislative framework, and SMR nuclear feasibility studies (Mott MacDonald appointed Sept 2025).

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** Energy 2050 Committee Report + "Building Singapore's Capabilities to Assess Nuclear Energy"
- **Body:** Energy Market Authority (EMA)
- **Year:** 2022 (report); 2024–2025 (nuclear feasibility/tenders)
- **URL:** https://www.ema.gov.sg/resources/industry-reports/energy-2050-committee-report ; https://www.ema.gov.sg/resources/corporate-publications/building-sg-capabilities-to-assess-nuclear-energy
- **Summary:** Evaluates advanced nuclear (esp. SMRs) as a pathway to net-zero by 2050, given ~95% reliance on imported gas and land limits capping solar at ~10% of 2050 demand. Not yet committed to deployment; funding feasibility studies (Mott MacDonald appointed late 2025) to assess SMR safety and commercial readiness. Building indigenous capability and partnerships, including a US "123 Agreement" (July 2024) and MOUs with Battelle and Idaho National Laboratory (2025).

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** National AI Strategy 2.0 (NAIS 2.0) + Update to NAIS (2026)
- **Body:** Smart Nation Group / National AI Council / MDDI / IMDA
- **Year:** December 2023 (NAIS 2.0); updated May 2026
- **URL:** https://www.smartnation.gov.sg/initiatives/national-ai-strategy/
- **Summary:** Shifts from isolated pilots to economy-wide AI integration ("AI for the Public Good"). Funding envelope >S$1B for national compute, talent, and AI Centres of Excellence across nine priority sectors (healthcare, finance, transport, smart cities); the National AI R&D (NAIRD) Plan was refreshed in January 2026. Budget 2026 (February) established the National AI Council (NAIC), chaired by PM Lawrence Wong, to set strategic direction and commission targeted AI missions. On 20 May 2026, MDDI Minister Josephine Teo released the "Update to NAIS (2026)" at the ATxSummit, setting 10 refreshed priorities (notably AI-bilingual talent and scaling shared AI solutions); the National AI Impact Programme (NAIIP, March 2026) aims to move 10,000 SMEs from AI experimentation to operations. Governance continues via IMDA's AI Verify framework and Project Moonshot (open-source LLM evaluation/red-teaming).

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** National Quantum Strategy (NQS) + RIE2030 Quantum Thematic Plan
- **Body:** NRF / National Quantum Office (hosted by A*STAR)
- **Year:** May 2024 (NQS); RIE2030 Quantum Thematic Plan outlined April 2026
- **URL:** https://www.nqo.sg/ ; https://www.cqt.sg/highlight/2024-05-singapore-national-quantum-strategy/
- **Summary:** ~S$300M under RIE2025 launched the NQS to make Singapore a global quantum hub across four thrusts: scientific excellence (elevating CQT to a flagship national center), engineering translation (QEP, National Quantum Processor Initiative, National Quantum Federated Foundry), talent (National Quantum Scholarships — 100 PhD + 100 Master), and enterprise. In 2025–26 the strategy moved into downstream translation and infrastructure: the National Quantum Federated Foundry (NQFF) held its Industry Day in February 2026, networking national cleanrooms to fabricate quantum micro/nano-devices. At the 3rd Quantum Industry Day (April 2026), NRF Chairman Heng Swee Keat outlined the upcoming RIE2030 Quantum Thematic Plan and announced a National Quantum Office partnership with Quantinuum, which set up an R&D and Operations Centre in Singapore in early 2026. Continues to deploy the National Quantum-Safe Network (Plus) — SE Asia's first quantum-safe network infrastructure.

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** RIE2030 — "Human Health and Potential" (HHP) domain
- **Body:** NRF / Ministry of Health / A*STAR
- **Year:** December 2025 (RIE2030, 2026–2030); succeeds RIE2025 (2020)
- **URL:** https://www.nrf.gov.sg/rie-ecosystem/
- **Summary:** The "Human Health and Potential" domain carries into RIE2030 (endorsed Dec 2025, funded at Budget 2026) to tackle an ageing population and low birth rates, now under the record S$37B envelope. Priorities span early childhood development, healthy longevity, and the National Precision Medicine Programme (mapping the population genome for Asian-phenotype-targeted therapies), with the PREPARE programme funding epidemic preparedness post-COVID. Under RIE2030, A*STAR is scaling national innovation platforms — including the Nucleic Acid Therapeutics Initiative (NATi) and the MedTech Catapult — to accelerate translation of biomedical research into clinical and healthcare solutions.

## 5. Materials Science
- **Plan:** RIE2030 — Manufacturing, Trade and Connectivity (MTC) domain, Advanced Materials sector
- **Body:** NRF / A*STAR
- **Year:** December 2025 (RIE2030, 2026–2030); succeeds RIE2025 (2020)
- **URL:** https://www.nrf.gov.sg/rie-ecosystem/ ; https://www.a-star.edu.sg (IAF-PP MTC grant materials)
- **Summary:** Applied materials R&D feeds aerospace, semiconductor, and automotive supply chains via the Industry Alignment Fund (IAF-PP / IAF-ICP), now resourced under RIE2030's S$37B envelope. Targets continuous fibre-reinforced polymers, metallic additive materials, and high-entropy metals. Hallmark "Corporate Lab" model (e.g. Applied Materials-NUS Advanced Materials Corporate Lab, Phase 2 from late 2024; AUMOVIO/Continental-NTU) co-funds MNC R&D to de-risk discovery and embed local expertise. Advanced materials are increasingly tied to the Semiconductor RIE Flagship's needs in advanced packaging and photonics.

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** Semiconductor RIE Flagship (under RIE2030) + Manufacturing 2030 Vision
- **Body:** EDB / A*STAR / MTI
- **Year:** 2021 (Manufacturing 2030); RIE Flagship anchored in RIE2030 (Dec 2025)
- **URL:** https://www.edb.gov.sg/en/our-industries/semiconductor.html ; https://nstic.sg/
- **Summary:** Singapore holds ~10% of global chip output and ~20% of semiconductor equipment manufacturing (~6% of GDP). Rather than a subsidy arms race, a targeted S$800M Semiconductor RIE Flagship — co-led by A*STAR and EDB and confirmed as a major pillar of RIE2030 (endorsed Dec 2025) — focuses on advanced packaging, heterogeneous integration, advanced photonics, and wide-bandgap (GaN), addressing the power and materials demands of global AI infrastructure. A*STAR invests S$500M in the NSTIC R&D Fab (JTC nanoSpace @ Tampines, opening 2027; 8"/12" wafers) plus NSTIC (GaN), launched June 2025. Addresses talent via ITE/polytechnic curricula and the Shortage Occupation List.

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** Smart Nation 2.0 + Digital Connectivity Blueprint (DCB) + Digital Infrastructure Act (DIA)
- **Body:** Smart Nation Group / IMDA / Ministry of Digital Development and Information (MDDI)
- **Year:** June 2023 (DCB); October 2024 (Smart Nation 2.0); DIA to be tabled 2026
- **URL:** https://www.smartnation.gov.sg/about/our-vision/sn2/ ; https://www.imda.gov.sg/how-we-can-help/digital-connectivity-blueprint
- **Summary:** Three pillars — Trust, Growth, Community. DCB commits to seamless 10 Gbps domestic connectivity, doubling submarine cable capacity over a decade, Wi-Fi 6E / 5G Standalone spectrum, and a green-data-center standard for tropical climates. Under the "Trust" pillar, IMDA issued Advisory Guidelines on Resilience and Security of Data Centres (Feb 2025), and on 24 February 2026 MDSOS Tan Kiat How confirmed the Digital Infrastructure Act (DIA) will be tabled in Parliament later in 2026 — imposing binding baseline obligations on major cloud service providers and data-centre operators (mandatory incident reporting, resilience against power/cooling failures, and Power Usage Effectiveness requirements). Builds the Singapore Digital Utility Stack for secure e-transactions.

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** National Robotics Programme (NRP) + LTA AV Legislative Framework / Smart Mobility
- **Body:** A*STAR / NRF (robotics); LTA / MOT (mobility)
- **Year:** 2016 (NRP); 2024 (S$60M RoboCluster); AV legislative framework consultation May 2026
- **URL:** https://www.lta.gov.sg/content/ltagov/en/industry_innovations/technologies/autonomous_vehicles.html ; https://www.mot.gov.sg
- **Summary:** Automation is driven by labor constraints and ageing. NRP (>S$450M historical funding + S$60M 2024 "RoboCluster") accelerates robotics in facilities management, manufacturing, and healthcare; produced the open-source Robotics Middleware Framework (Open-RMF). In May 2026 the NRP, with IMDA, JTC, and SIT, launched an "Embodied AI" (Physical AI) testbed at Punggol Digital District, with LTA granting a precinct-level exemption under the Active Mobility Act for robots/AVs operating alongside pedestrians. On 4 May 2026 MOT and LTA opened a public consultation (to 30 June 2026) on a holistic AV legal framework — covering operator/fleet accountability, insurance, data/cybersecurity, and human-to-machine liability handover — as the precursor to an "AV Act" planned for tabling in 2027. Pilot autonomous public bus services (routes 191 one-north and 400 Marina Bay; ~S$8.14M contract) launch mid-2026, with safety operators onboard and worker reskilling into AV remote-operator/fleet-tech roles.
