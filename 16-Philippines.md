# Philippines — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Country 16 (Batch 3). ASEAN bloc.*

The Philippines is pivoting from a service-oriented, tech-consuming economy (BPO/OSAT) toward indigenous innovation and value-added manufacturing, anchored in *AmBisyon Natin 2040* and the *Philippine Development Plan 2023–2028*. Constraints: low R&D spend (~0.3% of GDP vs. UNESCO's 1%), fragmented bureaucracy, and talent gaps. Notable reversal: nuclear energy is back via Executive Order 164, including the mothballed Bataan plant question.

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** Republic Act No. 12305 (Philippine National Nuclear Energy Safety Act / PhilATOM Act) + Executive Order No. 164 (National Position for a Nuclear Energy Program) + Philippine Energy Plan 2023–2050
- **Body:** Office of the President / Department of Energy (DOE) / Philippine Atomic Energy Regulatory Authority (PhilATOM)
- **Year:** 2025 (RA 12305); 2022 (EO 164); 2023 (PEP)
- **URL:** https://www.pna.gov.ph/articles/1259538
- **Summary:** President Marcos signed RA 12305 on 18 September 2025, enacting what had been the PhilAtom Bill: it creates PhilATOM, an independent quasi-judicial nuclear safety regulator aligned to IAEA standards, with sole jurisdiction over the siting, construction, operation, and decommissioning of nuclear and radiation facilities and mandated waste-management and decommissioning trust funds. This completes the legal framework that EO 164 (2022) had only set in policy — EO 164 integrates nuclear as clean baseload, targeting 1,200 MW by 2032, 2,400 MW by 2035, and 4,800 MW by 2050, favouring SMRs suited to the archipelago. The DOE announced it will begin accepting nuclear power plant license applications under PhilATOM in 2026, assessing 15+ candidate sites including rehabilitation of the mothballed Bataan Nuclear Power Plant (with Korea's KHNP) and SMR deployment. A US–Philippines "123 Agreement" entered into force July 2024.

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** National AI Strategy of the Philippines (NAIS-PH) + National Artificial Intelligence Strategy Roadmap 2.0 (NAISR 2.0)
- **Body:** Department of Science and Technology (DOST) / Department of Trade and Industry (DTI) / Center for AI Research (CAIR)
- **Year:** 2025 (NAIS-PH); July 2024 (NAISR 2.0)
- **URL:** https://www.dost.gov.ph/knowledge-resources/news/86-2025-news/3989-dost-builds-on-ai-national-strategy.html
- **Summary:** Building on DTI's 2024 NAISR 2.0, DOST elevated AI into a whole-of-nation framework — the National AI Strategy of the Philippines (NAIS-PH), approved by the President in 2025 and launched into implementation at the National AI Stakeholders' Conference (Manila, 13–14 October 2025) — targeting an "AI-powered Philippines by 2028." It is organised around five pillars: infrastructure, workforce, innovation, ethics, and policy. Commitments include a 26-fold increase in national high-performance computing power by 2028, training ~10,000 AI-capable workers, and standing up a National AI Center for Research and Innovation (with DOST injecting over PHP 2.3B into AI R&D). The predecessor NAISR 2.0 still anchors the goal of evolving the vulnerable IT-BPM sector up the value chain; a comprehensive Philippine AI Act (risk-based, EU-style) remains pending in Congress as of mid-2026.

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** Quantum Technology Roadmap
- **Body:** DOST-PCIEERD (Philippine Council for Industry, Energy and Emerging Technology R&D)
- **Year:** 2021 (timeline extended to 2035)
- **URL:** https://pcieerd.dost.gov.ph/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/Quantum_Technology_Roadmap_Dec15.pdf
- **Summary:** An R&D/capability-building blueprint to make the Philippines a SE Asian quantum hub by 2035 (recalibrated from 2030). Funded the Quantum Innovation Laboratory (QIL, DOST-ASTI, leveraging the COARE HPC) and QISLaP (with TIP) applying hybrid quantum models to power-grid load forecasting and renewables. Relies on collaboration (e.g. Japan's RIKEN); bottlenecks are a severe researcher shortage (addressed via the Balik Scientist program) and siloed academia/government/industry.

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** National Biotechnology Strategy (proposed unifying framework under PAGTANAW 2050)
- **Body:** DOST / Department of Agriculture (DA) / National Committee on Biosafety of the Philippines (NCBP)
- **Year:** 2025 (NBS policy brief, 9 June 2025); PAGTANAW 2050 foresight 2021
- **URL:** https://ncbp.dost.gov.ph/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/brief-11_...Developing-a-National-Biotechnology-Strategy.pdf
- **Summary:** The NCBP/DOST published "Developing a National Biotechnology Strategy: A Path Forward for Food Security, Education, and Materials Innovation" on 9 June 2025 — a whole-of-nation framework steering the country toward a bio-based economy (BBE) by capitalising on its vast biological resources, with priorities in agricultural biotech (biofertilizers), sustainable materials (bioplastics, biomass-to-energy and sustainable aviation fuel), and specialised biotech education. It is unified conceptually under PAGTANAW 2050 (NAST). The Philippines pioneered SE Asian biosafety (NCBP, 1990) and Asia's first GM crop (Bt Corn, 2002), and funds genomics, CRISPR/Cas9 facilities (incl. the first aquaculture gene-editing lab), and resilient crops. Progress is hampered by GM opposition (Golden Rice, Bt Eggplant judicial pushback) and overlapping DA/DENR/DOH mandates, which the unified NBS seeks to streamline.

## 5. Materials Science / Critical Minerals
- **Plan:** Philippine Mineral Industry Roadmap + Advanced Materials R&D Roadmap (within the Harmonized National R&D Agenda 2023–2028)
- **Body:** DENR-Mines and Geosciences Bureau (MGB) / DOST-PCIEERD / DTI-BOI
- **Year:** 2023 (Harmonized Agenda); Mineral Roadmap phases 2022–2040
- **URL:** https://pidswebs.pids.gov.ph/CDN/document/MGB-Engr-Danny-Berches-June-15-2023-webinar.pdf
- **Summary:** Aims to move from raw-mineral exporter to high-value processor, especially for nickel and copper (EV/green-energy demand). Three phases: investor confidence (2022–24), expanded ore production (2023–30), and downstream value-adding to global markets (2026–40) — attracting HPAL/processing plants à la Indonesia, synergized with the Tatak Pinoy Act. Parallel DOST Advanced Materials R&D funds nanomaterials (graphene), biodegradable polymers, and battery precursors, with strict ESG/Natural Capital Accounting.

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** Semiconductor and Electronics Industry Roadmap (updated under the Tatak Pinoy Industrial Policy + CREATE MORE)
- **Body:** DTI / Semiconductor and Electronics Industries in the Philippines Foundation (SEIPI)
- **Year:** 2024 (updating the 2013/14 roadmap)
- **URL:** https://dtiwebfiles.s3-ap-southeast-1.amazonaws.com/HRAS/Procurement/2024/April/PR%20BPR%2071235.pdf
- **Summary:** Electronics/semiconductors are >50% of exports but stuck in low-value OSAT/EMS. Driven by the Tatak Pinoy Act (RA 11981), DTI funded a feasibility study (~PHP 10M) for the country's first front-end wafer fabrication plant — long identified (2013/14 roadmaps) as the critical supply-chain gap. Aims to climb to IC design and fab, leveraging "China Plus One," TESDA workforce programs (Front-of-Line operations), and CREATE MORE fiscal incentives to offset fab capex.

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** National Digital Connectivity Plan (NDCP) 2024–2028 + E-Governance Act (Republic Act No. 12254)
- **Body:** Department of Information and Communications Technology (DICT)
- **Year:** 2026 (NDCP, approved 26 Jan 2026); 2025 (RA 12254, signed 5 Sept 2025)
- **URL:** https://dict.gov.ph/news-and-updates/25583
- **Summary:** The NDCP — the country's first-ever national digital infrastructure master plan and the expanded successor to the 2017 National Broadband Plan — was approved by the Economy and Development (ED) Council on 26 January 2026 and rolled out at the Philippine Telecommunications Summit 2026. Targeting roughly PHP 5.6 trillion in public and private investment, it uses a multimodal mix (fiber backbone, 5G, satellite, submarine cables) to deliver fast, affordable internet to every Filipino by 2028, especially in geographically isolated and disadvantaged areas (GIDAs), and is reinforced by the pending Konektadong Pinoy Act. In parallel, RA 12254 (E-Governance Act, signed 5 September 2025) legally institutionalises digital government across all branches and LGUs via a Philippine Government Interoperability Framework, with the eGovPH Super App as the central citizen platform; its IRR was signed in March 2026. The agenda remains insulated by the National Cybersecurity Plan 2023–2028.

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** Inclusive Innovation Industrial Strategy (i³S) + Industry 4.0 Pilot Factory Program (with DOST Harmonized R&D and CAAP PCAR Part 11 for drones)
- **Body:** DTI / DOST-PCIEERD / Civil Aviation Authority of the Philippines (CAAP)
- **Year:** 2022–2024 (Industry 4.0 rollout); ongoing CAAP updates
- **URL:** https://www.pna.gov.ph/articles/1226719 (Industry 4.0 Factory)
- **Summary:** Under i³S, the DTI built the first Industry 4.0 Pilot Factory in Calabarzon (~PHP 300–400M) — an experiential sandbox for MSMEs to access automation, robots, and drones (MOUs with Siemens, Bosch). DOST-PCIEERD funds "Unmanned Vehicle Systems" and "Robotics" R&D (e.g. the Cuatro program), applied to precision agriculture and disaster risk reduction (hazard mapping). Drones are regulated by CAAP via Philippine Civil Aviation Regulations (PCAR) Part 11.
