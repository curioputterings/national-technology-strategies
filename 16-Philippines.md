# Philippines — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Country 16 (Batch 3). ASEAN bloc.*

The Philippines is pivoting from a service-oriented, tech-consuming economy (BPO/OSAT) toward indigenous innovation and value-added manufacturing, anchored in *AmBisyon Natin 2040* and the *Philippine Development Plan 2023–2028*. Constraints: low R&D spend (~0.3% of GDP vs. UNESCO's 1%), fragmented bureaucracy, and talent gaps. Notable reversal: nuclear energy is back via Executive Order 164, including the mothballed Bataan plant question.

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** Executive Order No. 164 (National Position for a Nuclear Energy Program) + Philippine Energy Plan 2023–2050 + PNEP Roadmap
- **Body:** Office of the President / Department of Energy (DOE) / Nuclear Energy Program Inter-Agency Committee (NEP-IAC)
- **Year:** 2022 (EO 164); 2023 (PEP)
- **URL:** https://lawphil.net/executive/execord/eo2022/pdf/eo_164_2022.pdf
- **Summary:** EO 164 integrates nuclear into the energy mix as clean baseload. Targets 1,200 MW by 2032, 2,400 MW by 2035, and 4,800 MW by 2050. Pursues SMRs (suited to the archipelago — Palawan, Mindanao) and a two-phase feasibility study (from early 2025, with Korea's KHNP) on rehabilitating the mothballed Bataan Nuclear Power Plant (BNPP, never operated). The PhilAtom Bill would create an independent regulator; a US–Philippines "123 Agreement" entered into force July 2024.

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** National Artificial Intelligence Strategy Roadmap 2.0 (NAISR 2.0)
- **Body:** Department of Trade and Industry (DTI) / Center for AI Research (CAIR)
- **Year:** July 2024
- **URL:** https://erikalegara.com/uploads/NAISR2.0_July2024.pdf
- **Summary:** A mid-cycle upgrade (with ADB backing) to make the Philippines an ASEAN AI hub, with seven strategic imperatives (connectivity, data, education, workforce, ethics, research, innovation). Commits to raising R&D toward 1% of GDP and established CAIR as the first national AI hub. Critically aims to evolve the vulnerable IT-BPM sector up the value chain (NEDA estimates AI could add ~PHP 2T/yr). Tied to the Tatak Pinoy Act; Senate Bill 25 (AI Regulation Act) is in early stages.

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** Quantum Technology Roadmap
- **Body:** DOST-PCIEERD (Philippine Council for Industry, Energy and Emerging Technology R&D)
- **Year:** 2021 (timeline extended to 2035)
- **URL:** https://pcieerd.dost.gov.ph/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/Quantum_Technology_Roadmap_Dec15.pdf
- **Summary:** An R&D/capability-building blueprint to make the Philippines a SE Asian quantum hub by 2035 (recalibrated from 2030). Funded the Quantum Innovation Laboratory (QIL, DOST-ASTI, leveraging the COARE HPC) and QISLaP (with TIP) applying hybrid quantum models to power-grid load forecasting and renewables. Relies on collaboration (e.g. Japan's RIKEN); bottlenecks are a severe researcher shortage (addressed via the Balik Scientist program) and siloed academia/government/industry.

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** National Biotechnology Strategy (proposed unifying framework under PAGTANAW 2050)
- **Body:** DOST / Department of Agriculture (DA) / National Committee on Biosafety of the Philippines (NCBP)
- **Year:** 2021 (PAGTANAW 2050 foresight); regulatory circulars 2023/24
- **URL:** https://ncbp.dost.gov.ph/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/brief-11_...Developing-a-National-Biotechnology-Strategy.pdf
- **Summary:** A multi-agency strategy for food security, biomedical diagnostics, and a circular bioeconomy, unified conceptually under PAGTANAW 2050 (NAST). The Philippines pioneered SE Asian biosafety (NCBP, 1990) and Asia's first GM crop (Bt Corn, 2002). Funds genomics, CRISPR/Cas9 facilities (incl. the first aquaculture gene-editing lab), and resilient crops. Hampered by GM opposition (Golden Rice, Bt Eggplant judicial pushback) and overlapping DA/DENR/DOH mandates; the unified NBS seeks to streamline approvals.

## 5. Materials Science / Critical Minerals
- **Plan:** Philippine Mineral Industry Roadmap + Advanced Materials R&D Roadmap (within the Harmonized National R&D Agenda 2023–2028)
- **Body:** DENR-Mines and Geosciences Bureau (MGB) / DOST-PCIEERD / DTI-BOI
- **Year:** 2023 (Harmonized Agenda); Mineral Roadmap phases 2022–2040
- **URL:** https://pidswebs.pids.gov.ph/CDN/document/MGB-Engr-Danny-Berches-June-15-2023-webinar.pdf
- **Summary:** Aims to move from raw-mineral exporter to high-value processor, especially for nickel and copper (EV/green-energy demand). Three phases: investor confidence (2022–24), expanded ore production (2023–30), and downstream value-adding to global markets (2026–40) — attracting HPAL/processing plants à la Indonesia, synergized with the Tatak Pinoy Act. Parallel DOST Advanced Materials R&D funds nanomaterials (graphene), biodegradable polymers, and battery precursors, with strict ESG/Natural Capital Accounting.

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** Semiconductor and Electronics Industry Roadmap (updated under the Tatak Pinoy Industrial Policy + CREATE MORE)
- **Body:** DTI / Semiconductor and Electronics Industries in the Philippines Foundation (SEIPI)
- **Year:** 2024 (updating the 2013/14 roadmap)
- **URL:** https://dtiwebfiles.s3-ap-southeast-1.amazonaws.com/HRAS/Procurement/2024/April/PR%20BPR%2071235.pdf
- **Summary:** Electronics/semiconductors are >50% of exports but stuck in low-value OSAT/EMS. Driven by the Tatak Pinoy Act (RA 11981), DTI funded a feasibility study (~PHP 10M) for the country's first front-end wafer fabrication plant — long identified (2013/14 roadmaps) as the critical supply-chain gap. Aims to climb to IC design and fab, leveraging "China Plus One," TESDA workforce programs (Front-of-Line operations), and CREATE MORE fiscal incentives to offset fab capex.

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** National Broadband Plan (NBP) + E-Government Masterplan 2022 (EGMP 2022)
- **Body:** Department of Information and Communications Technology (DICT)
- **Year:** 2017 (NBP); 2019 (EGMP 2022)
- **URL:** https://cms-cdn.e.gov.ph/DICT/pdf/2017.08.09-National-Broadband-Plan.pdf
- **Summary:** Master blueprint to deliver open, pervasive, affordable internet and streamline government despite challenging archipelagic geography and a telco duopoly. NBP builds the National Fiber Optic Cable Backbone, Accelerated Common Tower policy, and Free Wi-Fi for All (targeting GIDAs). EGMP 2022 drives interoperable e-government — the eGovPH Super App centralizes 1,000+ services, linked to PhilSys and TradeNet. Insulated by the National Cybersecurity Plan 2023–2028; goal: ICT at 10% of GDP by 2025.

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** Inclusive Innovation Industrial Strategy (i³S) + Industry 4.0 Pilot Factory Program (with DOST Harmonized R&D and CAAP PCAR Part 11 for drones)
- **Body:** DTI / DOST-PCIEERD / Civil Aviation Authority of the Philippines (CAAP)
- **Year:** 2022–2024 (Industry 4.0 rollout); ongoing CAAP updates
- **URL:** https://www.pna.gov.ph/articles/1226719 (Industry 4.0 Factory)
- **Summary:** Under i³S, the DTI built the first Industry 4.0 Pilot Factory in Calabarzon (~PHP 300–400M) — an experiential sandbox for MSMEs to access automation, robots, and drones (MOUs with Siemens, Bosch). DOST-PCIEERD funds "Unmanned Vehicle Systems" and "Robotics" R&D (e.g. the Cuatro program), applied to precision agriculture and disaster risk reduction (hazard mapping). Drones are regulated by CAAP via Philippine Civil Aviation Regulations (PCAR) Part 11.
