# United Arab Emirates (UAE) — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Entity 25 (Batch 5).*

The UAE runs a highly coordinated, state-led pivot from a hydrocarbon to a knowledge economy, framed by **UAE Centennial 2071** and **We the UAE 2031**. Massive sovereign capital (Mubadala, ADQ, and the new ~$100B MGX fund) and bespoke institutions (Ministry of State for AI — a world first; ATRC/TII; ENEC) deploy hundreds of billions toward "technological sovereignty." Distinctive: an operating nuclear plant (Barakah) and aggressive regulatory agility (AV law, post-quantum mandate).

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** UAE Policy on the Evaluation and Potential Development of Peaceful Nuclear Energy
- **Body:** Federal Government / Emirates Nuclear Energy Corporation (ENEC) / Federal Authority for Nuclear Regulation (FANR)
- **Year:** 2008 (foundational); Barakah full-fleet operation 2024; ADVANCE Programme expansion 2025–26
- **URL:** https://www.enec.ae/doc/uae-peaceful-nuclear-energy-policy-5722278a2952f.pdf
- **Summary:** A "gold standard" peaceful program — legally forgoing domestic enrichment and reprocessing — anchored by the Barakah plant (4 APR1400 reactors, 5,600 MW baseload), built with Korea's KEPCO; Unit 4 reached full commercial operation in September 2024, with all four reactors now supplying ~25% of UAE electricity. With Barakah complete, ENEC has pivoted to its **ADVANCE Programme** for next-generation nuclear: in 2025 it signed SMR-evaluation MoUs for the BWRX-300 (GE Vernova Hitachi, May 2025) and with Samsung C&T (July 2025), plus an international-investment MoU with Westinghouse on AP1000 deployment (July 2025). FANR is preparing a new regulatory masterplan (2025–2029) covering SMR licensing, cybersecurity, and AI at nuclear sites. Supports the Energy Strategy 2050 (target doubling nuclear toward ~8 reactors) and remains regulated independently by FANR to IAEA standards — a model for emerging nuclear nations.

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** UAE National Strategy for Artificial Intelligence 2031
- **Body:** Ministry of State for AI, Digital Economy and Remote Work; Artificial Intelligence and Advanced Technology Council (AIATC)
- **Year:** 2017 (launch; world's first AI minister); 2025 (US–UAE partnership; AI-in-legislation)
- **URL:** https://dig.watch/resource/the-uae-national-strategy-for-artificial-intelligence-2031
- **Summary:** Targets ~AED 335B (~$91.2B) of AI-driven growth by 2031 across eight pillars (reputation, ecosystem, talent, governance), aiming to become a sovereign AI creator. The AIATC (2024) and the sovereign fund MGX (Mubadala + G42) deploy up to $100B globally into AI infrastructure, LLMs, and compute. In May 2025 the UAE and US announced the **US–UAE AI Acceleration Partnership** and a 5GW UAE–US AI Campus in Abu Dhabi (G42-led), under which **Stargate UAE** — a 1GW cluster operated by OpenAI/Oracle (first "OpenAI for Countries" deployment) — broke ground, with the first 200MW due Q3 2026. In April 2025 the Cabinet established a **Regulatory Intelligence Office** to use AI in drafting and reviewing legislation — a claimed world first — aiming to accelerate lawmaking by up to 70% under human oversight. Navigates East-West partnerships while protecting data sovereignty.

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** UAE National Encryption Policy + National Post-Quantum Migration Programme
- **Body:** UAE Cybersecurity Council, Advanced Technology Research Council (ATRC), Technology Innovation Institute (TII)
- **Year:** 2024–2026
- **URL:** https://www.atrc.gov.ae/news/uae-cyber-security-council-partners-atrc-advance-national-post-quantum-security-transition
- **Summary:** Mandates a nationwide transition to post-quantum cryptography to defend against "store now, decrypt later" attacks — requiring government and critical-infrastructure operators to map crypto assets via a Crypto Discovery Tool (with ATRC-backed QuantumGate). In 2025–26 the programme moved from planning to large-scale implementation: a May 2026 operational agreement between the Cyber Security Council and VentureOne/QuantumGate scales TII-developed cryptographic libraries and Entanglement-based Quantum Key Distribution (EQKD) into one of the world's first coordinated national post-quantum migration plans, and October 2025 saw the UAE's first quantum-computing deployment for the energy sector (TII/ADNOC). TII's Quantum Research Centre develops homegrown qubits (region's first superconducting qubit) and open-source software (Qibo); partners with NYU Abu Dhabi's quantum center.

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** National Genome Strategy / Emirati Genome Program (EGP)
- **Body:** Emirates Genome Council / Department of Health (Abu Dhabi)
- **Year:** 2023 (strategy); 2024 (Healthy Longevity Medicine framework)
- **URL:** https://genome.gov.ae/about/national-genome-strategy/
- **Summary:** Aims to sequence one million genomes to build a population genomic database for preventive/personalized medicine, focused on oncology and rare/metabolic diseases prevalent in the region. Integrates AI with genomics and a pioneering "Healthy Longevity Medicine" clinical specialty (Abu Dhabi DoH, 2024). Targets a regional genomics market ~$1.2B by 2027 and has drawn significant biotech FDI.

## 5. Materials Science
- **Plan:** National Strategy for Industry and Advanced Technology ("Operation 300bn") + US–UAE Framework on Securing Supply in the Mining and Processing of Critical Minerals and Rare Earths
- **Body:** Ministry of Industry and Advanced Technology (MoIAT); Ministry of Investment
- **Year:** 2021 (targeting 2031); Critical Minerals Framework Feb 2026
- **URL:** https://www.uae-embassy.org/news/uae-and-united-states-sign-framework-strengthen-secure-supply-critical-minerals-and-rare
- **Summary:** Aims to raise manufacturing GDP from AED 133B to AED 300B by 2031, prioritizing localized advanced materials — aerospace carbon-fibre prepregs, critical medical supplies, renewable-energy components — and empowering 13,500 SMEs with an AED 40B financing initiative. Flagship: Strata Syensqo Advanced Materials (Al Ain) producing carbon-fibre prepreg for Boeing's 777X. In February 2026 the UAE (Ministry of Investment) and the US signed a **Framework on Securing Supply in the Mining and Processing of Critical Minerals and Rare Earths**, committing both states to mobilize public and private investment across mining, separation, processing, and recycling — leveraging UAE strategic reserves and sovereign vehicles against US industrial demand, with priority joint projects and financing targeted within six months. Uses "Make it in the Emirates" and In-Country Value procurement levers.

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** MGX Investment Strategy (within the AIATC) + National Industry & Advanced Tech strategy
- **Body:** AIATC / MGX / MoIAT
- **Year:** 2024 (MGX & AIATC launch); 2026 (Tawazun chiplet ecosystem)
- **URL:** https://www.mubadala.com/en/news/abu-dhabi-launches-comprehensive-global-investment-strategy-on-artificial-intelligence
- **Summary:** Localizing chip capability to feed AI infrastructure: MGX designates semiconductors a core pillar of its ~$100B deployment (custom GPUs, advanced memory, chipmaking processes). Dual-track with defense microelectronics — a national chiplet-microelectronics ecosystem advanced from a November 2025 Letter of Intent (Tawazun Council, EDGE Group, Khalifa University, Lockheed Martin) to an execution-phase agreement at Make it in the Emirates in May 2026 to build an advanced chiplet design-and-assembly facility in Abu Dhabi, with EDGE's HALCON integrating chiplet processors into guidance/autonomous systems and Khalifa University running R&D and talent. Combines buying into top-tier global fabrication networks with cultivating domestic assembly/R&D, aiming to be a central node by 2035.

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** "We the UAE 2031" Vision (incl. UAE Digital Economy Strategy)
- **Body:** UAE Cabinet / Federal Government
- **Year:** 2022 (targeting 2031); 5GW AI Campus / Stargate UAE 2025
- **URL:** https://uaecabinet.ae/en/about-government-strategy-archive
- **Summary:** The connective national action plan operationalizing Centennial 2071, emphasizing digital transformation, cyber resilience, and a knowledge economy — aiming to roughly double the digital economy's GDP contribution. Mandates AI and agile digital methods across all government entities (an "AI-native" structure), pairs sovereign cloud/data-center buildout with aggressive global talent attraction (blockchain, analytics, software). The buildout is anchored by the May 2025 **UAE–US 5GW AI Campus** in Abu Dhabi (~10 sq mi, G42-led) and its first phase **Stargate UAE** — a 1GW cluster (Khazna/G42 with OpenAI, Oracle, NVIDIA GB300, Cisco, SoftBank), the largest AI data-centre campus outside the US, with the first 200MW targeted for Q3 2026 and powered partly by nuclear and solar.

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** Dubai Smart Self-Driving Transport Strategy (multi-vendor robotaxi rollout; Law No. 9 of 2023 regulating autonomous vehicles)
- **Body:** Dubai Roads and Transport Authority (RTA) / Dubai Future Foundation (DFF)
- **Year:** 2016 (strategy); 2023 (legal framework); 2026 (commercial driverless launch; targeting 2030)
- **URL:** https://www.intelligentcio.com/me/2026/04/13/weride-and-uber-launch-fully-driverless-robotaxi-fare-charging-operations-in-dubai-accelerating-autonomous-mobility-in-the-middle-east/
- **Summary:** Targets converting 25% of all Dubai mobility journeys to autonomous by 2030 — projected to cut transport costs 44%, pollution 12%, and yield ~AED 22B/yr. Backed by Law No. 9/2023 (RTA powers to license, zone, and assign liability). Having moved on from its earlier exclusive Cruise (GM) deal, the RTA shifted to an open multi-vendor model: **WeRide + Uber** launched fully driverless (Level 4) fare-charging robotaxi operations on 31 March 2026 (RTA driverless permit Feb 2026, fleet via Tawasul), **Baidu Apollo Go + Dubai Taxi Company** opened a commercial driverless service in April 2026 (50 vehicles, scaling to 1,000+), and **Pony.ai** began fully driverless testing (April 2026) toward a H2-2026 commercial launch — making Dubai one of the first cities outside the US/China with public, commercially operating L4 ride-hailing. Complemented by federal hyperloop and aerial-drone exploration.
