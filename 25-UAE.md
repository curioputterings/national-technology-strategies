# United Arab Emirates (UAE) — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Entity 25 (Batch 5).*

The UAE runs a highly coordinated, state-led pivot from a hydrocarbon to a knowledge economy, framed by **UAE Centennial 2071** and **We the UAE 2031**. Massive sovereign capital (Mubadala, ADQ, and the new ~$100B MGX fund) and bespoke institutions (Ministry of State for AI — a world first; ATRC/TII; ENEC) deploy hundreds of billions toward "technological sovereignty." Distinctive: an operating nuclear plant (Barakah) and aggressive regulatory agility (AV law, post-quantum mandate).

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** UAE Policy on the Evaluation and Potential Development of Peaceful Nuclear Energy
- **Body:** Federal Government / Emirates Nuclear Energy Corporation (ENEC) / Federal Authority for Nuclear Regulation (FANR)
- **Year:** 2008 (foundational); Barakah operational milestones 2020–2024
- **URL:** https://www.enec.ae/doc/uae-peaceful-nuclear-energy-policy-5722278a2952f.pdf
- **Summary:** A "gold standard" peaceful program — legally forgoing domestic enrichment and reprocessing — anchored by the Barakah plant (4 APR1400 reactors, 5,600 MW baseload), built with Korea's KEPCO. Supports the Energy Strategy 2050 (50% clean energy; preventing ~21M tons of CO₂/yr). Generated a $4.8B local supply chain and 3,000+ jobs (60%+ Emirati). Regulated independently by FANR to IAEA standards — a model for emerging nuclear nations.

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** UAE National Strategy for Artificial Intelligence 2031
- **Body:** Ministry of State for AI, Digital Economy and Remote Work; Artificial Intelligence and Advanced Technology Council (AIATC)
- **Year:** 2017 (launch; world's first AI minister)
- **URL:** https://dig.watch/resource/the-uae-national-strategy-for-artificial-intelligence-2031
- **Summary:** Targets ~AED 335B (~$91.2B) of AI-driven growth by 2031 across eight pillars (reputation, ecosystem, talent, governance), aiming to become a sovereign AI creator. The AIATC (2024) and the sovereign fund MGX (Mubadala + G42) deploy up to $100B globally into AI infrastructure, LLMs, and compute. Integrates generative AI into governance/public services; navigates East-West partnerships while protecting data sovereignty.

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** UAE National Encryption Policy + National Post-Quantum Migration Programme
- **Body:** UAE Cybersecurity Council, Advanced Technology Research Council (ATRC), Technology Innovation Institute (TII)
- **Year:** 2024/2025
- **URL:** https://www.atrc.gov.ae/news/uae-cybersecurity-council-advances-national-readiness-future-quantum-threats-atrcs-quantumgate
- **Summary:** Mandates a nationwide transition to post-quantum cryptography to defend against "store now, decrypt later" attacks — requiring government and critical-infrastructure operators to map crypto assets via a Crypto Discovery Tool (with ATRC-backed QuantumGate). TII's Quantum Research Centre develops homegrown qubits (region's first superconducting qubit) and open-source software (Qibo); partners with NYU Abu Dhabi's quantum center.

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** National Genome Strategy / Emirati Genome Program (EGP)
- **Body:** Emirates Genome Council / Department of Health (Abu Dhabi)
- **Year:** 2023 (strategy); 2024 (Healthy Longevity Medicine framework)
- **URL:** https://genome.gov.ae/about/national-genome-strategy/
- **Summary:** Aims to sequence one million genomes to build a population genomic database for preventive/personalized medicine, focused on oncology and rare/metabolic diseases prevalent in the region. Integrates AI with genomics and a pioneering "Healthy Longevity Medicine" clinical specialty (Abu Dhabi DoH, 2024). Targets a regional genomics market ~$1.2B by 2027 and has drawn significant biotech FDI.

## 5. Materials Science
- **Plan:** National Strategy for Industry and Advanced Technology ("Operation 300bn")
- **Body:** Ministry of Industry and Advanced Technology (MoIAT)
- **Year:** 2021 (targeting 2031)
- **URL:** https://moiat.gov.ae
- **Summary:** Aims to raise manufacturing GDP from AED 133B to AED 300B by 2031, prioritizing localized advanced materials — aerospace carbon-fibre prepregs, critical medical supplies, renewable-energy components — and empowering 13,500 SMEs with an AED 40B financing initiative. Flagship: Strata Syensqo Advanced Materials (Al Ain) producing carbon-fibre prepreg for Boeing's 777X. Uses "Make it in the Emirates" and In-Country Value procurement levers.

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** MGX Investment Strategy (within the AIATC) + National Industry & Advanced Tech strategy
- **Body:** AIATC / MGX / MoIAT
- **Year:** 2024 (MGX & AIATC launch)
- **URL:** https://www.mubadala.com/en/news/abu-dhabi-launches-comprehensive-global-investment-strategy-on-artificial-intelligence
- **Summary:** Localizing chip capability to feed AI infrastructure: MGX designates semiconductors a core pillar of its ~$100B deployment (custom GPUs, advanced memory, chipmaking processes). Dual-track with defense microelectronics — a 2026 Tawazun/EDGE partnership with Lockheed Martin to build an advanced chiplet-design and assembly facility in Abu Dhabi. Combines buying into top-tier global fabrication networks with cultivating domestic assembly/R&D, aiming to be a central node by 2035.

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** "We the UAE 2031" Vision (incl. UAE Digital Economy Strategy)
- **Body:** UAE Cabinet / Federal Government
- **Year:** 2022 (targeting 2031)
- **URL:** https://uaecabinet.ae/en/about-government-strategy-archive
- **Summary:** The connective national action plan operationalizing Centennial 2071, emphasizing digital transformation, cyber resilience, and a knowledge economy — aiming to roughly double the digital economy's GDP contribution. Mandates AI and agile digital methods across all government entities (an "AI-native" structure), pairs sovereign cloud/data-center buildout with aggressive global talent attraction (blockchain, analytics, software).

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** Dubai Autonomous Transportation Strategy (+ Law No. 9 of 2023 regulating autonomous vehicles)
- **Body:** Dubai Roads and Transport Authority (RTA) / Dubai Future Foundation (DFF)
- **Year:** 2016 (strategy); 2023 (legal framework; targeting 2030)
- **URL:** https://natlawreview.com/article/driverless-dubai-autonomous-vehicle-regulation-advances-united-arab-emirates
- **Summary:** Targets converting 25% of all Dubai transport to autonomous by 2030 — projected to cut transport costs 44%, pollution 12%, and yield ~AED 22B/yr. Backed by Law No. 9/2023 (RTA powers to license, zone, and assign liability), enabling ~4,000 robotaxis by 2030. Secured an exclusive deal with Cruise (GM) — making Dubai the first non-US city to commercialize robotaxis at scale; complemented by federal hyperloop and aerial-drone exploration.
