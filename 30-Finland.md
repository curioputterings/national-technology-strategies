# Finland — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Entity 30 (Batch 6). OECD/EU/Nordic.*

Finland pursues technological sovereignty in highly specialized niches (6G, full-stack quantum, sustainable microelectronics) rather than mass manufacturing, leveraging a Nokia telecom legacy, deeply digitized health registries, and strong public-private synergy (Business Finland, VTT, Research Council flagships). Tightly aligned with EU frameworks (Chips Act, CRMA, Digital Decade). Strongly pro-nuclear — reforming its Nuclear Energy Act for SMRs after Olkiluoto 3.

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** Comprehensive Reform of the Nuclear Energy Act (Ydinenergialain kokonaisuudistus)
- **Body:** Ministry of Economic Affairs and Employment (TEM) / Finnish Government; STUK (safety authority)
- **Year:** 2026 (proposal submitted March 12, 2026; intended in force Jan 1, 2027)
- **URL:** https://valtioneuvosto.fi/en/-/1410877/reform-of-nuclear-energy-act-to-modernise-nuclear-plant-regulation-and-ease-new-nuclear-energy-investments
- **Summary:** A 550-page reform of the 1987 Act, moving to a risk-informed, technology-neutral framework to streamline SMR licensing — building on the successful Olkiluoto 3 EPR. Allows reactors closer to consumption (industry, urban district heating). Flagship pilot: Steady Energy's SMR in the turbine hall of Helen's Salmisaari coal plant (decarbonizing Helsinki district heating). Business Finland backs spin-offs to make Finland a nuclear-heating export hub.

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** Artificial Intelligence 4.0 Programme + AuroraAI National AI Programme
- **Body:** TEM (AI 4.0); Ministry of Finance (AuroraAI)
- **Year:** 2022 (AI 4.0); AuroraAI ongoing (initial strategy 2017)
- **URL:** https://digital-skills-jobs.europa.eu/en/initiatives/national-strategies/finland-artificial-intelligence-40-programme
- **Summary:** One of the first national AI strategies (2017), now focused on niches: human-centric public services, industrial AI, the green/digital "twin transition," and SME integration. AI 4.0 drives AI/IoT/HPC adoption in industrial SMEs (using the LUMI supercomputer); AuroraAI is a "life-events" decentralized public-service network. Backed by Business Finland (~€100M initially) and flagship funding for the Finnish Centre for AI (FCAI).

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** Finnish Quantum Technology Strategy 2025–2035 (preceded by InstituteQ's Finnish Quantum Agenda)
- **Body:** TEM, executed via InstituteQ (Aalto, Helsinki, VTT) and VTT
- **Year:** 2025 (published April 24, 2025)
- **URL:** https://julkaisut.valtioneuvosto.fi/bitstreams/d58ab039-a9c7-47dd-a816-58718208a18e/download
- **Summary:** One of few countries building full-stack superconducting quantum computers (VTT + IQM): "Helmi" 5-qubit (2021) → 20 → 50-qubit (early 2025), aiming for 300 qubits and quantum advantage. ~€70M committed to scale VTT's machines, plus the Finnish Quantum Flagship. InstituteQ coordinates the national competence center; leverages LUMI for hybrid quantum-HPC and the FiQCI infrastructure; develops quantum-secure cryptography.

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** Health Sector Growth Strategy for Research and Innovation + National Genome Strategy
- **Body:** Jointly Ministry of Social Affairs and Health (STM), TEM, Ministry of Education and Culture (OKM)
- **Year:** 2020 (Growth Strategy roadmap); Genome Strategy updated 2023
- **URL:** https://stm.fi/en/-/better-health-through-genomic-data-genome-strategy-updated
- **Summary:** Anchored in Finland's unique genetic heritage, 100% electronic medical records, and longitudinal health registries since the 1950s. Aims to be a model country for personalized medicine via the FINBB biobank network, a National Genome Center (Genome Act), and flagship FinnGen (genotype-phenotype). Targets full integration of genomic data into centralized digital healthcare by 2030; funds the National Neurocenter, Cancer Center, and Genome Center.

## 5. Materials Science / Critical Minerals
- **Plan:** National Battery Strategy 2025 + National Minerals Strategy
- **Body:** Ministry of Economic Affairs and Employment (TEM)
- **Year:** 2021 (Battery Strategy); 2024–2025 (Minerals Strategy update)
- **URL:** https://julkaisut.valtioneuvosto.fi/bitstream/handle/10024/162685/TEM_2021_6.pdf
- **Summary:** Leverages significant reserves (cobalt, nickel, lithium, graphite) and processing capacity to be a sustainable battery hub across the full value chain (incl. circular-economy recycling via BATCircle). Includes a €300M injection into the Finnish Minerals Group for precursor/cathode production. The allied Minerals Strategy responds to the EU Critical Raw Materials Act — responsible exploitation, streamlined permitting, and maximizing the domestic mineral cluster's value-add.

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** "Chips from the North – Semiconductor Strategy for Finland"
- **Body:** Technology Industries of Finland (Semiconductor Branch Group), with Business Finland, VTT, TEM
- **Year:** 2024
- **URL:** https://teknologiateollisuus.fi/wp-content/uploads/2024/09/Chips-from-the-North-Semiconductor-Strategy-for-Finland.pdf
- **Summary:** Targets tripling Finnish semiconductor revenue to €5–6B and up to 15,000 jobs by 2035, focusing on niche strengths: mobile SoCs, MEMS/sensors, integrated photonics, and quantum hardware. Calls for €5B in public-private R&D over a decade, anchored by the Kvanttinova piloting environment (Espoo/Otaniemi) and Micronova cleanrooms offering 200mm/300mm pilot production. Focus on ultra-low-power edge-AI chips and microelectronics-quantum integration; aligned with the EU Chips Act.

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** Finland's Digital Compass + National 6G Roadmap
- **Body:** Ministry of Transport and Communications (LVM), Ministry of Finance, 6G Flagship consortium
- **Year:** 2022 (Digital Compass); 2025 (6G Roadmap)
- **URL:** https://julkaisut.valtioneuvosto.fi/bitstreams/363d6643-0cf9-4c95-8fb1-aa67c9ec9858/download
- **Summary:** The Digital Compass aligns with the EU Digital Decade 2030 (skills, public-service digitalization, business transformation, secure infrastructure; universal 100 Mbps). Finland leads 6G globally via the 6G Flagship (University of Oulu — the world's first major 6G program); the 2025 6G Roadmap sets spectrum/security/standardization frameworks (with Nokia and Business Finland's 6G Bridge) to shape global 6G standards ahead of 2030s commercialization.

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** Finnish Drone Strategy (Kansallinen dronestrategia)
- **Body:** Finnish Defence and Aviation Industry Association (PIA), with Business Finland, VTT, government
- **Year:** 2025 (published February 10, 2025)
- **URL:** https://teknologiateollisuus.fi/pia/wp-content/uploads/sites/13/2025/03/Finnish-Drone-Strategy.pdf
- **Summary:** Aims to make Finland the leading global drone testbed and business environment by 2030 via a five-year national program (regulation, training, security of supply). Strong dual-use (civil/defense) emphasis given the Ukraine war and NATO accession; priorities include wide-area test infrastructure (incl. GPS-denied and 5G control zones), streamlined BVLOS permits, and urban air mobility. Funded via Business Finland and defense budgets; VTT integrates drones into healthcare logistics and infrastructure monitoring.
