# Oman (Sultanate of Oman) — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Entity 44 (Batch 9). Gulf.*

Oman is diversifying off hydrocarbons under **Oman Vision 2040**, with the MTCIT orchestrating the digital economy and the sovereign wealth fund (OIA) and its ICT arm (**ITHCA**) making targeted bets to localize complex industries. Distinctive Gulf positioning: Oman has *explicitly opted out* of nuclear power in favor of renewables and **green hydrogen (Hydrom)** + green steel; it is bootstrapping a fabless semiconductor industry (the "Oman-1/Oman-2" chips); and it favors regulatory agility (AI sandboxing, the "Serb" drone UTM platform). Quantum remains nascent, folded into the AI/cyber agenda.

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** Regulation of Radiation Protection and the Security and Safety of Radiation Sources and Nuclear Materials (Resolution No. 79/2023) *(explicitly no nuclear power)*
- **Body:** Environment Authority (EA)
- **Year:** 2023
- **URL:** https://www.ea.gov.om
- **Summary:** A unique GCC case — Oman has explicitly decided *against* domestic nuclear power plants or research reactors, judging its demand profile and renewables/green-hydrogen pivot to make nuclear unnecessary. Its policy is strictly non-power: regulating radioactive materials in medical, industrial, and research uses. The legal framework is distributed (anchored in the Environment Protection Law, RD 114/2001), with Resolution 79/2023 mandating licensing, air-only import/export, approved storage, a ban on burying imported radioactive waste, and the "justification of radiation practices" principle. Emphasis falls on NORM in the oil/gas sector (PDO's SP-1170) and medical cyclotrons/PET-CT at Sultan Qaboos University Hospital. Oman is party to 19 international nuclear-safety conventions and cooperates regionally (a 2023 MoU with Saudi Arabia's NRRC on early-warning monitoring and emergency preparedness).

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** National Programme for Artificial Intelligence and Advanced Digital Technologies (2024–2026) + National/General Policy for the Safe and Ethical Use of AI; Ma'een LLM; Oman Artificial Intelligence Zone
- **Body:** Ministry of Transport, Communications and Information Technology (MTCIT)
- **Year:** 2024 (programme); 2025 (ethical policy); 2025–2026 (AI Zone + updated programme phase)
- **URL:** https://mtcit.gov.om
- **Summary:** AI is treated as an existential diversification pillar. The National Programme sets KPIs to lift AI investment from $20M (2021) to $250M (2025) and $400M (2030), plus 20%/yr growth in AI research output. Oman invested ~OR 60M ($156M) in 2024 (establishing 22 AI enterprises) and OR 15M ($39M) in 2025. A digital-sovereignty centerpiece is **Ma'een**, Oman's first national LLM trained on Omani historical/cultural/scientific datasets for Arabic NLP and government decision-making, paired with a National AI Platform and the "Omani AI Studio." The 2025 ethical policy stresses data privacy and algorithmic justice; Oman is a UNESCO RAM pilot country. Partnerships include a WEF Fourth Industrial Revolution Centre (Muscat, 2025) and the OIA's December 2024 stake in Elon Musk's xAI. Sectoral wins: breast-cancer detection AI at 96% accuracy across five hospitals; computer vision in agriculture/logistics. At COMEX 2025 (September) MTCIT launched a **National Open Data Portal** alongside Ma'een and the Omani AI Studio, and in 2026 the government established the **Oman Artificial Intelligence Zone** in Muscat (administered by the Public Establishment for Special Economic Zones and Free Zones). An updated phase of the National AI Programme reportedly targets ~30,000 AI-focused jobs and a multi-billion-dollar economic impact by 2030, including a national AI supercomputer and expanded local LLM capacity.

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** Integrated within the National Programme for AI and Advanced Digital Technologies / 2026–2030 Digital Economy Roadmap *(no standalone quantum strategy)*
- **Body:** MTCIT
- **Year:** 2024/2026
- **URL:** https://mtcit.gov.om
- **Summary:** Highly nascent — no standalone national quantum strategy, but quantum is recognized as a frontier technology under the National AI Programme and tied to Vision 2040. The initial focus is capacity building, risk assessment, and academic exposure (the "AI and Quantum Computing Forum" at COMEX 2025 explored Quantum AI/QML as a future ICT building block). The 2026–2030 Digital Economy Roadmap formally adds "quantum computing readiness." Cybersecurity agencies are increasingly focused on "Q-Day" and "Harvest Now, Decrypt Later" threats to Oman's RSA/AES-protected financial and government networks (a low Q-Day preparedness score), so future mandates are expected to guide banking/telecom toward NIST post-quantum cryptography standards, likely via sandboxing. MTCIT's 2025/2026 digital-transformation roadmap floats a **Quantum Cybersecurity Centre** to apply post-quantum cryptography to critical national infrastructure, and the COMEX 2025 "AI and Quantum Computing Forum" (September 2025) became a recurring platform for quantum-readiness capacity building.

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** National Biotechnology Strategy (red/green/white biotech) *(under development)* + Health Vision 2050 for Health Research
- **Body:** Research and Innovation Authority (RIA) / Ministry of Higher Education, Research and Innovation (MoHERI); Ministry of Health (MoH) — Centre of Studies and Research
- **Year:** 2014 (Health Vision); 2025–2026 (Biotechnology Strategy in development)
- **URL:** https://ria.gov.om / https://www.mohcsr.gov.om
- **Summary:** The foundational text (built on 24 strategic studies) aims to make Oman a regional health-research leader, addressing historic gaps in research infrastructure and output. It sets national research priorities from epidemiological data, strengthens human capital/clinical-trial infrastructure, and translates genomic evidence into policy — driven by Oman's NCD burden (cardiovascular 33% of deaths, diabetes 10.2%, cancers 9.5%). Genomics focuses on localized genetic challenges: the National Program for the Control of Genetic Blood Disorders (premarital screening, counseling across eight hospitals) and growing investment in Next-Generation Sequencing, microarrays, and pharmacogenomics (Sultan Qaboos University). During COVID-19, the MoH's Central Public Health Laboratory served as a WHO regional reference lab. Health Technology Assessments govern cost-effective, ethical adoption. Crucially, Oman is now elevating biotech from a health-research adjunct to a standalone economic sector: in July 2025 MoHERI/RIA floated an international tender to build the executive roadmap and financial plans for **red, green, and white biotechnology**, and through 2025–2026 a Biotechnology Supervisory Committee has advanced the strategy in phases (a governance workshop in February 2026; its 15th meeting in April 2026 endorsing phase 3 and approving phase 4 field studies / biopharma feasibility), with a final draft targeted for end-2026. The MoH also signed a local-content agreement with Opal BioPharma (2025) to localize biopharmaceutical production.

## 5. Materials Science / Critical Minerals, Green Hydrogen & Green Steel
- **Plan:** Mineral Wealth Law (Royal Decree 19/2019) + Oman Green Hydrogen Strategy (Hydrom)
- **Body:** Ministry of Energy and Minerals (MEM) / Hydrom (National Hydrogen Orchestrator)
- **Year:** 2019 (Mineral Law); 2022 (Green Hydrogen Strategy)
- **URL:** https://hydrom.om
- **Summary:** One of Oman's most capitalized and legally mature domains. The 2019 Mineral Wealth Law streamlined concessions (exploration 1–3 yrs; exploitation 5 yrs; large concessions 20–30 yrs), set financial terms (≥1% project-cost guarantee, ≥5% production rental), and added stiff anti-illegal-mining penalties — accelerating copper (Al-Batinah) and chromite projects. The flagship is **green hydrogen**: Hydrom orchestrates land auctions and infrastructure to target 1–1.5 million tonnes/yr by 2030, leveraging solar/wind and deep-water ports (Duqm, Sohar). This converges into **green steel** — Oman has a fully integrated pelletizing→DRI→EAF chain (with Vale, Jindal) expanding by ~12.5 Mtpa DRI/HBI, with future DRI designed to run on green hydrogen for zero-carbon steel exports to the EU. A $1.6B, 100,000 t/yr **polysilicon plant** at Sohar Freezone (United Solar Holding) links Oman directly to the semiconductor/solar-PV supply chains. Hydrom continued execution through 2025–2026 with a third auction round and new fiscal incentives (showcased at the Green Hydrogen Summit Oman 2025), plus European offtake linkages (e.g., H2Global), to scale toward ~1.38 Mtpa of hydrogen by 2030.

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** National Semiconductor Ecosystem Strategy + OSAT investment framework + ITHCA Group ICT strategy
- **Body:** MTCIT / Oman Investment Authority (OIA) via ITHCA Group
- **Year:** 2025 (consultant procurement); 2026 (strategy launch + OSAT framework)
- **URL:** https://ithca.om / https://mtcit.gov.om
- **Summary:** Historically absent, Oman is executing an aggressive, well-funded push into a domestic semiconductor ecosystem — leveraging the OIA, geopolitical neutrality, and zero-income-tax incentives to become a regional fabless-design hub and eventually OSAT. In June 2025 the MTCIT began procuring a consultant for the National Semiconductor Ecosystem Strategy (including a Muscat "Centre of Excellence for Semiconductors"). The operational arm is **ITHCA** (OIA's ICT investor), under the "Omani Angle" tech-transfer philosophy: it invested OR 5M ($13M) in fabless GS Microelectronics (which opened Muscat's first IC design center, trained 100+ Omani engineers, and taped out the indigenous **Oman-1** and **Oman-2** chips); OR 7.7M ($20M) in Movandi (5G RF/antenna); $5M in Lumotive (programmable optics/LiDAR); and a VLSI-design partnership with India's Kaynes. Phase 1 is fabless design; Phase 2 envisions an OSAT hub (~$130–140M). Following the June 2025 consultant procurement, the **National Semiconductor Ecosystem Strategy** was unveiled at a 2026 Semiconductor Ecosystem forum/summit (SK Hynix, ULVAC, TSMC-ecosystem players), paired with an **Outsourced Semiconductor Assembly and Testing (OSAT) investment framework** and a Muscat "Centre of Excellence for Semiconductors." To draw investors, the package reportedly offers long-tenor (up to ~25-year) tax exemptions, free/subsidized premises, and multi-year salary subsidies for Omani hires.

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** National Digital Economy Programme (NDEP) 2021–2025 & 2026–2030 ("Harvesting the Digital Economy") + Government Digital Transformation Programme ("Tahawul") + Cloud Computing & Data-Centre Regulation
- **Body:** MTCIT / Telecommunications Regulatory Authority (TRA)
- **Year:** 2021 (launch); 2026 (2026–2030 roadmap unveiled); 2025 (cloud/data-centre regulation)
- **URL:** https://mtcit.gov.om / https://tra.gov.om
- **Summary:** Digital infrastructure is the bedrock of diversification. The NDEP (Oct 2021) targets lifting the digital economy's GDP share from 2% (2021) to 3% (2025), 5% (2030), and 10% (2040). Phase 1 results: ~OR 800M ($2B) GDP contribution (2023), 2,277+ government services digitized via **Tahawul** (11.4M digital transactions Jan–May 2025, ~80% completion). The 2026–2030 roadmap shifts to high-end infrastructure: governorate tech decentralization (11 centers), sovereign cloud + local server manufacturing, and mandatory e-invoicing (Fawtara). A physical centerpiece is the **Oman Digital Triangle** (with the IDCA) — three interconnected hubs for AI-scale, renewable-powered "green AI" data centers — plus the OR 150M ($390M) Green Data City for blockchain/crypto/AI processing. The 2026–2030 phase ("Harvesting the Digital Economy") was formally unveiled in 2026. Cloud and data-centre services are now governed by a dedicated TRA/MTCIT regulation (in force 2025) mandating licensing, cybersecurity standards, and fair-competition rules, underpinning a 2026 "Data Centres Establishment" investment drive to position Oman as a sovereign and regional cloud/AI-inference hub.

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** Executive Regulation No. 1412/2023 of the Civil Aviation Law / CAR 102 (Rev. 2); "Serb" UTM platform; Foundational Charter for the Advanced Air Mobility (AAM) System
- **Body:** Civil Aviation Authority (CAA)
- **Year:** 2023 (regulation); 2024 (Serb launch); 2025–2026 (Advanced Air Mobility)
- **URL:** https://caa.gov.om / https://flyserb.com
- **Summary:** Oman takes a progressive, regulated-integration approach to drones rather than blanket bans. The 2023 Executive Regulation 1412 + CAR 102 (Rev. 2) govern import, sale, ownership, and operation: retail outlets need commercial licenses; import/export needs CAA + security approval; ownership transfer must complete via a state digital portal before handover; operators must be 18+, pass a knowledge test, hold liability insurance, and get per-flight outdoor permits; BVLOS requires active tracking and collision avoidance. To manage airspace, the CAA granted Omani startup Tahleeq Drone Services an exclusive license for **Serb**, the national Unmanned Traffic Management (UTM) platform — handling digital registration, flight authorizations against No-Fly Zones, drone NOTAMs, and altitude limits for manned/unmanned deconfliction. The Firnas Academy (first CAA-authorized drone training center) builds certified commercial pilots. Oman is now extending this posture into **Advanced Air Mobility (AAM)**: in September 2025 the CAA and MTCIT signed a proof-of-concept agreement with Odys Aviation to trial hybrid-electric VTOL aircraft under the ICAO Specific Operations Risk Assessment (SORA) framework, and in 2026 the CAA's Advanced Air Mobility Forum adopted a **Foundational Charter for the Advanced Air Mobility System** — a unified government-private governance framework for drones and electric air taxis.
