# United Kingdom — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Country 9 (Batch 2). OECD bloc.*

The UK pursues "science and technology superpower" status, coordinated largely through the Department for Science, Innovation and Technology (DSIT). Approach: government as "anchor tenant" for public-private partnerships, regulatory sandboxes, and targeted funding. Strong cross-domain dependencies (AI compute → energy/nuclear; autonomy → wireless infrastructure).

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** Civil Nuclear: Roadmap to 2050
- **Body:** Department for Energy Security and Net Zero (DESNZ)
- **Year:** January 2024
- **URL:** https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/civil-nuclear-roadmap-to-2050
- **Summary:** Aims to quadruple civil nuclear capacity to 24 GW by 2050 (~25% of projected electricity demand). Commits up to £300M to domestically produce HALEU (breaking Russian dominance) plus £10M Nuclear Fuel Fund. Targets investment decisions for 3–7 GW of new projects every five years from 2030–2044, spanning gigawatt-scale plants and SMRs. The Office for Nuclear Regulation is streamlining its Generic Design Assessment to accelerate SMR/AMR licensing.

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** AI Opportunities Action Plan
- **Body:** DSIT
- **Year:** January 2025
- **URL:** https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/ai-opportunities-action-plan/ai-opportunities-action-plan
- **Summary:** A 50-point plan (led by Matt Clifford) to make the UK "an AI maker, not an AI taker," projecting +£400B economic growth by 2030. Scales the AI Research Resource (AIRR) compute ≥20x by 2030 and creates "AI Growth Zones" with fast-tracked planning and grid access (flagship: a 100MW→500MW data centre at Culham). Adds a national data library, lifelong-learning upskilling, NHS AI diagnostics, and up to £500M for a Sovereign AI Unit.

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** National Quantum Strategy (+ National Quantum Strategy Missions)
- **Body:** DSIT
- **Year:** March 2023 (Missions Oct 2023)
- **URL:** https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/national-quantum-strategy
- **Summary:** £2.5B public funding over ten years (2024–2034) to build a quantum-enabled economy, aiming to catalyze £1B private investment. Five time-bound missions, including a large-scale quantum network by 2035 and quantum-sensing in every NHS Trust by 2030. Computational target: accessible quantum computers running 1 trillion error-free operations by 2035. The National Quantum Computing Centre (NQCC) has funding through 2037.

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** National Vision for Engineering Biology (+ Life Sciences Vision)
- **Body:** DSIT / Office for Life Sciences
- **Year:** December 2023 (Engineering Biology); July 2021 (Life Sciences Vision)
- **URL:** https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/national-vision-for-engineering-biology
- **Summary:** Aims to capture economic value and security from biology-derived products, with six priorities: world-leading R&D, infrastructure, talent, regulation, economic integration, and responsible-innovation leadership by 2030. Treats engineering biology as the convergence of biosciences with engineering/computing/AI, serving both healthcare and net-zero (sustainable biomanufacturing). An Engineering Biology Steering Group unites academia and startups; aligns with the Life Sciences Vision for clinical pull-through (cancer, dementia).

## 5. Materials Science / Critical Minerals
- **Plan:** UK Critical Minerals Strategy: Vision 2035
- **Body:** Department for Business and Trade (DBT) / DSIT
- **Year:** November 2025 (updated Jan 2026)
- **URL:** https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/uk-critical-minerals-strategy/vision-2035-critical-minerals-strategy
- **Summary:** A 10-year framework to secure critical raw materials for clean energy, defense, and advanced manufacturing, backed by up to £50M. Targets ≥10% of annual industrial demand via domestic production (incl. 50,000 tonnes of lithium by 2035) and 20% via recycling of end-of-life products. Introduces a cap so no single foreign country supplies >60% of any individual critical mineral. Prioritizes the "midstream" (processing, refining, recycling) where the UK has an edge.

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** National Semiconductor Strategy
- **Body:** DSIT
- **Year:** May 2023
- **URL:** https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/national-semiconductor-strategy
- **Summary:** A niche strategy (not competing with US/EU mega-fab subsidies) focused on UK strengths: compound semiconductors, R&D, and IP/design (e.g. ARM, photonics, advanced packaging). Commits £200M (2023–25) and up to £1B over a decade via the UK Semiconductor Infrastructure Initiative, lowering barriers for startups to access prototyping tools and IP. Three objectives: grow the sector, protect supply chains, and safeguard national security (linked to the National Security and Investment Act).

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** UK Wireless Infrastructure Strategy
- **Body:** DSIT
- **Year:** April 2023
- **URL:** https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/uk-wireless-infrastructure-strategy/uk-wireless-infrastructure-strategy
- **Summary:** Targets nationwide standalone 5G across all populated areas by 2030 and 4G across 95% of UK landmass. Allocates £40M for "5G Innovation Regions" (using local public services as anchor tenants to de-risk private investment) and £100M for 6G R&D to shape future standards. Complements Project Gigabit and the Shared Rural Network; standalone 5G enables network slicing for industrial IoT. Drives supply-chain diversification (Open RAN) post-Huawei.

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** Transport AI Action Plan (+ Automated Vehicles Act, Advanced Manufacturing Sector Plan)
- **Body:** Department for Transport (DfT) / DSIT
- **Year:** June 2025 (Transport AI Action Plan); 2024 (AV Act)
- **URL:** https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/transport-artificial-intelligence-action-plan
- **Summary:** Targets a £42B domestic self-driving market and 38,000 jobs by 2035. The Automated Vehicles Act gives a legal framework for self-driving without safety drivers; the Advanced Manufacturing Sector Plan allocates £40M for a UK-wide network of Robotics Adoption Hubs. Combines £2.8B targeted R&D with regulatory sandboxes, overseen by the Centre for Connected and Autonomous Vehicles (CCAV) and the new Regulatory Innovation Office (RIO), covering AVs, drones, and robotic logistics.
