# United Kingdom — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Country 9 (Batch 2). OECD bloc.*

The UK pursues "science and technology superpower" status, coordinated largely through the Department for Science, Innovation and Technology (DSIT). Approach: government as "anchor tenant" for public-private partnerships, regulatory sandboxes, and targeted funding. Strong cross-domain dependencies (AI compute → energy/nuclear; autonomy → wireless infrastructure).

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** Civil Nuclear: Roadmap to 2050 (+ Rolls-Royce SMR technology selection)
- **Body:** Department for Energy Security and Net Zero (DESNZ) / Great British Energy – Nuclear (GBE-N)
- **Year:** January 2024 (Roadmap); 10 June 2025 (SMR selection)
- **URL:** https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/civil-nuclear-roadmap-to-2050 ; https://www.gov.uk/government/news/rolls-royce-smr-selected-to-build-small-modular-nuclear-reactors
- **Summary:** The Roadmap aims to quadruple civil nuclear capacity to 24 GW by 2050 (~25% of projected electricity demand), commits up to £300M to domestically produce HALEU, and targets investment decisions for 3–7 GW every five years from 2030–2044. In a major delivery step, on 10 June 2025 the government selected Rolls-Royce SMR as preferred bidder to partner Great British Energy – Nuclear (the rebranded Great British Nuclear) on the UK's first fleet of small modular reactors, committing over £2.5B across the spending-review period and targeting ~3 million homes and up to 3,000 peak jobs, with grid connection in the mid-2030s. The contract was signed and Defra's regulatory justification approved in 2026, moving the SMR programme from competition to deployment. The Office for Nuclear Regulation continues to streamline its Generic Design Assessment to accelerate SMR/AMR licensing.

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** AI Opportunities Action Plan (+ AI Opportunities Action Plan: One Year On; Sovereign AI)
- **Body:** DSIT
- **Year:** January 2025 (Action Plan); 29 January 2026 (One Year On); April 2026 (first Sovereign AI backing)
- **URL:** https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/ai-opportunities-action-plan/ai-opportunities-action-plan ; https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/ai-opportunities-action-plan-one-year-on
- **Summary:** A 50-point plan (led by Matt Clifford) to make the UK "an AI maker, not an AI taker," projecting +£400B economic growth by 2030, scaling AI Research Resource (AIRR) compute ≥20x by 2030 and creating "AI Growth Zones" with fast-tracked planning and grid access. The "One Year On" progress report (29 January 2026) reported the government had met its commitments on 38 of the 50 actions, organised around laying foundations, embracing AI, and securing the future with homegrown AI. AI Growth Zones were formally designated from November 2025 (including North Wales, South Wales, the North East and Oxfordshire/Culham). The £500M Sovereign AI programme became operational, making its first backing of UK AI companies in April 2026 with early-stage capital (£1m–£10m), up to ~1 million GPU hours per startup, fast-track visas, and government procurement.

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** National Quantum Strategy (+ National Quantum Strategy Missions)
- **Body:** DSIT
- **Year:** March 2023 (Missions Oct 2023)
- **URL:** https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/national-quantum-strategy
- **Summary:** £2.5B public funding over ten years (2024–2034) to build a quantum-enabled economy, aiming to catalyze £1B private investment. Five time-bound missions, including a large-scale quantum network by 2035 and quantum-sensing in every NHS Trust by 2030. Computational target: accessible quantum computers running 1 trillion error-free operations by 2035. The National Quantum Computing Centre (NQCC) has funding through 2037.

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** Life Sciences Sector Plan (+ National Vision for Engineering Biology)
- **Body:** DBT / DHSC / DSIT / Office for Life Sciences (OLS)
- **Year:** 16 July 2025 (Life Sciences Sector Plan); December 2023 (Engineering Biology)
- **URL:** https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/life-sciences-sector-plan ; https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/national-vision-for-engineering-biology
- **Summary:** The Life Sciences Sector Plan, a pillar of the 2025 Modern Industrial Strategy, supersedes the 2021 Life Sciences Vision as the headline biomedical strategy, aiming to make the UK Europe's leading life sciences economy by 2030 and third globally (behind the US and China) by 2035. It sets targeted actions on world-class R&D, investment attraction, advanced manufacturing, and faster health innovation, including a Health Data Research Service (HDRS) unifying data assets across Secure Data Environments and going live in 2026. The National Vision for Engineering Biology remains the convergence agenda (biosciences with engineering/computing/AI), and the 2026 Digital and Technologies Sector Plan update earmarked new funding such as ~£196M for the National Engineering Biology Programme and ~£184M for scale-up infrastructure. Together these serve both healthcare pull-through (cancer, dementia) and net-zero biomanufacturing.

## 5. Materials Science / Critical Minerals
- **Plan:** UK Critical Minerals Strategy: Vision 2035
- **Body:** Department for Business and Trade (DBT) / DSIT
- **Year:** November 2025 (updated Jan 2026)
- **URL:** https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/uk-critical-minerals-strategy/vision-2035-critical-minerals-strategy
- **Summary:** A 10-year framework to secure critical raw materials for clean energy, defense, and advanced manufacturing, backed by up to £50M. Targets ≥10% of annual industrial demand via domestic production (incl. 50,000 tonnes of lithium by 2035) and 20% via recycling of end-of-life products. Introduces a cap so no single foreign country supplies >60% of any individual critical mineral. Prioritizes the "midstream" (processing, refining, recycling) where the UK has an edge.

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** National Semiconductor Strategy (+ £1.1B AI hardware/chip plan & Semiconductor Sector Study 2026)
- **Body:** DSIT
- **Year:** May 2023 (Strategy); 8 June 2026 (£1.1B plan / Sector Study)
- **URL:** https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/national-semiconductor-strategy ; https://www.gov.uk/government/news/a-decisive-shift-to-power-british-ai-new-11-billion-plan-to-back-chip-firms-boost-computing-power-and-skills-for-the-ai-revolution
- **Summary:** The 2023 niche strategy (not competing with US/EU mega-fab subsidies) targets UK strengths in compound semiconductors, R&D, and IP/design (ARM, photonics, advanced packaging), committing £200M (2023–25) and up to £1B over a decade via the UK Semiconductor Infrastructure Initiative. On 8 June 2026 the government announced a £1.1B plan tilting the strategy toward AI hardware: ~£750M for a national AI supercomputer by 2030 using heterogeneous British-designed chips, ~£400M for next-generation AI chips (incl. £150M to buy novel chips from UK startups), ~£120M for an AI Hardware Innovation Programme, and ~£45M for skills, plus a British Business Bank-backed investment fund. It also launched the UK Semiconductor Centre (UKSC) and was accompanied by the Semiconductor Sector Study 2026, reinforcing clusters in South Wales and the North East. The three original objectives—grow the sector, protect supply chains, safeguard national security (linked to the NSI Act)—remain.

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** Digital and Technologies Sector Plan (+ UK Wireless Infrastructure Strategy)
- **Body:** Department for Business and Trade (DBT) / DSIT
- **Year:** 23 June 2025 (Sector Plan, updated 5 Aug 2025); April 2023 (Wireless Infrastructure Strategy)
- **URL:** https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/digital-and-technologies-sector-plan ; https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/uk-wireless-infrastructure-strategy/uk-wireless-infrastructure-strategy
- **Summary:** The Digital and Technologies Sector Plan is the new flagship digital strategy and a central pillar of the 2025 Modern Industrial Strategy, casting government as an active partner to scale the UK tech ecosystem across six prioritised frontier technologies (AI, quantum, advanced connectivity, cybersecurity, semiconductors, engineering biology) with a "Year One Update" in June 2026. It builds on the underlying UK Wireless Infrastructure Strategy, which targets nationwide standalone 5G across all populated areas by 2030 and 4G across 95% of landmass, with £40M for "5G Innovation Regions" and £100M for 6G R&D. In 2026 DSIT also launched a Global Coalition on Telecoms 6G security-and-resilience principles (with seven partner nations) and a Digital Standards Strategy (2026–2030) to shape global technical standards. Together with Project Gigabit, the Shared Rural Network, and Open RAN supply-chain diversification, the package aims at a highly competitive regulatory environment for startups and spinouts.

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** Transport AI Action Plan + Automated Vehicles Act (+ self-driving passenger services scheme; Future of Flight)
- **Body:** Department for Transport (DfT) / DSIT / Centre for Connected and Autonomous Vehicles (CCAV)
- **Year:** June 2025 (Transport AI Action Plan); 2024 (AV Act); July 2025 (passenger services scheme); 2026 (first commercial services)
- **URL:** https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/transport-artificial-intelligence-action-plan ; https://www.gov.uk/government/news/next-steps-for-self-driving-vehicles-as-future-passengers-help-shape-self-driving-vehicles-law
- **Summary:** Targets a £42B domestic self-driving market and 38,000 jobs by 2035, with the Automated Vehicles Act providing the legal framework for self-driving without safety drivers and the Advanced Manufacturing Sector Plan funding Robotics Adoption Hubs. In July 2025 CCAV set out an automated passenger services permitting scheme and consultation on AV law, clearing the way for the first commercial self-driving services on UK roads in spring 2026. A 2026 Future of Flight package added ~£50M for drones and advanced air mobility (incl. ~£26M to accelerate drone deliveries and electric air taxis, and ~£20.5M for a drone identification/"numberplate" system). The programme combines £2.8B targeted R&D with regulatory sandboxes, overseen by CCAV and the Regulatory Innovation Office (RIO), covering AVs, drones, and robotic logistics.
