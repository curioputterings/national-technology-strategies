# Norway — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Entity 32 (Batch 7). OECD/EEA/Nordic.*

Norway runs a coordinated, publicly funded "Nordic model" of innovation, aligning with the EU twin transition (green + digital) while leveraging vast resource wealth (hydropower, marine biomass, minerals). Distinctive: historically no nuclear power (90% hydro) but now formally assessing SMRs; a world-leading maritime-autonomy ecosystem; and a controversial move to open its continental shelf to seabed mining.

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** Mandate for the Nuclear Energy Commission (Kjernekraftutvalget) + Impact Assessment Program for the Aure/Heim SMR plant. *(No finalized national nuclear strategy — Norway is historically non-nuclear.)*
- **Body:** Ministry of Energy (with Health and Climate ministries)
- **Year:** 2024 (Commission); February 2026 (impact-assessment approval)
- **URL:** https://www.regjeringen.no/no/aktuelt/konsekvensutgreiingsprogram-for-kjernekraftverk-i-aure-og-heim-kommunar/id3148890/
- **Summary:** Norway has long had a strict no-nuclear stance (≈90% hydropower; research reactors at Halden/Kjeller now being decommissioned). Rising demand (AI data centers, electrification) reignited the debate, focused on SMRs. The 2024 Nuclear Energy Commission assesses feasibility; private firm Norsk Kjernekraft (with Trondheimsleia Kjernekraft) targets up to 1,500 MW at Taftøy — the government approved the impact-assessment program (Feb 2026), a historic first licensing step, but commits no state construction funding. Even the maritime sector is exploring SMR propulsion (DNV Approval in Principle).

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** National Strategy for Artificial Intelligence + the "KI-milliarden" (AI Billion) research initiative
- **Body:** Ministry of Digitalisation and Public Governance; Research Council of Norway
- **Year:** 2020 (strategy); 2024–2025 (AI Billion rollout)
- **URL:** https://www.regjeringen.no/no/dokumenter/nasjonal-strategi-for-kunstig-intelligens/id2685594/
- **Summary:** Positions Norway as a leader in ethical/trustworthy AI (aligned with OECD principles), leveraging high societal trust and open public data ("as open as possible, as closed as necessary"). The "KI-milliarden" commits 1B+ NOK over five years; in June 2025 the Research Council funded six national AI centers (hybrid intelligence, trustworthy AI, creative-industry AI, embodied AI/robotics). Builds sovereign Norwegian language models; the EU AI Act enters Norwegian law in 2026; Datatilsynet runs an AI regulatory sandbox.

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** National Strategy for Quantum Technology (under development)
- **Body:** Ministries of Trade/Industry/Fisheries, Education & Research, Defence; drafted by Research Council, Innovation Norway, National Security Authority
- **Year:** Announced May 2025; final strategy 2026
- **URL:** https://www.regjeringen.no/no/aktuelt/na-starter-arbeidet-med-nasjonal-strategi-for-kvanteteknologi/id3100025/
- **Summary:** Norway has lagged its Nordic neighbors on quantum; the forthcoming strategy aims for a coordinated national approach to computing, communication, and sensing, intersecting innovation with national security. Funding for quantum research rose by 70M NOK/yr from 2025. Leverages the "Nordic Quantum Network" (e.g. Niels Bohr Institute) to offset a small domestic base; University of Bergen launches Norway's first quantum-engineering MSc in 2026.

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** National Strategy for Biotechnology 2011–2020 + Centre for Digital Life Norway (DLN, current hub)
- **Body:** Ministry of Education and Research; Research Council of Norway (funds DLN)
- **Year:** 2011 (strategy); DLN 2016–2026 (now DLN 2.0)
- **URL:** https://www.digitallifenorway.org/
- **Summary:** Driven by marine/agricultural biomass and a strong public health system. The DLN is the national transdisciplinary hub (40+ projects across NTNU, UiO, UiB, NMBU, UiT, SINTEF) marrying biotech with AI/big data/bioinformatics. Emphasizes "Responsible Research and Innovation" (RRI), precision medicine via Norway's health registries, sustainable aquaculture, and marine bioprospecting (new enzymes, nutrition, pharma). (Builds on the earlier 1.6B NOK FUGE program.)

## 5. Materials Science / Critical Minerals & Seabed Mining
- **Plan:** Norway's Mineral Strategy + White Paper on opening the continental shelf / Seabed Minerals Act
- **Body:** Ministry of Trade, Industry and Fisheries; Ministry of Energy (seabed)
- **Year:** June 2023 (Mineral Strategy); 2024 (continental-shelf opening)
- **URL:** https://www.regjeringen.no/contentassets/1614eb7b10cd4a7cb58fa6245159a547/no/pdfs/norges-mineralstrategi.pdf
- **Summary:** Aims to build "the world's most sustainable mineral industry" via fast-track permitting for critical terrestrial minerals and mandatory circular-economy plans. Most scrutinized: in 2024 Norway opened parts of its continental shelf to seabed mineral exploration (sulphides, ferromanganese crusts) for strategic autonomy vs. China — committing 150M NOK to environmental/resource mapping (NGU, Offshore Directorate). Highly controversial (WWF lawsuit); proceeding via a step-by-step precautionary approach.

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** EU Chips Act implementation via CC-NorChip (national competence center for chips and sensors)
- **Body:** Research Council of Norway; co-funded by the EU Chips Joint Undertaking (SINTEF, NTNU, UiO, UiT, USN, Electronic Coast)
- **Year:** 2024–2025 (CC-NorChip established; EU Chips Act 2023)
- **URL:** https://www.ccnorchip.no/en/om
- **Summary:** As an EEA member, Norway plugs into the EU Chips Act (20% global share by 2030). Niche strengths in sensors, microelectronics design, and piezoMEMS. CC-NorChip (~90M NOK; 44M from the EU) connects Norwegian SMEs to European pilot lines, design platforms, and Chips JU funding, and joins the "Nordic Chip Collaboration" (with Finland, Sweden, Denmark) to build a unified regional value proposition.

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** "The Digital Norway of the Future" — National Digitalisation Strategy 2024–2030
- **Body:** Ministry of Digitalisation and Public Governance
- **Year:** September 2024
- **URL:** https://www.regjeringen.no/contentassets/c499c3b6c93740bd989c43d886f65924/no/pdfs/nasjonal-digitaliseringsstrategi_ny.pdf
- **Summary:** Already among the world's most digitalized societies (BankID, Altinn); aims to be the world's most digitalized country by 2030. Frames digitalization as a tool for demographic/climate challenges, prioritizing "personnel-saving" automation amid labor shortages. Expands secure 5G/broadband nationwide, hardens cybersecurity against hybrid threats/disinformation, incentivizes public-data sharing ("fuel" of the digital economy), and plans a Data Centre Strategy for data sovereignty (aligned with the EU Digital Decade).

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** Drone Strategy for the Defence Sector + SFI AutoShip / MV Yara Birkeland (maritime autonomy)
- **Body:** Ministry of Defence (drones); Research Council of Norway (SFI AutoShip)
- **Year:** December 2025 (defence drones); SFI AutoShip ongoing
- **URL:** https://www.regjeringen.no (Dronestrategi for forsvarssektoren); https://www.sfiautoship.no
- **Summary:** A global frontrunner, bifurcated into defense and maritime. The 2025 Defence Drone Strategy (shaped by Ukraine) integrates UAVs/USVs/UUVs across the military with four priorities (counter-drone, tech adoption, civil/allied cooperation, expertise); AI swarming tested in the Arctic. In the civilian sphere, the MV Yara Birkeland is the world's first fully electric, autonomous container ship (Kongsberg-equipped), cutting 40,000 diesel truck trips/yr — underpinned by SFI AutoShip and the 2026 zero-emission mandate for World Heritage Fjords.
