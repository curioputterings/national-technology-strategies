# Norway — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Entity 32 (Batch 7). OECD/EEA/Nordic.*

Norway runs a coordinated, publicly funded "Nordic model" of innovation, aligning with the EU twin transition (green + digital) while leveraging vast resource wealth (hydropower, marine biomass, minerals). Distinctive: historically no nuclear power (90% hydro) but now formally assessing SMRs; a world-leading maritime-autonomy ecosystem; and a controversial move to open its continental shelf to seabed mining.

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** Nuclear Power Commission report **NOU 2026:4 "Kjernekraft i Norge? Fordeler, ulemper og forutsetninger"** + Impact Assessment Program for the Aure/Heim SMR plant. *(Still no government decision to build — Norway is historically non-nuclear.)*
- **Body:** Ministry of Energy (Kjernekraftutvalget, led by Kristin Halvorsen; with Health and Climate ministries)
- **Year:** 2024 (Commission appointed); February 2026 (impact-assessment approval); 8 April 2026 (NOU report)
- **URL:** https://www.regjeringen.no/no/dokumenter/nou-2026-4/id3155361/
- **Summary:** Norway has long had a strict no-nuclear stance (≈90% hydropower; research reactors at Halden/Kjeller now being decommissioned). Rising demand (AI data centers, electrification) reignited the debate, focused on SMRs. On 8 April 2026 the Kjernekraftutvalget delivered NOU 2026:4 "Kjernekraft i Norge?" to the Ministry of Energy, the first comprehensive official assessment of nuclear's feasibility, costs, safety and regulatory prerequisites. Private firm Norsk Kjernekraft (with Trondheimsleia Kjernekraft) targets up to 1,500 MW at Taftøy (Aure/Heim) — the government approved the impact-assessment program (Feb 2026), a historic first licensing step, but commits no state construction funding. Even the maritime sector is exploring SMR propulsion (DNV Approval in Principle).

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** National Strategy for Artificial Intelligence + the "KI-milliarden" (AI Billion) research initiative + the new **"KI Norge"** national AI hub
- **Body:** Ministry of Digitalisation and Public Governance; Research Council of Norway; Norwegian Digitalisation Agency
- **Year:** 2020 (strategy); 2024–2025 (AI Billion rollout); June 2026 (KI Norge launch)
- **URL:** https://www.regjeringen.no/no/dokumenter/nasjonal-strategi-for-kunstig-intelligens/id2685594/ ; https://www.regjeringen.no/no/aktuelt/slik-blir-ki-norge-samler-krefter-for-ansvarlig-og-innovativ-kunstig-intelligens/id3165132/
- **Summary:** Positions Norway as a leader in ethical/trustworthy AI (aligned with OECD principles), leveraging high societal trust and open public data ("as open as possible, as closed as necessary"). The "KI-milliarden" commits 1B+ NOK over five years; in June 2025 the Research Council funded six national AI centers (hybrid intelligence, trustworthy AI, creative-industry AI, embodied AI/robotics). On 12 June 2026 the government launched **KI Norge**, a national spearhead and advisory hub (under the Norwegian Digitalisation Agency) to help public and private bodies adopt AI safely and responsibly. Builds sovereign Norwegian language models; the EU AI Act enters Norwegian law in 2026; Datatilsynet runs an AI regulatory sandbox.

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** National Strategy for Quantum Technology (under development) + four new national quantum research centres
- **Body:** Ministries of Education & Research and Defence (and Trade/Industry/Fisheries); Research Council, Innovation Norway, National Security Authority
- **Year:** Announced May 2025; centres awarded December 2025; final strategy due before end of 2026
- **URL:** https://www.regjeringen.no/no/aktuelt/na-starter-arbeidet-med-nasjonal-strategi-for-kvanteteknologi/id3100025/ ; https://www.regjeringen.no/no/aktuelt/noreg-far-fire-nye-forskingssenter-for-kvanteteknologi/id3143328/
- **Summary:** Norway has lagged its Nordic neighbors on quantum; the forthcoming strategy aims for a coordinated national approach to computing, communication, and sensing, intersecting innovation with national security. On 17 December 2025 the government committed at least 1.1 billion NOK to quantum research over five years and established four new national research centres (≈61M NOK each, spanning quantum computing, sensing and communication) — a concrete pre-emptive down-payment ahead of the strategy. Leverages the "Nordic Quantum Network" (e.g. Niels Bohr Institute) to offset a small domestic base; University of Bergen launches Norway's first quantum-engineering MSc in 2026.

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** National Strategy for Biotechnology 2011–2020 + Centre for Digital Life Norway (DLN, current hub)
- **Body:** Ministry of Education and Research; Research Council of Norway (funds DLN)
- **Year:** 2011 (strategy); DLN 2016–2026 (now DLN 2.0)
- **URL:** https://www.digitallifenorway.org/
- **Summary:** Driven by marine/agricultural biomass and a strong public health system. The DLN is the national transdisciplinary hub (40+ projects across NTNU, UiO, UiB, NMBU, UiT, SINTEF) marrying biotech with AI/big data/bioinformatics. Emphasizes "Responsible Research and Innovation" (RRI), precision medicine via Norway's health registries, sustainable aquaculture, and marine bioprospecting (new enzymes, nutrition, pharma). (Builds on the earlier 1.6B NOK FUGE program.)

## 5. Materials Science / Critical Minerals & Seabed Mining
- **Plan:** Norway's Mineral Strategy + White Paper on opening the continental shelf / Seabed Minerals Act (first licensing round now paused)
- **Body:** Ministry of Trade, Industry and Fisheries; Ministry of Energy (seabed)
- **Year:** June 2023 (Mineral Strategy); 2024 (continental-shelf opening); December 2025 (licensing pause)
- **URL:** https://www.regjeringen.no/contentassets/1614eb7b10cd4a7cb58fa6245159a547/no/pdfs/norges-mineralstrategi.pdf ; https://www.regjeringen.no/no/aktuelt/horing-av-forste-konsesjonsrunde-for-havbunnsmineraler/id3047008/
- **Summary:** Aims to build "the world's most sustainable mineral industry" via fast-track permitting for critical terrestrial minerals and mandatory circular-economy plans. Most scrutinized: in 2024 Norway opened parts of its continental shelf to seabed mineral exploration (sulphides, ferromanganese crusts) for strategic autonomy vs. China — committing 150M NOK to environmental/resource mapping. In a major reversal, after the 2026 state-budget negotiations the government confirmed (Dec 2025) it would **not** issue the planned first seabed-minerals licensing round during this parliamentary term (to ~2029), effectively pausing deep-sea mining. Terrestrial critical minerals remain a priority (e.g. the Fensfeltet rare-earth deposit, Europe's largest); the seabed move had drawn a WWF lawsuit and heavy criticism.

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** EU Chips Act implementation via CC-NorChip (national competence center for chips and sensors)
- **Body:** Research Council of Norway; co-funded by the EU Chips Joint Undertaking (SINTEF, NTNU, UiO, UiT, USN, Electronic Coast)
- **Year:** 2024–2025 (CC-NorChip established; EU Chips Act 2023)
- **URL:** https://www.ccnorchip.no/en/om
- **Summary:** As an EEA member, Norway plugs into the EU Chips Act (20% global share by 2030). Niche strengths in sensors, microelectronics design, and piezoMEMS. CC-NorChip (~90M NOK; 44M from the EU) connects Norwegian SMEs to European pilot lines, design platforms, and Chips JU funding, and joins the "Nordic Chip Collaboration" (with Finland, Sweden, Denmark) to build a unified regional value proposition.

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** "The Digital Norway of the Future" — National Digitalisation Strategy 2024–2030
- **Body:** Ministry of Digitalisation and Public Governance
- **Year:** September 2024
- **URL:** https://www.regjeringen.no/contentassets/c499c3b6c93740bd989c43d886f65924/no/pdfs/nasjonal-digitaliseringsstrategi_ny.pdf
- **Summary:** Already among the world's most digitalized societies (BankID, Altinn); aims to be the world's most digitalized country by 2030. Frames digitalization as a tool for demographic/climate challenges, prioritizing "personnel-saving" automation amid labor shortages. Expands secure 5G/broadband nationwide, hardens cybersecurity against hybrid threats/disinformation, incentivizes public-data sharing ("fuel" of the digital economy), and plans a Data Centre Strategy for data sovereignty (aligned with the EU Digital Decade).

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** Drone Strategy for the Defence Sector + SFI AutoShip / MV Yara Birkeland (maritime autonomy)
- **Body:** Ministry of Defence (drones); Research Council of Norway (SFI AutoShip)
- **Year:** December 2025 (defence drones); SFI AutoShip ongoing
- **URL:** https://www.regjeringen.no/en/documents/drone-strategy-for-the-defence-sector/id3141461/ ; https://www.sfiautoship.no
- **Summary:** A global frontrunner, bifurcated into defense and maritime. The 2025 Defence Drone Strategy (shaped by Ukraine) integrates UAVs/USVs/UUVs across the military with four priorities (counter-drone, tech adoption, civil/allied cooperation, expertise); AI swarming tested in the Arctic. In the civilian sphere, the MV Yara Birkeland is the world's first fully electric, autonomous container ship (Kongsberg-equipped), cutting 40,000 diesel truck trips/yr — underpinned by SFI AutoShip and the 2026 zero-emission mandate for World Heritage Fjords.
