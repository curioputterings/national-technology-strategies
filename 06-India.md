# India — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Country 6 of 6 (added by request).*

India's strategy is framed by "Aatmanirbhar Bharat" (Self-Reliant India) and "Viksit Bharat 2047" (Developed India by 2047), pursuing technological sovereignty via large mission-mode programs with substantial budgets. The eight domains are deliberately interlinked (AI/quantum depend on semiconductors and critical minerals).

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** Nuclear Energy Mission (enabled by the SHANTI Act, 2025 — Sustainable Harnessing and Advancement of Nuclear Energy for Transforming India)
- **Body:** Department of Atomic Energy (DAE), with Ministry of Finance / PMO
- **Year:** Announced Union Budget 2025–26 (Feb 2025); SHANTI Act passed Dec 2025
- **URL:** https://www.pib.gov.in/FactsheetDetails.aspx?Id=150617&reg=3&lang=3
- **Summary:** Targets 100 GW nuclear capacity by 2047 (a ~12x increase) to support net-zero by 2070. Dedicates ₹20,000 cr for indigenous Small Modular Reactors, aiming to operationalize ≥5 SMRs by 2033 (BSMR-200, SMR-55, an HTGR for hydrogen). The SHANTI Act repeals the 1962 Atomic Energy Act and 2010 Civil Liability Act, opening the sector to private/foreign participation, capping operator liability at 300M SDR, and seeking to mobilize ₹15–20 lakh cr in private capital. India's 500 MWe Prototype Fast Breeder Reactor (Kalpakkam) reached first criticality April 2026.

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** IndiaAI Mission
- **Body:** Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY)
- **Year:** Approved by Union Cabinet March 7, 2024
- **URL:** https://indiaai.gov.in/
- **Summary:** ₹10,371.92 cr (~$1.2B) over five years across seven pillars (Compute, Innovation Centre, Datasets, Application Development, FutureSkills, Startup Financing, Safe & Trusted AI). Centerpiece: 10,000+ GPUs via public-private partnership for affordable compute. Prioritizes indigenous multilingual/multimodal foundation models (integrating Bhashini) and a national datasets platform (AIKosh). Light-touch, risk-based governance; ethos "make AI in India and make AI work for India."

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** National Quantum Mission (NQM)
- **Body:** Department of Science and Technology (DST)
- **Year:** Approved April 19, 2023 (2023–24 to 2030–31); T-Hubs announced Sept 2024
- **URL:** https://dst.gov.in/national-quantum-mission-nqm
- **Summary:** ₹6,003.65 cr over 2023–31. Hub-and-spoke model with four Thematic Hubs: Quantum Computing (IISc Bengaluru), Communication (IIT Madras + C-DOT), Sensing & Metrology (IIT Bombay), Materials & Devices (IIT Delhi). Targets intermediate-scale quantum computers of 50–1,000 physical qubits within eight years, 2,000 km satellite-based quantum communication, inter-city QKD networks (ISRO developing QKD satellites), and atomic clocks/magnetometers.

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** BioE3 Policy (Biotechnology for Economy, Environment and Employment)
- **Body:** Department of Biotechnology (DBT)
- **Year:** Approved by Union Cabinet August 2024
- **URL:** https://bmi.dbt.gov.in/
- **Summary:** Treats biotech as a core industrial capability for a circular bioeconomy and net-zero. Six verticals: bio-based chemicals/enzymes; functional foods & smart proteins; precision biotherapeutics (vaccines, gene therapy, biologics); climate-resilient agriculture; carbon capture & utilization; marine/space biotech. Integrates "Bio-AI"; establishes biomanufacturing hubs, biofoundries, and Bio-AI hubs in tier-II/III cities for regional employment.

## 5. Materials Science / Critical Minerals
- **Plan:** National Critical Mineral Mission (NCMM)
- **Body:** Ministry of Mines / Union Cabinet
- **Year:** Announced Budget July 2024; approved Jan 29, 2025 (FY2024-25 to 2030-31)
- **URL:** https://mines.gov.in/admin/storage/ckeditor/NCMM_1739251643.pdf
- **Summary:** ₹34,300 cr over seven years (₹16,300 cr government + ₹18,000 cr PSU investment) to cut import dependency across 24 critical minerals (India imports >80% of needs). Plans 1,200 domestic exploration projects, 100+ block auctions, and overseas asset acquisition. Includes ₹1,500 cr for a 270 kilo-ton/yr recycling capacity (e-waste, EV batteries), a national stockpile, 7 Centres of Excellence, and 1,000 patents by 2030. Built on the 2023 MMDR Act amendment.

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** India Semiconductor Mission (ISM) / Program for Development of Semiconductor and Display Ecosystem
- **Body:** MeitY / Digital India Corporation
- **Year:** Launched Dec 2021 (ISM 1.0); ISM 2.0 parameters through 2024–25
- **URL:** https://ism.gov.in/
- **Summary:** ₹76,000 cr (~$10B) to make India a chip/display manufacturing hub, offering up to 50% of capex for fabs, display fabs, compound semiconductors, and ATMP/OSAT. ISM 1.0 catalyzed Tata Electronics' ~$10B fab (Gujarat, ~80,000 wafers/month), Micron's $2.75B ATMP, and plants in Assam/UP. ISM 2.0 expands to upstream equipment, materials, and Design IP; the Design Linked Incentive (DLI) scheme supports indigenous IC/SoC design.

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** Digital India Programme (Expanded)
- **Body:** MeitY
- **Year:** Launched July 1, 2015; five-year expansion approved Aug 2023
- **URL:** https://www.digitalindia.gov.in/
- **Summary:** Foundation of India's Digital Public Infrastructure ("India Stack": Aadhaar, UPI/BHIM, DigiLocker, UMANG); internet connections grew from 25 cr (2014) to ~97 cr (2024). The 2023 expansion (₹14,903 cr over five years) re-skills 6.25 lakh IT professionals (FutureSkills Prime), trains 2.65 lakh in cybersecurity, deploys 9 supercomputers (National Supercomputing Mission), expands Bhashini to all 22 official languages, and sustains BharatNet rural broadband and PMGDISHA digital literacy.

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** Draft National Strategy on Robotics (NSR) + Production-Linked Incentive (PLI) Scheme for Drones and Drone Components
- **Body:** MeitY (robotics); Ministry of Civil Aviation (drones)
- **Year:** Drone PLI notified Sept 30, 2021; Draft NSR Sept 2023 (revised Feb 2024)
- **URL:** https://www.meity.gov.in/static/uploads/2024/02/Draft-National-Strategy-Robotics.pdf ; https://plidrone.ifciltd.com/docs/Guidelines%20Drones%20And%20Drone%20Components.pdf
- **Summary:** The NSR envisions India as a global robotics hub by 2030 via a National Robotics Mission (manufacturing, healthcare, agriculture, defense), emphasizing "Embodied Intelligence," cobots, and hardware/software sovereignty — explicitly drawing on IndiaAI (cognitive models) and ISM (sensors/actuators). The liberalized 2021 Drone Rules opened 90% of airspace; the Drone PLI allocates ₹120 cr over three years with an exceptionally high constant 20% incentive on value addition (40% threshold) to build a domestic drone industry.
