# Kazakhstan — National Technology Strategies (8 Domains)

*Source: Gemini deep research, 2026. Entity 45 (Batch 9). Central Asia.*

Kazakhstan leverages a "multi-vector" foreign policy to position itself as a technology-supply-chain node balancing China, Russia, the EU, and the US — and is pivoting from raw extraction toward deep processing and a high-value digital economy. It is setting Central Asian regulatory precedents with the 2026 **Digital Code** and a landmark **Law on Artificial Intelligence**. Maturity varies sharply: digital infrastructure, critical minerals, and nuclear energy are mature; quantum, semiconductors, and autonomous systems remain nascent (academic/JV-driven). Defining assets: world #1 uranium producer (Kazatomprom, ~43%), a fresh 2024 referendum approving its first NPP, and ~130 rare-earth deposits courted by both East and West.

## 1. Nuclear Energy
- **Plan:** 2024 NPP Referendum → Rosatom-led consortium (first NPP) + CNNC (second/third NPPs) + Nuclear Industry Development Strategy through 2050
- **Body:** President of Kazakhstan (Tokayev) / Atomic Energy Agency (Chairman Almasadam Satkaliyev); Kazatomprom (fuel); Rosatom & CNNC (vendors)
- **Year:** 2024 (referendum) – 2026 (contract & 2050 strategy)
- **URL:** https://www.akorda.kz / https://www.gov.kz / https://adilet.zan.kz
- **Summary:** As the world's largest uranium producer (~43% of global output via Kazatomprom), Kazakhstan is moving up the nuclear value chain after an October 6, 2024 referendum (71.12% yes) approved its first NPP. In **June 2025** the Atomic Energy Agency selected **Rosatom** to lead the international consortium for the first plant at **Ülken** (Lake Balkhash); preliminary site surveys began August 2025. On **28 May 2026**, during Putin's state visit to Astana, an intergovernmental construction agreement was signed (Satkaliyev / Rosatom CEO Likhachev) for **two Generation III+ VVER-1200 reactors (2.4 GW)**, a ~$16.4B project ~85% financed by a Russian state export loan, with construction from 2027 and commissioning targeted 2034–2036. Pursuing its multi-vector hedge, Kazakhstan in **July 2025** picked China's **CNNC** (HPR-1000/Hualong One) for the second and third NPPs, and Government Resolution No. 40 (**26 Jan 2026**) sited the second plant adjacent to Ülken. President Tokayev approved the **Nuclear Industry Development Strategy through 2050** on **17 April 2026**, formalizing a goal of at least three operating NPPs by 2050 (with SMRs under evaluation for the third site) to phase out coal.

## 2. Artificial Intelligence
- **Plan:** Concept for the Development of AI 2024–2029 (Decree No. 592) + Law on Artificial Intelligence (No. 230-VIII) + "Digital Qazaqstan" strategy / 2026 "Year of Digitalization and AI"
- **Body:** Ministry of Artificial Intelligence and Digital Development / Parliament
- **Year:** 2024 (Concept); 2025–2026 (Law)
- **URL:** https://adilet.zan.kz/rus/docs/P2400000592 / https://www.akorda.kz / https://alem.ai
- **Summary:** The Concept (July 2024) candidly notes Kazakhstan ranked 72nd on the Government AI Readiness Index (2023); targets include 5× more AI-integrated products by 2029, training up to 5 million citizens, and ~$20B added to the economy by 2030. Governance was elevated by creating a dedicated **Ministry of Artificial Intelligence and Digital Development**, and President Tokayev declared **2026 the "Year of Digitalization and Artificial Intelligence"** (decree signed 6 Jan 2026) under a goal to become a "fully digital nation" within three years. The **Law on AI No. 230-VIII** — the first such act in the Central Asian/EAEU space — was **signed 17 November 2025 and entered into force 18 January 2026**: AI systems are treated as tools under human control, with a risk-based framework, prohibitions on manipulative/exploitative AI, mandatory deepfake/synthetic-content labeling, and administrative liability up to suspension of non-compliant systems. On infrastructure sovereignty, the national supercomputer **Alem.cloud** (built with UAE partner **Presight**, NITEC-operated) debuted at **#86 on the global TOP-500 (Nov 2025) at 20.48 PFlop/s**, housed alongside the **Alem.ai** international AI hub opened October 2025 at the Digital Bridge forum, complementing the sovereign Kazakh-language model **KazLLM**.

## 3. Quantum Information Science
- **Plan:** Kazakh-Singaporean Center for Quantum Technologies *(no standalone national quantum strategy)*
- **Body:** Ministry of Science and Higher Education / Al-Farabi Kazakh National University (KazNU)
- **Year:** 2026 (launch)
- **URL:** https://farabi.university
- **Summary:** Explicitly nascent — no overarching national quantum strategy; the domain is incubated in academia. The milestone is the February 2026 inauguration of the **Kazakh-Singaporean Center for Quantum Technologies** at KazNU (Almaty) — a trilateral collaboration of KazNU, Singapore's ASTRASEC, and domestic startup Qubitera. Priorities: quantum cybersecurity/cryptography (a first-phase lab with photonic systems for quantum-secured communications), a talent pipeline bridging academic physics and industry, and alignment with UN SDGs 4 and 17. The international tech-transfer approach signals intent to avoid falling behind in the post-silicon "quantum revolution."

## 4. Biomedical Sciences / Biotechnology
- **Plan:** Strategic programs of QazBioPharm & the National Center for Biotechnology (NCB)
- **Body:** Ministry of Healthcare / QazBioPharm JSC
- **Year:** NCB established 1993; continuous updates
- **URL:** http://www.biocenter.kz / https://qbp-holding.kz
- **Summary:** The NCB (1993, presidential decree) runs 22 specialized labs (biosafety, pharmacology, cell tech, applied genetics) as a subsidiary of state holding **QazBioPharm**, which coordinates biosecurity and domestic biomanufacturing. Kazakhstan developed its own COVID-19 vaccine (**QazVac**), proving end-to-end pharmaceutical capability. A recent leap: QazBioPharm partnered with China's **BGI Genomics** to launch the **Astana Genetic Center** — Central Asia's first such lab — ending the need to export biomaterials for sequencing, spanning precision-medicine biomarkers and agricultural ecology (anti-desertification genomics near the Aral Sea). On agri-biotech, policy is strictly conservative: GE-crop commercialization/cultivation is banned (limited to labs), and Kazakhstan defers to the EAEU (effectively Russian) framework for GE imports — blocking commercialization of domestically engineered varieties.

## 5. Materials Science / Critical Raw Materials
- **Plan:** Comprehensive Plan for the Development of the Rare and Rare-Earth Metals Industry 2024–2028 (+ US & EU critical-minerals deals; DBK $1B financing programme)
- **Body:** Ministry of Industry and Construction / Development Bank of Kazakhstan (DBK) / National Geological Service
- **Year:** Plan 2024–2028; deals 2025–2026
- **URL:** https://www.gov.kz / https://www.kdb.kz
- **Summary:** Kazakhstan produces 19+ of the 34 materials the EU deems critical and holds ~130 rare-earth/associated-mineral deposits; the overarching strategy is now the named **"Comprehensive Plan for the Development of the Rare and Rare-Earth Metals Industry 2024–2028"** (Ministry of Industry and Construction), targeting a ~40% rise in investment/production, lifted secrecy regimes, and more early-stage exploration — financed by a **DBK $1 billion (2025–2030) programme** launched October 2025. The **April 2025 "Zhana Kazakhstan"** discovery (South Irgiz cluster, Karagandy; ~20M tonnes of REE-bearing ore rich in heavy REEs) could make the country #3 globally in reserves. Pivoting from near-total dependence on China, Astana signed a **US–Kazakhstan critical-minerals MOU on 6 November 2025** (Minister Nagaspayev / US Commerce Secretary Lutnick), followed by a **C5+1 Critical Minerals Dialogue in Astana (June 2026)** discussing ~$700M for tungsten, and a US JV (Kaz Resources Inc.) for defense-base supply. With the EU, Tokayev's **23–24 June 2026** Brussels visit yielded over **$12 billion** in contracts plus a planned **Regional Research Center for Rare Earth Metals**, built on offtake agreements and Kazakhstan's offer of 21 of the EU's 34 critical raw materials. Headwinds: capital intensity (10-yr lead times) and fears of Russian/Chinese monopolization.

## 6. Semiconductors / Microelectronics
- **Plan:** Emerging via academia/JV — "One Student, One Chip" + Suto Kazakhstan plant *(no national Chip Act)*
- **Body:** Ministry of Science and Higher Education / Nazarbayev University / Suto Kazakhstan (JV)
- **Year:** 2024–2025
- **URL:** https://nu.edu.kz
- **Summary:** Explicitly nascent — no multi-billion-dollar Chip Act. The strategy avoids the "fab-first trap," focusing on fabless design and verification (Nazarbayev University). In 2024 NU adopted the Chinese Academy of Sciences' **"One Student, One Chip"** program (students design open-source RISC-V processors), yielding Kazakhstan's first locally designed microchip in 2025 — IP domestic, fabrication outsourced to a Chinese partner via Multi-Project Wafer. This spawned startups like TEXER.AI (EDA/verification, backed by QazInnovation grants), aiming to provide IP/verification services globally without a domestic cleanroom. On physical manufacturing, a $50M chip/electronics plant began construction in 2025 in the Abai region (Ondiris zone) via **Suto Kazakhstan** (Kazakh-Chinese JV, part of a $1.5B bilateral drive) — phased through 2028, producing lower-to-mid-tier microchips, optoelectronic modules, controllers, and displays.

## 7. Digital Technology / Digital Infrastructure
- **Plan:** "Digital Kazakhstan" → "Digital Qazaqstan" nationwide strategy (to 2029) + Digital Code of Kazakhstan (No. 255-VIII)
- **Body:** Ministry of Artificial Intelligence and Digital Development / President / Parliament
- **Year:** 2017 (Digital Kazakhstan); strategy approved Nov 2025; Digital Code 2026 (in force July 2026)
- **URL:** https://www.gov.kz / https://primeminister.kz / https://adilet.zan.kz
- **Summary:** The most mature, aggressively-funded domain. **Digital Kazakhstan** (2017) built five vectors (industry digitization, e-government, "Digital Silk Road" data infrastructure, human capital, innovation), establishing **Astana Hub** and the **eGov** portal. It has now been superseded by the unified **"Digital Qazaqstan"** strategy (model approved by the Digital Headquarters on 24 November 2025), which consolidates all programmes (including World Bank GovTech work) toward a single **QazTech** platform model, more data centers, and a "fully digital nation within three years" — reinforced by the **2026 "Year of Digitalization and AI"** decree. The **Digital Code No. 255-VIII** (signed **9 January 2026**, entering into force **11 July 2026**) is among the world's first — a unified framework consolidating scattered tech law and establishing digital human rights (protection from arbitrary algorithmic restrictions, a right to "digital identity"), data-control rights via a personal "digital wallet," normalized everyday "digital confirmations" (SMS/biometrics/OTP), platform-economy and smart-contract rules, and advanced cybersecurity — operating in tandem with the AI Law.

## 8. Autonomous Systems
- **Plan:** Rules for the Operation of Unmanned Aerial Vehicles *(no national drone-manufacturing strategy)*
- **Body:** Civil Aviation Committee (CAC), Ministry of Industry and Infrastructure Development; Aviation Administration of Kazakhstan (AAK)
- **Year:** Continuous updates
- **URL:** https://www.gov.kz
- **Summary:** Drones are tightly regulated for airspace safety (no visionary national drone strategy). The CAC (with the AAK, and collaborating with JARUS) requires registration of drones above weight thresholds (historically >0.25 kg high-risk, >1.5 kg generally) via the e-government portal (up to 10 working days). Flight rules: strictly VLOS, ≤120 m altitude, no night flights, ≥150 m from crowds and ≥50 m from vehicles/buildings on takeoff, and bans within 10 km of airports or over military/government infrastructure without multi-agency clearance. Commercial operators need a Kazakhstan drone-pilot license and liability insurance. Domestic production is nascent — educational robotics in schools and regional drone-operator training (e.g., Turkistan), but no prominent industrial/military drone manufacturing; policy favors safe consumer integration over export ambitions.
